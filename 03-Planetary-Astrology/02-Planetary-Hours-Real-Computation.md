# Planetary Hours — The Real Computation

## The Definition

A **planetary hour** (Greek *hora planētikē*; Arabic *al-sa'a al-kawkabiyya*; Latin *hora planetaria*) is one of the **24 unequal hours** of the day and night, each ruled by one of the **seven classical planets** (Sun, Moon, Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Saturn — in the **Chaldean order** from slowest to fastest: Saturn, Jupiter, Mars, Sun, Venus, Mercury, Moon).

The planetary hours are **not** the **modern** **equal** hours (60 minutes each). The planetary hours are the **unequal** **temporal** hours of the **ancient** day, where the **day** (sunrise to sunset) and the **night** (sunset to sunrise) are **each** divided into **12** hours of **equal** **length** within themselves, but the **day** hours and the **night** hours are **unequal** to each other (the **day** hours are **longer** in **summer**, **shorter** in **winter**; the **night** hours are **shorter** in **summer**, **longer** in **winter**).

The planetary hours are **the** **operating** **system** **of** the **ancient** and **medieval** **astrological** **magical** **tradition**. The planetary hours **determine** the **planetary** **influence** **of** the **time** of the **action**. The **action** **taken** in the **planetary** **hour** of the **planet** **is** **empowered** **by** the **planet**; the **action** **taken** **against** the **planetary** **hour** **is** **weakened** **by** the **planet**.

## The Chaldean Order

The **Chaldean order** is the **sequence** of the **planets** from the **slowest** to the **fastest** in the **ancient** **astrological** **tradition** (the **order** **is** **based** on the **ancient** **astronomical** **observation** of the **planets** **in** the **heavens**):

1. **Saturn** — the **slowest** (29.5-year orbit)
2. **Jupiter** — 12-year orbit
3. **Mars** — 1.88-year orbit
4. **Sun** — 1-year orbit
5. **Venus** — 225-day orbit
6. **Mercury** — 88-day orbit
7. **Moon** — the **fastest** (27.3-day orbit)

The **order** is **the** **basis** **of** the **sequence** **of** the **planetary** **hours** **of** the **day**. The **order** **is** **not** **the** **order** **of** the **planets** **in** **the** **modern** **solar** **system** (the **modern** **order** **from** the **sun** **is** **Mercury**, **Venus**, **Earth**, **Mars**, **Jupiter**, **Saturn**, **Uranus**, **Neptune** — the **modern** **order** **is** **based** **on** the **orbital** **radius**, **not** **the** **orbital** **period**).

The **Chaldean** **order** **is** **not** **arbitrary**. The **Chaldean** **order** **is** **based** **on** **the** **Babilonian** **astronomical** **tradition** (the **Chaldeans** **were** the **Babylonian** **astronomers** of the **5th**-**3rd** **c.** **BCE**, **known** **to** the **Greeks** **as** **the** **masters** **of** **astrology**). The **Chaldean** **order** **is** **preserved** **in** **all** the **major** **astrological** **traditions** (the **Hellenistic**, the **Arabic**, the **Western**, the **Indian** — the **Indian** **order** **is** **the** **same** as the **Chaldean** **order** **from** **Saturn** **to** **the** **Moon**, but the **Indian** **tradition** **includes** **the** **outer** **planets** **Rahu** and **Ketu**, the **lunar** **nodes**).

## The Day and the Night

The **day** (in **the** **planetary** **hour** **system**) is the **period** **from** **sunrise** **to** **sunset**. The **day** is **divided** **into** **12** **planetary** **hours**. The **day** **hours** **are** **equal** **to** each **other** **in** **length**, **but** **unequal** **to** the **night** **hours** (the **day** **hours** **are** **longer** **in** **summer**, **shorter** **in** **winter**).

The **night** (in **the** **planetary** **hour** **system**) is the **period** **from** **sunset** **to** **sunrise**. The **night** is **divided** **into** **12** **planetary** **hours**. The **night** **hours** **are** **equal** **to** each **other** **in** **length**, **but** **unequal** **to** the **day** **hours** (the **night** **hours** **are** **shorter** **in** **summer**, **longer** **in** **winter**).

The **12** **day** **hours** + the **12** **night** **hours** = the **24** **planetary** **hours** of **the** **full** **day** (the **24**-hour **day**, but **with** **unequal** **hours**).

## The Algorithm

The **planetary** **hours** **are** **computed** **from** the **local** **sunrise** and **sunset** **times**. The **algorithm** **is** **simple** (the **algorithm** **is** **preserved** **in** **al-Buni** and **in** **Agrippa**):

### Step 1: Find the Local Sunrise and Sunset

The **local** **sunrise** and **sunset** **times** **are** **found** **from** an **astronomical** **almanac** or **from** a **software** **calculator** (the **Swiss** **Ephemeris** (Astrodienst) is the **standard** **modern** **source**; the **NOAA** **Solar** **Calculator** is the **standard** **free** **source**; the **USNO** **Astronomical** **Applications** is the **standard** **US** **government** **source**).

### Step 2: Compute the Day Length

The **day** **length** = sunset - sunrise (in **minutes** or **in** **seconds**, as **needed**).

### Step 3: Compute the Day Hour Length

The **day** **hour** **length** = day_length / 12 (the **day** is **divided** **into** **12** **equal** **hours**).

### Step 4: Compute the Night Length and the Night Hour Length

The **night** **length** = 24:00:00 - day_length (in **minutes** or **in** **seconds**). The **night** **hour** **length** = night_length / 12 (the **night** is **divided** **into** **12** **equal** **hours**).

### Step 5: Determine the Day-Ruler

The **day-ruler** is the **planet** **of** **the** **day** **of** **the** **week** (the **day** **ruler** is the **planet** **of** **the** **day** **on** the **Chaldean** **order**):

| Day of week | Day-ruler (planet) |
|---|---|
| **Sunday** | Sun |
| **Monday** | Moon |
| **Tuesday** | Mars |
| **Wednesday** | Mercury |
| **Thursday** | Jupiter |
| **Friday** | Venus |
| **Saturday** | Saturn |

The **association** **of** the **days** **of** the **week** **with** the **planets** **is** **the** **standard** **astrological** **association** (the **association** **is** **based** on the **Hellenistic** **tradition**; the **association** **is** **preserved** **in** **all** the **major** **astrological** **traditions**).

### Step 6: Assign the Planets to the Hours

The **first** **day** **hour** (the **hour** **starting** **at** **sunrise**) **is** **ruled** **by** the **day-ruler**. The **second** **day** **hour** **is** **ruled** **by** the **next** **planet** **in** the **Chaldean** **order** (**the** **planet** **that** **follows** **the** **day-ruler** **in** the **Chaldean** **order**). The **third** **day** **hour** **is** **ruled** **by** the **next** **planet** **after** **the** **second**, **and** **so** **on**.

The **example** for **Sunday** (the **Sun** **is** the **day-ruler**):

| Hour # | Start (Sunday) | Planet |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | sunrise | **Sun** |
| 2 | 1 day-hour after sunrise | **Venus** (next in Chaldean order) |
| 3 | 2 day-hours after | **Mercury** |
| 4 | 3 day-hours after | **Moon** |
| 5 | 4 day-hours after | **Saturn** |
| 6 | 5 day-hours after | **Jupiter** |
| 7 | 6 day-hours after | **Mars** |
| 8 | 7 day-hours after | **Sun** (the 8th hour returns to the Sun) |
| 9 | 8 day-hours after | Venus |
| 10 | 9 day-hours after | Mercury |
| 11 | 10 day-hours after | Moon |
| 12 | 11 day-hours after | Saturn |

The **12** **night** **hours** **continue** **the** **sequence** **from** **the** **12th** **day** **hour**. The **first** **night** **hour** (the **hour** **starting** **at** **sunset**) **is** **ruled** **by** the **next** **planet** **in** **the** **Chaldean** **order** **from** **the** **12th** **day** **hour** (which **would** **be** **Jupiter** **for** **Sunday**).

The **sequence** **is** **continuous** **through** **the** **day** **and** **the** **night**. The **24** **planetary** **hours** **of** **the** **full** **day** **are** **assigned** **planets** **in** **the** **Chaldean** **order**, **starting** **from** **the** **day-ruler** **at** **sunrise**.

### Step 7: The Day-Ruler of the Next Day

The **25th** **hour** (**sunrise** of **the** **next** **day**) **is** **ruled** **by** **the** **next** **planet** **in** the **Chaldean** **order** **from** **the** **24th** **hour** of **the** **previous** **day**. The **24th** **hour** **of** **Sunday** **night** **is** **ruled** **by** **the** **Moon** (for **Sunday**, **the** **24th** **hour** **is** **the** **Moon**). The **25th** **hour** (**sunrise** of **Monday**) **is** **ruled** **by** **the** **Moon** (the **day-ruler** of **Monday** is **the** **Moon**, **and** **the** **25th** **hour** **is** **the** **first** **hour** of **Monday**).

The **continuity** **of** the **sequence** **is** **preserved** **through** **the** **night**. The **24** **planetary** **hours** **of** **the** **day** **are** **a** **single** **sequence** **starting** **at** **sunrise** **of** **day** **1** **and** **ending** **at** **sunrise** of **day** **2**.

## The Day and the Night

The **planetary** **hours** **are** **the** **unequal** **temporal** **hours**. The **day** **hours** **are** **the** **hours** **from** **sunrise** **to** **sunset**. The **night** **hours** **are** **the** **hours** **from** **sunset** **to** **sunrise**.

The **unequal** **temporal** **hours** **were** **the** **only** **hours** **used** **in** **the** **Western** **tradition** **from** **antiquity** **until** **the** **14th** **c.** **CE**. The **mechanical** **clock** **was** **introduced** **in** **the** **13th**-**14th** **c.** **CE** (the **first** **mechanical** **clocks** **were** **in** **Italy** and **England**, **c.** **1270**-**1330** **CE**), but the **mechanical** **clock** **did** **not** **replace** **the** **unequal** **hours** **in** **the** **general** **use** **until** **the** **17th** **c.** **CE**. The **unequal** **hours** **were** **preserved** **in** **the** **astrological** **tradition** **because** **the** **astrological** **tradition** **required** **the** **unequal** **hours** for the **planetary** **hour** **calculation**.

The **unequal** **hours** **are** **preserved** **today** **in** **the** **traditional** **astrological** **tradition** (the **astrological** **tradition** **of** **the** **17th** **c.** **CE**, the **astrological** **tradition** **of** **the** **modern** **revival** of the **1990s**-**present**). The **unequal** **hours** **are** **used** **in** **the** **electional** **astrology** (the **astrology** of **choosing** **the** **right** **time** for the **action**), in the **horary** **astrology** (the **astrology** of **the** **question**), and in the **magical** **operations** (the **planetary** **talismans**).

## The Modern Calculation

The **modern** **calculation** **of** **the** **planetary** **hours** **is** **performed** **by** **software**. The **standard** **modern** **software** **for** **the** **planetary** **hour** **calculation** **is**:

1. **Astrodienst Planetary Hours** — the **free** **web** **service** of the **Swiss** **Ephemeris** (the **standard** **modern** **astrological** **calculation** **software**). The **service** **calculates** **the** **planetary** **hours** **for** **any** **location** **and** **any** **date**.
2. **Time Nomad** — the **mobile** **app** (**iOS** and **Android**) for the **planetary** **hour** **calculation**. The **app** **calculates** **the** **planetary** **hours** **for** **the** **current** **location** and **the** **current** **time**. The **app** **also** **calculates** **the** **day** **and** **night** **hours** for the **date**.
3. **iPhemeris** — the **mobile** **app** (**iOS** and **Android**) for the **planetary** **hour** **calculation**. The **app** **calculates** **the** **planetary** **hours** for **any** **location** and **any** **date**. The **app** **also** **calculates** **the** **planetary** **day** **and** **night** **hours** for the **date**.
4. **Astro.com** — the **free** **web** **service** of **Astrodienst** (the **standard** **modern** **astrological** **service**). The **service** **calculates** **the** **planetary** **hours** for **any** **location** and **any** **date**. The **service** **also** **calculates** **the** **day** and **night** **hours** for the **date**.

The **modern** **calculation** **is** **based** on the **same** **algorithm** **as** the **ancient** **calculation** (the **Swiss** **Ephemeris** **implements** the **same** **algorithm** **as** **al-Buni** and **Agrippa**). The **modern** **calculation** **is** **more** **accurate** **than** the **ancient** **calculation** (the **modern** **calculation** **uses** the **modern** **astronomical** **constants**, **the** **modern** **time** **zones**, the **modern** **geographic** **coordinates**).

## The Electional Use

The **planetary** **hours** **are** **used** in the **electional** **astrology** (the **astrology** of **choosing** **the** **right** **time** for the **action**). The **principle** **is** **simple**: the **action** **is** **empowered** **by** **the** **planetary** **hour** **of** **the** **planet** of the **action**; the **action** **is** **weakened** **by** **the** **planetary** **hour** of **the** **planet** **opposite** **to** the **planet** of the **action**.

The **mapping** of the **planets** **to** the **actions** **is** the **standard** **planetary** **correspondence**:

- **Sun** — **authority**, **leadership**, **public** **speaking**, **promotion**, **dignity**, **king**, **father**, **husband**
- **Moon** — **public**, **emotion**, **mother**, **wife**, **travel**, **navigation**, **water**
- **Mars** — **action**, **war**, **competition**, **sport**, **surgery**, **conflict**, **anger**
- **Mercury** — **commerce**, **communication**, **writing**, **learning**, **contracts**, **travel**, **short** **journeys**
- **Jupiter** — **expansion**, **growth**, **investment**, **education**, **legal**, **optimism**
- **Venus** — **love**, **partnership**, **art**, **beauty**, **pleasure**, **harmony**
- **Saturn** — **structure**, **endurance**, **real** **estate**, **long-term** **contracts**, **banishment**, **mourning**

The **mapping** **is** **not** **arbitrary**. The **mapping** **is** **based** on the **planetary** **correspondence** **in** the **planetary** **talismanic** **tradition** (see [[05-Talismanic-Magick/02-Planetary-Talismans]]), the **planetary** **correspondence** in the **horary** **astrology** (the **astrology** **of** **the** **question**), the **planetary** **correspondence** in the **Picatrix** and **the** **Agrippa**.

## The Example

A **concrete** **example**: a **wedding** **to** **be** **planned** for the **month** **of** **June** in **Bandung, Indonesia** (latitude -6.917°, longitude 107.619°).

The **wedding** **is** **a** **Venus** **action** (the **wedding** **is** a **partnership**). The **wedding** **should** **be** **planned** for the **Friday** (the **Venus** **day**) and **for** **the** **planetary** **hour** **of** **the** **Venus** **on** **Friday**.

The **planetary** **hours** **on** **Friday** in **Bandung** **on** **a** **specific** **date** **are** **calculated** by **the** **algorithm** **above**. The **first** **Friday** **hour** (the **hour** **starting** **at** **sunrise** of **Friday**) **is** **ruled** **by** **the** **Venus** (the **Friday** **day-ruler**). The **second** **Friday** **hour** **is** **ruled** **by** **Saturn** (the **next** **planet** **in** **the** **Chaldean** **order** **after** **the** **Venus**). The **third** **Friday** **hour** **is** **ruled** **by** **Jupiter**. The **fourth** **Friday** **hour** **is** **ruled** **by** **Mars**. The **fifth** **Friday** **hour** **is** **ruled** **by** **the** **Sun**. The **sixth** **Friday** **hour** **is** **ruled** **by** **the** **Venus** (the **Venus** **returns** **at** **the** **sixth** **hour**). The **seventh** **Friday** **hour** **is** **ruled** **by** **Mercury**. The **eighth** **Friday** **hour** **is** **ruled** **by** **the** **Moon**. The **ninth** **Friday** **hour** **is** **ruled** **by** **Saturn**. The **tenth** **Friday** **hour** **is** **ruled** **by** **Jupiter**. The **eleventh** **Friday** **hour** **is** **ruled** **by** **Mars**. The **twelfth** **Friday** **hour** **is** **ruled** **by** **the** **Sun**.

The **first** **Friday** **hour** and the **sixth** **Friday** **hour** **are** the **Venus** **hours** of **Friday**. The **wedding** **is** **planned** for the **first** **Friday** **hour** (the **most** **powerful** **Venus** **hour** of **Friday**) or for the **sixth** **Friday** **hour** (the **second** **most** **powerful** **Venus** **hour** of **Friday**).

The **astrological** **practitioner** **also** **checks** the **Moon** **sign** (the **Moon** **should** **be** in a **favorable** **sign** for the **wedding** — the **sign** of **Taurus** or **Libra**, the **Venus** **signs**, **are** **the** **best** for the **wedding**), the **malefic** **planets** (the **Mars** and **Saturn** **should** **not** **be** in the **Ascendant** of the **wedding** **chart**), the **benefic** **planets** (the **Jupiter** and **Venus** **should** **be** in the **Ascendant** of the **wedding** **chart**), the **lunar** **phase** (the **Full** **Moon** or the **Waxing** **Moon** **are** the **best** for the **wedding**).

The **calculation** **is** **complex** **but** **the** **principle** **is** **simple**: the **planetary** **hour** **is** **the** **first** **step** of the **electional** **astrology**; the **planetary** **hour** **is** **the** **foundation** of the **electional** **practice**.

## The Practice

A **modern** **practitioner** **of** the **planetary** **hours** **should**:

1. **Use** the **Swiss** **Ephemeris** or **the** **equivalent** **software** to **calculate** the **planetary** **hours** for the **location** and the **date**
2. **Match** the **planetary** **hour** to the **action** (the **Venus** **hour** for the **love** action, the **Mars** **hour** for the **competition** action, the **Mercury** **hour** for the **commerce** action, etc.)
3. **Use** the **planetary** **hour** as the **first** **step** of the **electional** **astrology** (the **planetary** **hour** is **necessary** but **not** **sufficient** for the **electional** **astrology**)
4. **Track** the **outcomes** of the **planetary** **hour** in the **personal** **log** (the **log** is the **empirical** **evidence** for the **planetary** **hour** **practice**)
5. **Adjust** the **practice** based on the **log** (the **log** is the **feedback** for the **planetary** **hour** **practice**)

The **planetary** **hour** **is** **not** **a** **self-help** **technique**. The **planetary** **hour** **is** the **astrological** **practice** of the **planetary** **talismanic** **tradition**. The **planetary** **hour** **is** **the** **first** **step** of the **astrological** **electional** **practice**. The **practice** **requires** the **software**, the **log**, the **chain** (the **astrological** **tradition** of the **practitioner**'s **tradition**).
