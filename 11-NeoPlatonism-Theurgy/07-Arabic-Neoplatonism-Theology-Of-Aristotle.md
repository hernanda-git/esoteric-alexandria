# The Arabic Reception of Neoplatonism — Theology of Aristotle & Liber de Causis

## The Pseudo-Aristotelian Texts

The **most influential** Neoplatonic texts in the Islamic world were **not** labeled as Neoplatonic. Two **pseudo-Aristotelian** works carried the **Plotinian** and **Proclean** doctrines into Arabic:

1. **The Theology of Aristotle** (*Uthulujiyya Aristatalis*) — an **Arabic paraphrase** of **Plotinus'** *Enneads* **IV-VI**, produced in the **9th-c.** **Kindi** circle. The text is **not** by Aristotle; it is **Plotinus** in **Arabic dress**, with the **emanation** of **Nous** from the **One**, the **descent** of **Soul**, and the **return** of the **intellect** to its **source**.
2. **The Liber de Causis** (*Kitab al-Idah fi al-Khayr al-Mahd*, "Book of the Pure Good") — a **compilation** drawn from **Proclus'** *Elements of Theology* (notably propositions 1-3, 8, 11-13, 109, and the **causal** chain). The work was **ascribed** to Aristotle in the **Latin** West until **Thomas Aquinas** proved (in the *Summa Theologiae*, c. 1268-1271) that it was **post-Aristotelian** — in fact **Proclean**.

The **misattribution** is the **key** to their influence: because they were **thought** to be **Aristotle**, the **Islamic** and **Latin** philosophers read **Neoplatonism** as the **esoteric** teaching of the **Master** himself. The **One** entered the **commentarial** tradition **disguised** as the **First Cause**.

## Al-Farabi — The Second Teacher

**Al-Farabi** (c. 872-950), "the Second Teacher" (after Aristotle), built the first **systematic** Islamic **emanationism** in **al-Madina al-Fadila** ("The Virtuous City"). His **cosmology** is a **chain**: the **One** emanates the **First Intellect**; each **intellect** emanates the **next** and a **celestial sphere**; the chain **descends** to the **Active Intellect** (*al-'aql al-fa''al*) and the **sublunar** world. The **structure** is **Plotinian**; the **vocabulary** is **Aristotelian**. Al-Farabi is the **foundation** of the **Arabic** Neoplatonic **current**.

## Avicenna — The Oriental Philosophy

**Avicenna** (Ibn Sina, 980-1037) **completed** the synthesis. His **Metaphysics** of the **Shifa'** ("The Healing") and his **al-Hikma al-Mashriqiyya** ("Oriental Philosophy") present the **emanation** of the **intelligences** and the **proof** of God as the **Necessary Existent** (*wajib al-wujud*). His **"floating man"** thought-experiment (a man **created** in **mid-air**, with no **sensation**, who nonetheless **knows** his own **existence**) is a **Neoplatonic** argument for the **separability** of the **soul**. His **narrative** works — **Hayy ibn Yaqzan** and **The Recital** — frame the **ascent** of the **soul** in **mythic** Neoplatonic form.

## The Bridge to Sufism

The **Arabic** Neoplatonism **fed** **Sufism**. The **emanationist** vocabulary of the **theology** schools — the **unity** of the **source**, the **descent** of **being**, the **return** of the **soul** — supplied the **conceptual** frame for **Ibn Arabi's** **wahdat al-wujud** ("unity of being"). The **pseudo-Theology of Aristotle** was read by **Suhrawardi** (the **Ishraqi**, "Illuminationist") and by the **Sufi** metaphysicians. The **Neoplatonic** **chain of being** is the **hidden** link between **falsafa** (philosophy) and **tasawwuf** (Sufism).

## The Latin Afterlife

Through the **12th-c.** translators (**Gerard of Cremona**, **Michael Scot**), the **Liber de Causis** and the **pseudo-Theology** reached the **Latin West**. The **Liber de Causis** was a **set text** at **Paris** until **Aquinas** **unmasked** it. The **Arabic** Plotinus shaped **Albertus Magnus**, **Bonaventure**, and the **Renaissance** **Platonic** revival (Ficino). The **emanationist** **current** that **Proclus** and **Plotinus** founded reached **Europe** **via** **Baghdad** and **Toledo**.

## The Modern Mapping

The Arabic Neoplatonism **maps** onto modern categories:

1. **The Great Chain of Being** — Arthur Lovejoy identified the **emanationist** hierarchy (One → Intellect → Soul → Nature) as the **root** metaphor of **Western** **ontology** from the **Middle Ages** to the **Enlightenment**.
2. **Top-down causality** — the **Neoplatonic** model, where **higher** levels **contain** and **produce** the **lower** without **diminution**, is a **precursor** to **holistic** and **information-theoretic** accounts of **causation** (the **whole** **determines** the **part**).
3. **The disguised source** — the **pseudo-Aristotelian** **misattribution** is a **case study** in how **traditions** **transmit** **ideas** through **authoritative** **veils**, a **pattern** visible also in the **Hermetica** (see [[10-Hermeticism-Gnosticism/01-Corpus-Hermeticum]]).

The Arabic reception is the **reason** Neoplatonism **survived** the **closure** of the **Academy** (529 CE). The reception is the **bridge** from **Late Antiquity** to the **Renaissance**.
