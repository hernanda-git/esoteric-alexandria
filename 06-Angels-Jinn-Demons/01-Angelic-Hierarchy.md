# The Angelic Hierarchy — Nine Choirs, Three Triads

## The Source

**Pseudo-Dionysius the Areopagite** (5th-6th c. CE, real name unknown) wrote **De Coelesti Hierarchia** ("On the Celestial Hierarchy") as part of a corpus that was, for 1000 years, attributed to Dionysius the Areopagite, a convert of St. Paul mentioned in Acts 17:34. The attribution was not challenged until the 19th century (by Lorenzo Valla and Erasmus).

The text systematized the **9 choirs of angels** into **3 triads** based on their proximity to the divine source. The system was adopted by **Thomas Aquinas** in the *Summa Theologica*, and became the **canonical Western angelic hierarchy**.

## The 9 Choirs in 3 Triads

### First Triad — The Divine Council (closest to God)

| # | Choir | Greek | Role | Domain |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Seraphim** | Σεραφίμ ("burning ones") | The closest to God, perpetually singing "Holy, Holy, Holy" | Divine love, purification by fire |
| 2 | **Cherubim** | Χερουβίμ ("the powerful") | The guardians of Eden, the throne of God | Divine wisdom, knowledge, the mysteries of God |
| 3 | **Thrones** (Ophanim in Hebrew) | Θρόνοι ("thrones") | The wheels of Ezekiel's vision, the "many-eyed ones" | Divine justice, the order of creation, the cosmic law |

### Second Triad — The Governors

| # | Choir | Greek | Role | Domain |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 4 | **Dominions** (Dominations) | Κυριότητες ("lordships") | The regulators of angelic duties, the "collar-and-tie angels" | Divine order, the organization of creation |
| 5 | **Virtues** | Δυνάμεις ("powers") | The miracle-workers, the patrons of courage | Divine strength, miracles, perseverance, patron of soldiers |
| 6 | **Powers** (Potestates) | Ἐξουσίαι ("authorities") | The warriors against evil, the "border guards" | Divine authority, the prevention of demonic invasion |

### Third Triad — The Messengers and Workers (closest to humans)

| # | Choir | Greek | Role | Domain |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 7 | **Principalities** (Archai) | Ἀρχαί ("beginnings") | The guardians of nations and cities, the "ambassadors" | Divine providence over nations, governance |
| 8 | **Archangels** | Ἀρχάγγελοι ("chief messengers") | The chief messengers, the named few (Michael, Gabriel, Raphael, Uriel) | Divine revelation, the great announcements |
| 9 | **Angels** (the generic) | Ἄγγελοι ("messengers") | The most numerous, the closest to humans | Divine guardianship of individuals |

## The Correspondence to the Kabbalah

The 9 choirs map directly to the **Kabbalistic Tree of Life** (see [[08-Kabbalah-Numerology/01-Gematria-Mapping]]):

| Kabbalistic Sephirah | Meaning | Pseudo-Dionysian Choir |
|---|---|---|
| Kether | Crown | Seraphim |
| Chokhmah | Wisdom | Cherubim |
| Binah | Understanding | Thrones |
| Chesed | Mercy | Dominions |
| Geburah | Severity | Powers |
| Tiphareth | Beauty | Virtues |
| Netzach | Victory | Principalities |
| Hod | Splendor | Archangels |
| Malkuth | Kingdom | Angels |

The correspondence is **exact**. The Kabbalah is the older tradition (1st-6th c. CE for the *Sefer Yetzirah* and *Zohar* material); Pseudo-Dionysius drew on it or on the same Neoplatonic source. The Western Christian Kabbalah (Pico della Mirandola, Reuchlin, Agrippa) saw the correspondence as a confirmation of the **prisca theologia** — the unity of all traditions.

## The Correspondence to the Planets

The 9 choirs also map to the planetary spheres (in the Ptolemaic / al-Buni system):

| Sphere | Planet | Choir |
|---|---|---|
| 1 (highest) | Primum Mobile (the first mover) | Seraphim |
| 2 | Zodiak (the 12 signs) | Cherubim |
| 3 | Saturn | Thrones |
| 4 | Jupiter | Dominions |
| 5 | Mars | Virtues |
| 6 | Sun | Powers |

The angel of each sphere is its **intelligence** — the mind that moves the planet, the speaker of the planetary prayers, the ruler of the planetary hour. See [[12-Agrippa-Picatrix-Grimoires/01-Three-Books-Agrippa]] for the Agrippan synthesis.

## The Named Archangels

The **four (or seven) Archangels** are the named few. The tradition varies:

### Christian tradition (4 archangels)

- **Michael** (מִיכָאֵל, "Who is like God?") — the warrior, the protector, the leader of the heavenly host against Satan
- **Gabriel** (גַּבְרִיאֵל, "God is my strength") — the messenger, the announcer (to Mary, to Muhammad, to Zacharias)
- **Raphael** (רָפָאֵל, "God heals") — the healer, the guide of Tobias
- **Uriel** (אוּרִיאֵל, "God is my light") — the light-bearer, the wisdom of God

### Jewish / Christian tradition (7 archangels)

Add to the four:

- **Chamuel** (חַמּוּאֵל, "He who sees God") — the peacemaker
- **Jophiel** (יוֹפִיאֵל, "Beauty of God") — the artist, the wisdom
- **Zadkiel** (צַדְקִיאֵל, "Righteousness of God") — the mercy, the forgiveness

The **Book of Enoch** (1 Enoch, 3rd c. BCE) and **2 Enoch** (1st-2nd c. CE) give longer lists of archangels. The **Shem ha-Mephorash** (the 72-fold name) gives 72 angels, each presiding over a 5-degree arc of the zodiac.

## The Function of the Hierarchy

The hierarchy is not a static list. It is a **functional map** of how the divine operates in the world:

1. The **divine essence** is one and indivisible
2. It is **transmitted** to the highest seraphim by the most direct contact
3. The seraphim **transmit** to the cherubim
4. The cherubim to the thrones
5. And so on down to the lowest angels, who **transmit** to the human
6. The human receives the divine at the **angelic level** and can **transmit** back up by prayer

The hierarchy is the **operating system** of divine governance. Each choir has a **specific office** (purification, illumination, perfection — Pseudo-Dionysius's three functions) and a **specific mode of reception** (the higher choirs receive the divine more directly, the lower more mediated).

## The Modern Note

The angelic hierarchy is **alive** in three communities:

1. **The Christian liturgical tradition** — the Eastern Orthodox liturgy, the Catholic Mass, the Anglican evensong invoke the 9 choirs explicitly. The **Cherubic Hymn** of the Orthodox liturgy is a prayer to the cherubim. The **Sanctus** of the Mass is a quotation of Isaiah's vision of the seraphim.
2. **The Western esoteric tradition** — Agrippa, the Golden Dawn, the modern Thelemic and ceremonial orders use the hierarchy as the **operating structure** of the conjuration ritual. The magician invokes the choirs in order, from the highest to the lowest, to "borrow" their authority.
3. **The new-age and angelic communities** — the modern angel healing community (Doreen Virtue, Kyle Gray, Lorna Byrne) has produced a popular literature of "angel communication" that draws loosely on the traditional hierarchy.

The library's category 06 documents the **classical** hierarchy. The modern popular literature is recorded in [[15-Modern-Parallels/]] and related categories.

## The Practice

A modern Christian Kabbalistic practice of the hierarchy:

1. **Study the sources** — Pseudo-Dionysius, Aquinas, the *Zohar*, Agrippa Book III
2. **Learn the correspondences** — the 9 choirs, the 9 sephirot, the 9 planetary spheres
3. **Practice invocation** — invoke the angel of the day, the hour, the current need
4. **Use the hierarchy as a structure** — the practitioner can "rise" through the choirs in contemplation, from the lowest to the highest

The popular modern practice of "calling on your guardian angel" is a **simplified** form of this tradition. The classical form is more structured, more demanding, and more rewarding.
