# The 72 Goetic Spirits and the Shem ha-Mephorash

## The 72-Fold Name

The **Shem ha-Mephorash** (שֵׁם הַמְפֹרָשׁ, "the explicit name") is the 72-fold name of God in the Jewish tradition. The name is derived from **Exodus 14:19-21** and **Exodus 15:1-18** — three verses, each of 72 letters, giving 216 letters total. The 216 letters are then **split into triplets** to give 72 tri-letter names.

The 72 names are the **most powerful** divine names in the Kabbalistic tradition. Each name:

- Has a **3-letter form** (e.g., *Vehuiah*, *Yeliel*, *Sitael*, *Elemiah*)
- Has a **corresponding angel** who bears that name
- Has a **corresponding demon** in the Goetic tradition
- Rules a **5-degree arc of the zodiac** (360° / 72 = 5°)
- Has a **specific operation** and **specific powers**

The Shem ha-Mephorash is the **master key** of the Kabbalistic magical tradition. Knowing the 72 names is the prerequisite for serious operative Kabbalah.

The 72 spirits are the **demons** of the Western tradition. The names are the same as the **daemons** of the Greek tradition, the **jinn** of the Islamic tradition (with significant overlap), and the **shadows** of the Kabbalistic angels.

## The Theological Reading

The pairing of **72 angels** and **72 demons** is theologically loaded:

- The **angelic reading**: God has 72 names, each is a different mode of divine action. The 72 angels bear these names. The 72 demons are the **distortions** of the divine names, the **shadows** of the divine actions. To invoke the angel is to invoke the divine mode; to invoke the demon is to invoke the distortion.

- The **dualist reading**: The 72 angels and 72 demons are **two equal principles**, locked in cosmic battle. The Christian tradition rejects this (it is Manichean, not Christian), but it has been the **popular** reading for centuries.

- The **psychological reading**: The 72 angels are **higher potentials** of the human; the 72 demons are **lower potentials**. The work of the magician is to **integrate** both, neither fleeing into the angelic nor being possessed by the demonic. The magician's authority is the **ability to navigate both** the angelic and the demonic without being ruled by either.

The library takes the **psychological reading** as the most accessible, while preserving the **angelic reading** as the most theologically serious. The **dualist reading** is documented but not endorsed.

## The Jinn Parallel

In the Islamic tradition, the **jinn** are the **closest** to the Western demons (see [[03-Jinn-Islamic-Spirit-Lore]]). The 72 jinn correspond approximately to the 72 Goetic spirits. The 9 most powerful jinn (**Naddah**, **Su'ada'**, etc., though the lists vary) parallel the 9 choirs of the angelic hierarchy.

The convergence is **strong**:

- Western demon → Islamic jinn
- Western angel → Islamic angel (mala'ika)
- Goetic spirit → powerful jinn
- Shem ha-Mephorash angel → one of the **ala mala'ika** ("the elevated angels")

The traditions are **not identical**, but the structures are the same.

## The Modern Note

The 72-fold system is alive in several communities:

1. **The Jewish Kabbalistic tradition** — the 72 names are still studied, though the operative use is rare
2. **The Christian Kabbalistic tradition** (Pico, Reuchlin, Agrippa, the Golden Dawn) — the 72 names are the master key of the magician's art
3. **The chaos magic tradition** — the spirits are treated as **archetypes** of the unconscious, the seals as **meditation mandalas**
4. **The demonolatry / Luciferian tradition** — the Goetia is treated literally, the spirits are invoked as real entities

The library's category 06 treats the 72-fold system as the **master index** of the spirit-world, regardless of which tradition you work in.

## The Practice

A modern practice of the 72-fold system has 7 steps:

1. **Study the source** — read the *Sefer ha-Razim*, the *Lesser Key*, the 3 Enoch, the *Book of Enoch*
2. **Learn the correspondences** — memorize the 72 names (or a subset), their zodiacal arcs, their planetary rulers
3. **Learn the invocations** — each name has a specific invocation in the classical tradition
4. **Practice the purification** — the 72 names cannot be invoked by an unprepared practitioner
5. **Choose the time** — the right time, the right day, the right planetary hour
6. **Invoke with the right attitude** — invoke for **the right purpose**, not for curiosity
7. **Close the ritual** — every invocation must be **dismissed**, every door **closed**

The library documents the tradition. The practice requires a teacher, a tradition, a community. The reader is reminded: the 72 names are **the most powerful** in the Western tradition, and misuse has consequences in the tradition's own accounting.
