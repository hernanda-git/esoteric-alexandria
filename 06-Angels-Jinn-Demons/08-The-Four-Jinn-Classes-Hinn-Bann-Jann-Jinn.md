# The Four Jinn Classes — Al-Hinn, Al-Bann, Al-Jann, and Al-Jinn

## Overview

The Islamic esoteric tradition recognizes a **fourfold classification** of the non-human intelligent beings created from fire — the **jinn**. This classification is attested in classical Quranic exegesis (*tafsir*), the Hadith literature, and the elaborate hierarchies of the occult sciences, particularly the *'ilm al-huruf* (science of letters) tradition systematized by **Ahmad al-Buni** (d. 1225 CE) in the *Shams al-Ma'arif*.

The four classes are:

| Class | Arabic | Element | Quranic Reference | Nature |
|-------|--------|---------|-------------------|--------|
| **Al-Jinn** | الجنّ | Smokeless fire (mārij min nār) | Quran 55:15 | The general race |
| **Al-Jann** | الجانّ | Scorching fire (nār al-samūm) | Quran 15:27 | The primordial progenitor |
| **Al-Hinn** | الحينّ | Earth / transformation | Esoteric tradition | The mutable jinn |
| **Al-Bann** | البنّ | Earth / fragrance | Esoteric tradition | The hidden jinn |

This fourfold taxonomy is a complement to the better-known five-tribe system (Jinn, Jann, Shayatin, Ifrit, Marid). It operates at an **ontological level** — grouping jinn by creation substance and realm rather than by behavioral traits.

---

## 1. Al-Jinn (الجنّ) — The General Race

**Al-Jinn** is the generic term for the unseen intelligent beings. Quran 55:15: "And the jinn He created from a smokeless flame of fire." The root **J-N-N** means "to cover" or "to conceal" — the jinn are the hidden beings.

The jinn possess **free will** and **moral responsibility** (*taklīf*). They can be Muslim or non-Muslim (Quran 72:1-15), have their own prophets (Quran 46:29-32), and face Judgment (Quran 72:15). They marry, reproduce, and die.

In the *'ilm al-huruf* tradition, Al-Jinn corresponds to the **letter Jīm** (ج, abjad value 3) — the letter of manifestation and body. The jinn are the **active intermediaries** between the angelic realm of light and the human realm of clay.

---

## 2. Al-Jann (الجانّ) — The Primordial Progenitor

**Al-Jann** appears in Quran 15:27: "And the Jann, We created before from scorching fire" — distinguishing it from the generic *jinn*. The *samūm* fire is a penetrating, poisonous wind-fire, fiercer than ordinary flame.

In classical exegesis, Al-Jann is understood as:

- **The father of the jinn** (*abū al-jānn*) — the singular progenitor from whom all jinn descend
- **The pre-Adamic creation** — the Jann were the original inhabitants of the earth (Quran 2:30 references their corruption and replacement)
- **Iblis connection** — Quran 18:50 refers to Iblis as "being of the jinn." In esoteric commentary (Ibn al-Arabi), Iblis was from the **Jann** — a primordial fire-being who worshipped God for millennia before Adam

In the *'ilm al-huruf* tradition, Al-Jann corresponds to the **doubled Jīm** (gemination) — representing self-replication and the boundary between the angelic and jinnic realms. The Jann are the **hidden kings** who rule the elemental jinn tribes.

---

## 3. Al-Hinn (الحينّ) — The Mutable Jinn

**Al-Hinn** derives from the root **H-Y-N** (ح-ي-ن), meaning "time" (*hīn*). The root extends to destruction, change, and the appointed hour. The Hinn are the jinn of **time, transformation, and decay** — not evil in the moral sense but cosmological entropy.

The Hinn occupy the **third station** — the midpoint between primordial fire-beings and material beings:

- Created from the **interaction of fire and earth** — the "cooling" of the primordial flame
- The **bridge between invisibility and visibility** — they manifest more readily than higher jinn
- Associated with the **"lower breath"** — winds that carry decay and transformation

In folk taxonomy, the Hinn are responsible for:
- **Possession** (*mās*) — the most common form of jinn affliction
- **Disease and epidemics** — carried by winds (a pre-modern cosmological model of contagion)
- **Decomposition** — the transformation of organic matter after death

**Pre-Islamic origins**: The Jahiliyya Arabs recognized *hinn* as spirits of barren places — wadis, caves where bones accumulated. Amira El-Zein (Islam, Arabs, and the Spirit World, 2009) documents the Hinn as a distinct category, often described as the "weakest" of the jinn.

**Esoteric dimension**: Al-Hinn corresponds to the **letter Hā'** (ح, abjad value 8):
- Element: Water/Earth (transitional)
- Planet: **Moon** — the sphere of change and decay
- Realm: The **liminal** — doorways, riverbanks, cemeteries, crossroads

The Hinn are the **jinn of the threshold** — beings that inhabit the transition zones where time and matter are in flux. This is why folk tradition considers them the most commonly encountered jinn.

---

## 4. Al-Bann (البنّ) — The Hidden Jinn

**Al-Bann** derives from the root **B-N-N** (ب-ن-ن). Two meaning clusters:

- **Bān** (بان) — a fragrant shrub (*Moringa peregrina*), associated with perfume and oil
- **Bann** (بنّ) — the hidden, the buried, what lies beneath the surface (related to *bātin* = the inner)

Al-Bann are the **jinn of the hidden earth** — the deepest and most material class:

- **Guardians of fragrance and essence** — the keepers of scents locked within the earth
- **Guardians of treasure** — buried wealth, ancient knowledge, the secrets of the deep
- **The "servant" jinn** — the class most amenable to binding through the *'azima*

In the **'ilm al-huruf** tradition, Al-Bann corresponds to the **letter Bā'** (ب, abjad value 2):
- Element: **Earth** — the Bā' is the foundation, the "house" (*bayt*)
- Planet: **Mercury** — the messenger, the boundary between worlds
- Significance: The Bā' is the first letter of the Quran (*Bismillah*) and the first letter of creation in *'ilm al-huruf* cosmology

In folk tradition, the Bann are more often **guardians** than aggressors — protecting treasures, sacred springs, mountain roots, and the seeds of esoteric knowledge. Some Sufi traditions mention the Bann as the **"true silent worshippers"** — jinn who praise God in the silence of the earth.

---

## Classification Synthesis

The four-class system complements the five-tribe system:

| Class (ontological) | Five-Tribe Equivalent (behavioral) | Primary Source |
|---------------------|------------------------------------|----------------|
| Al-Jinn | Jinn (general) + Marid (the powerful) | Quran 72, 55:15 |
| Al-Jann | Jann (tribe) + Iblis connection | Quran 15:27 |
| Al-Hinn | Shayatin (the tempter class) | Esoteric / Folk |
| Al-Bann | Ifrit (the bound class) | Esoteric / 'Ilm al-Huruf |

The **practice hierarchy**: Al-Bann (easiest to contact, most material) → Al-Hinn (threshold contact) → Al-Jinn (broad category, varied response) → Al-Jann (most difficult, most primordial). Lower classes are invoked for **material** operations; higher classes for **spiritual** operations.
