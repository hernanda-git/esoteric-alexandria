# The Four Stages — Nigredo, Albedo, Citrinitas, Rubedo

## The Standard Sequence

The *Opus Magnum* (the Great Work) is divided into **four stages** in the standard Western tradition:

| Stage | Latin | Color | Operation | Psychological correlate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1. **Nigredo** | *blackening* | Black | *putrefactio* (putrefaction) | Depression, shadow, dissolution of identity |
| 2. **Albedo** | *whitening* | White | *purificatio* (purification) | Discernment, clarity, separation of true from false |
| 3. **Citrinitas** | *yellowing* | Yellow | *sublimatio* (sublimation) | Insight, illumination, the "dawn" before full sun |
| 4. **Rubedo** | *reddening* | Red | *coniunctio* (conjunction) | Integration, the coniunctio oppositorum, the philosopher's stone |

Some traditions add a **fifth stage**, *quintessentia* (the fifth essence) or *lapis philosophorum* (the philosopher's stone) as the result. The 4-stage and 5-stage schemes are both present in the *Rosarium*.

## Stage 1: Nigredo

**Symbolism**: The matter in the flask goes black. The "black sun" (*sol niger*) is the dense, undifferentiated state of the prima materia before the work begins. The black is the death that precedes life.

**Operation**: *putrefactio* — the matter is sealed in the flask (*athanor*, the alchemical furnace) and heated gently for weeks. The practitioner descends into the dark.

**Psychological**: The encounter with the shadow. The depression, the loss, the failure, the dark night of the soul. The alchemists were explicit that this stage is **unavoidable**. The practitioner cannot skip it. The attempt to skip the nigredo is called *fugere nigredinem* (fleeing the blackness) and is the most common failure of the work.

**Symptoms**: depression, anxiety, breakdown of old certainties, encounter with one's own mortality, a sense of being "in the dark". The alchemists described it as a descent into the underworld, a *katabasis*.

**Modern parallel**: The **drawdown** in trading ([[20-Trading-Risk-Archetypes/01-Risk-As-Initiation]]). The job loss. The divorce. The illness. The midlife crisis. The encounter with one's own death.

**Practice**: Do not flee. Sit in the darkness. Let the putrefaction do its work. The alchemists warned: the nigredo lasts as long as it lasts, and trying to shorten it produces **artificial gold** (the manic defense against the depression).

**Duration**: Variable. In the *Rosarium*, the nigredo is described as a few weeks to several months. In a life, it can be a year or more.

## Stage 2: Albedo

**Symbolism**: The matter in the flask goes from black to white. The "white queen" (*regina alba*) emerges. The peacock's tail (*cauda pavonis*) is the iridescent transition between black and white.

**Operation**: *purificatio* — the matter is washed, distilled, separated. The "true" is separated from the "false". The volatile is separated from the fixed.

**Psychological**: The period of **discernment**. After the descent, the practitioner can see clearly. They know what they value, what they have lost, what they are. The albedo is the period of **clarity**.

**Symptoms**: peace after the depression, a sense of having "come through", the ability to see one's life clearly for the first time, increased capacity for solitude, a new ethical clarity.

**Modern parallel**: The recovery after the drawdown. The period of steady work after the layoff. The clarity that comes after the breakdown.

**Practice**: Do not rush. The clarity is fragile. The alchemists warned: the practitioner at the albedo stage is tempted to **stop** — to settle for the white and call it the goal. But the white is not the gold. The albedo is **half-way**, not done.

**Duration**: Months to a year in a life, weeks in a flask.

## Stage 3: Citrinitas

**Symbolism**: The matter goes from white to yellow. The "dawn" or "rising sun" (*aurora*). Some traditions fold the citrinitas into the rubedo, but the *Aurora Consurgens* keeps it distinct.

**Operation**: *sublimatio* — the matter is raised from the lower to the higher. The "fixed" becomes "volatile" and returns to the "fixed". The alchemists called this the *coagulatio* and *solutio* alternating cycle.

**Psychological**: The period of **illumination**. The clarity becomes insight. The discernment becomes wisdom. The practitioner is no longer merely clear; they are **illuminated**.

**Symptoms**: insight, integration of opposites, a sense of "the dawn has come", increased capacity to see the whole picture, the beginning of the synthesis.

**Modern parallel**: The first sustained period of **mastery** after the ordeal. The trader's first year of consistent profitability. The programmer's first year of independent design.

**Practice**: Do not mistake the citrinitas for the rubedo. The citrinitas is the **insight into** the goal; the rubedo is the **arrival at** the goal. The difference is in the embodiment, not the understanding.

**Duration**: Months in a life.

## Stage 4: Rubedo

**Symbolism**: The matter goes from yellow to red. The "red king" (*rex ruber*) and the "red queen" (*regina rubra*) meet. The *coniunctio oppositorum* (the conjunction of opposites) is achieved. The philosopher's stone is produced.

**Operation**: *coniunctio* — the sulfur and the mercury, the fixed and the volatile, the male and the female, the conscious and the unconscious are joined. The result is the *lapis* — a substance that transmutes base metals into gold and confers immortality.

**Psychological**: The **integration of opposites**. The practitioner can hold paradox without splitting. They are at home in the world. The alchemists describe this as the state of the *chrysopoeia* (gold-making) — the practitioner has become gold, the integrated self.

**Symptoms**: a sense of homecoming, a quality of presence, the ability to act decisively without anxiety, a deep love of the world, an ability to be alone and in relationship without strain.

**Modern parallel**: The state of **mastery** in any field. The surgeon who operates without anxiety. The trader who trades without fear. The parent who is fully present. The artist who makes the work without struggle.

**Practice**: Stay. The rubedo is a long, stable state, not a peak experience. The alchemists warn that the practitioner at the rubedo can still fall — the *lapis* can be **lost** through pride, through attachment, through failure to continue the work.

**Duration**: A state, not a stage. The rubedo is **a way of being**, not a moment.

## The Recursive Nature

The four stages are **recursive**, not linear. The alchemist does the work, and the practitioner does it again, at a deeper level. The first cycle of the *opus* takes years; the second takes less; the third is integrated into the daily life.

Every major life transition recapitulates the four stages. A marriage, a birth, a career change, a move, a serious illness — each is a new *opus* with its own nigredo, albedo, citrinitas, and rubedo.

The master is not the one who has **finished** the work. The master is the one who has **integrated** the work into the daily life and can do it lightly, again and again, as the world requires.
