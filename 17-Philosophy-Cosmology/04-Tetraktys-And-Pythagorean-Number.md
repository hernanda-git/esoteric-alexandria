# The Tetraktys and the Pythagorean Number-Paradise

## The Claim

The **Pythagorean** tradition is the **first Western philosophical tradition** to make **number** the **fundamental principle** of reality. The Pythagoreans held that **number is the essence of all things**; the cosmos is a **mathematical structure**; the visible world is a **reflection** of the numerical order.

The doctrine is the **foundation** of the Western scientific tradition. The Pythagorean claim that **the cosmos is a mathematical structure** is the **direct ancestor** of Galileo's "the book of nature is written in the language of mathematics" and of the modern physics tradition.

The doctrine is **also** the **foundation** of the Western esoteric tradition. The Pythagorean claim that **number is sacred** — that numbers have **divine properties**, that numbers are **principles** of the cosmos — is the **direct ancestor** of the Kabbalistic gematria (see [[08-Kabbalah-Numerology/02-Gematria-Mapping]]) and the Arabic *'ilm al-huruf* (see [[02-Hurufiyya-Lettrism/02-Abjad-Numerals]]).

## The Tetraktys

The **tetraktys** (τετρακτύς, "the four") is the **sacred symbol** of the Pythagorean school. The tetraktys is a triangular arrangement of 10 dots: 1 on top, 2 below, 3 below that, 4 at the bottom.

```
        •
       • •
      • • •
     • • • •
```

The 10 dots represent the **first four numbers** (1, 2, 3, 4) and the **first three ratios** (2:1, 3:2, 4:3). The sum 1+2+3+4 = 10 is the **tetractys** (literally, "the set of four"). The number 10 was considered by the Pythagoreans to be the **most sacred** of numbers (the **Decad**, containing all the principles).

The school oath (preserved in Iamblichus, *De Vita Pythagorica*):

> "I swear by him who transmitted to our souls the tetractys, the source and root of ever-flowing nature."

The tetraktys was the **sacred symbol** that Pythagoras and his students worshipped. The symbol was **drawn** in many contexts — on the walls, on the pottery, in the ground, in the air.

## The Four Numbers

The four numbers (1, 2, 3, 4) are the **principles** of the Pythagorean cosmology. Each number is a **principle of reality**:

| Number | Name | Meaning | Element | Figure | State |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | The Monad | The source, the unity, the principle of all | The point | The dimensionless | The first |
| 2 | The Dyad | The duality, the polarity, the principle of difference | The line | The 1-dimensional | The extension |
| 3 | The Triad | The triplicity, the mediation, the principle of synthesis | The plane | The 2-dimensional | The surface |
| 4 | The Tetrad | The solid, the stability, the principle of structure | The solid | The 3-dimensional | The volume |

The progression is the **principle of dimensional generation** — from the **point** (1D), to the **line** (2D), to the **plane** (3D), to the **solid** (4D). The progression is **the same** as the modern mathematical concept of **dimension**.

The Pythagorean discovery of the **mathematical basis of harmony** (the **1:2:3:4** ratios) is the **foundation** of the entire Western musical tradition (see [[16-Science-Resonance/02-Music-Of-The-Spheres-Pythagoras]]).

## The Sacred Geometry

The Pythagorean tradition gave us the **fundamental concepts of sacred geometry**:

- The **triangle** (the **3-4-5 right triangle** — the most famous right triangle, the one that produces a perfect right angle, the one the Egyptian rope-stretchers used)
- The **square** (the 4 sides, the 4 angles, the 4 elements)
- The **pentagon** (the **golden ratio** in the diagonals, the symbol of the Pythagorean school, the symbol of the **pentagram**)
- The **hexagon** (the 6 sides, the symbol of the honeycomb, the symbol of the **dodeca-** and **icosa-**-hedra)

The **pentagram** (five-pointed star) was the **secret symbol** of the Pythagorean school. The identification of the Pythagoreans was: "the pentagram has 5 points and 5 lines; each line is divided by the other two in the **golden ratio** (1:1.618); the golden ratio is the **principle of beauty**." The Pythagoreans used the pentagram as a **secret sign** of recognition.

The **golden ratio** (the Greek letter φ, phi) is the **most famous** of the mathematical constants. The ratio (1 + √5) / 2 ≈ 1.618 appears in the pentagon, in the Fibonacci sequence, in the spiral of the nautilus shell, in the proportions of the human body, in the design of the Parthenon. The Pythagorean discovery of the golden ratio is the **foundation** of the **Western aesthetic tradition**.

## The Number-Paradise

The Pythagoreans held that the **cosmos is a number-paradise**. Each number has a **principle**, each principle has a **cosmos**, each cosmos has a **god** (in the sense of the **ruling intelligence**).

The **decad** (10) is the **most sacred** of the numbers. The Pythagoreans held that the **whole cosmos** is contained in the decad:

- 1: the **Monad** (the One, the source)
- 2: the **Dyad** (the duality, the extension)
- 3: the **Triad** (the triplicity, the synthesis)
- 4: the **Tetrad** (the solid, the stability)
- 5: the **Pentad** (the marriage, the center)
- 6: the **Hexad** (the creation, the soul)
- 7: the **Heptad** (the virgin, the mystery)
- 8: the **Ogdoad** (the harmony, the cube)
- 9: the **Ennead** (the completion, the limit)
- 10: the **Decad** (the cosmos, the all)

Each number has a **character**, a **principle**, a **symbol**, a **mythological correspondence**. The Pythagorean number-paradise is the **foundation** of the **Western numerological tradition** (see [[08-Kabbalah-Numerology/02-Gematria-Mapping]] for the Kabbalistic continuation, and [[02-Hurufiyya-Lettrism/02-Abjad-Numerals]] for the Arabic continuation).

## The Modern Reading

The Pythagorean tradition has **two modern readings**:

1. **The mathematical reading** — the Pythagoreans are the **ancestors** of the modern mathematical physics. The modern physics of **string theory**, **loop quantum gravity**, **the cosmic microwave background** is a **direct descendant** of the Pythagorean claim that the cosmos is a mathematical structure. The discovery that the **frequencies of the cosmic microwave background** fit the **harmonic series** is the **modern proof** of the Pythagorean claim.

2. **The esoteric reading** — the Pythagoreans are the **ancestors** of the Western esoteric tradition. The Pythagorean tetraktys is the **direct ancestor** of the Kabbalistic Tree of Life, the Arabic *'ilm al-huruf*, the Western alchemical tradition, the modern Western magical orders (the Golden Dawn, the OTO).

The two readings are **not exclusive**. The Pythagorean tradition is **both** the foundation of modern science and the foundation of the Western esoteric tradition. The same claim — the cosmos is a number-paradise — has **two modern descendants**, the scientific and the esoteric, that have largely diverged in the modern era but **originally** were the same.

## The Modern Note

The Pythagorean tradition is **alive** in three communities:

1. **The mathematical community** — the Pythagorean theorem (a² + b² = c²) is the most famous theorem in mathematics; it is taught in every school
2. **The musical community** — the Pythagorean tuning of the Western scale is the foundation of the Western musical tradition
3. **The esoteric community** — the Pythagorean tradition has been revived in the modern Western esoteric orders (the **Pythagorean Society**, the **Mathesis** of Basel, the **Mathers** tradition in the Golden Dawn)

The library's category 17 treats the Pythagorean tradition as the **foundation** of the Western esoteric tradition. The Pythagorean claim is the **single most important** claim in the Western esoteric history.

## The Practice

A modern practice of the Pythagorean tradition requires:

1. **The foundation** — basic geometry, basic music theory, basic number theory
2. **The texts** — read the surviving Pythagorean fragments (in Guthrie's *Sourcebook*), Aristotle's *Metaphysics* Book A, Iamblichus's *De Vita Pythagorica*, the Platonic dialogues (the *Timaeus*, the *Republic* Book VII)
3. **The practice** — work with the **tetraktys**, study the **pentagon** and the **golden ratio**, explore the **mathematical ratios** in music and in geometry
4. **The tradition** — find a teacher in the modern Pythagorean tradition
5. **The community** — the practice is best learned in a **community** of students of the Pythagorean number-paradise

The library's category 17 is the **textual foundation**. The practice requires a teacher and a tradition.
