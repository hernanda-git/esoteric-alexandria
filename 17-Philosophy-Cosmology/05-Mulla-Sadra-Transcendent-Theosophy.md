# Mulla Sadra — The Transcendent Theosophy and the Primacy of Being

## The Claim

**Sadr al-Din Shirazi** (1571-1640 CE), called **Mulla Sadra**, founded the **School of Isfahan** and the philosophical system he named **al-hikma al-muta'aliya** — the **Transcendent Theosophy**. His project was a **synthesis**: the **Peripatetic** (Avicennan) logic, the **Illuminationist** (*ishraq*, Suhrawardi) metaphysics of light, and the **Sufi** *wahdat al-wujud* of **Ibn Arabi**. The result is the most influential metaphysic produced in the Islamic world after Ibn Arabi.

Two doctrines make the system a cosmology in the sense of this category: the **primacy of being** (*asalat al-wujud*) and **trans-substantial motion** (*al-harakat al-jawhariyya*). Together they describe a **universe that is one, graded, and perpetually becoming** — a direct descendant of the Neoplatonic emanation structure (see [[01-Emanation-And-Descent]]) and a striking anticipation of modern process and monist thought.

## The Primacy of Being

Against **Avicenna**, who held that **essence** (*mahiyya*) is prior in extramental reality (the *asalat al-mahiyya*), Mulla Sadra argues that **existence** (*wujud*) is what is real. Essence is a mental abstraction; **being is the concrete reality**. This has a cosmological consequence: reality is not a pile of fixed essences but a **single, self-diffusing act of existence**, graded by intensity — stronger (necessary, luminous) at the source, weaker (contingent, dim) at the periphery.

The graded unity is the bridge to **Ibn Arabi's** *wahdat al-wujud* (see [[09-Sufism-Irfan/04-Ibn-Arabi-And-Fusus-Al-Hikam]]). Mulla Sadra is explicit that this is **not pantheism**: being is one in origin and many in manifestation, like light passing through prisms. Theologians who accused him of identifying God with the world mistook the **gradation** for a **conflation**.

## Trans-Substantial Motion

The second pillar overturns Aristotelian physics. Aristotle allowed motion only in **accidents** — place, quantity, quality. Mulla Sadra argues that **substance itself moves**: the world is in **perpetual substantial flux**, each thing continuously generated anew. Nothing is the "same" substance from one moment to the next; persistence is a function of the continuous renewal of being.

This is a **dynamic cosmology**. The cosmos is not a clockwork of fixed forms but a **stream of becoming**. The doctrine anticipates, structurally, **Whitehead's** claim that "process is primary" (*Process and Reality*) and the modern insistence that **becoming precedes being** — as well as the evolutionary and Heraclitean conviction that one cannot step into the same world twice.

## The Four Journeys

The *Asfar* is organized as the **four intellectual journeys** (*asfar*): (1) from creation to the Necessary Existent (the **ascent**, *su'ud*); (2) within the Necessary Existent in itself; (3) from the Truth to creation (the **descent**, *tanazzul*); (4) back to the Truth with the return of all things. The descent is an **emanation**; the ascent is the soul's return. The structure is the same **down-and-back** cosmogram found in Neoplatonism and the Kabbalah (see [[08-Kabbalah-Numerology/01-Tree-Of-Life]]).

## The Modern Reading

Mulla Sadra has **two** legitimate modern readings:

1. **The monist reading** — his graded *wujud* is a near-relative of **neutral monism** and **contemporary monist philosophy of mind**: one reality, appearing as matter and as consciousness depending on vantage.
2. **The process reading** — *harakat jawhariyya* makes him a **process philosopher avant la lettre**, centuries before Whitehead, and a forerunner of **emergence theory** (wholeness generated from continuous sub-strata).

Both readings keep him inside this category's remit: a **cosmological** structure (emanation, hierarchy, return) rendered as a **philosophy of becoming**.

## The Critical Note

The comparison to modern monism/process thought is **structural**, not identity. Mulla Sadra's cosmos is **theistic and teleological**; the modern readings are typically **naturalistic**. The grade of being terminates in the **Necessary Existent**; a neutral-monism does not. A careful reading holds the **parallel without collapsing the frame**.
