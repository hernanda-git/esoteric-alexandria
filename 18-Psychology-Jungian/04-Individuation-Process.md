# The Individuation Process — The Modern Opus Magnum

## The Claim

**Individuation** is Jung's term for the **process of becoming a self** — the **integration** of the conscious and the unconscious, the **wholeness** of the personality. Individuation is **not** individualism; individuation is the **opposite** of individualism (individuation is the **realization** of the **collective** in the **individual**).

Individuation is the **modern psychological statement** of the alchemical *Opus Magnum* (see [[04-Alchemy-Transmutation/01-Alchemy-Overview]]). The stages of individuation correspond to the stages of the *Opus*; the symbols of individuation correspond to the symbols of the alchemists.

The individuation process is **not** a linear, one-time event. The individuation process is **recursive**, **cyclical**, and **lifelong**. The individuation process is the **shape** of a mature life.

## The Stages

Jung described the individuation process in **several stages**. The most common 4-stage model:

### Stage 1: The Encounter with the Shadow (Nigredo)

The first stage is the **encounter with the shadow** — the **rejected, the suppressed, the dark** in the personality. The shadow is the **sum total** of the **personal unconscious**, plus the **dark archetypal material**.

The encounter is **painful**. The shadow is the **part of the self** that the conscious mind has **denied**. The encounter is the **re-owning** of the denied part.

The encounter is **necessary**. The shadow is the **first** of the archetypes. The shadow is the **first thing** that must be **integrated** before the further work can begin.

The encounter is **typical in the early adult years** (the 30s and 40s), when the individual has accumulated enough life experience to have a **substantial shadow**. The encounter can be **triggered** by a major life event (a divorce, a job loss, a serious illness, a death) or by a **psychological crisis**.

The encounter is **represented** by the alchemical **nigredo** (the blackening, the putrefaction), the encounter with the **lead**, the **dragon**, the **dark**.

### Stage 2: The Encounter with the Anima/Animus (Albedo)

The second stage is the **encounter with the anima** (in the male) or the **animus** (in the female). The anima/animus is the **soul-image** — the inner figure of the opposite sex that the psyche uses to relate to the unconscious.

The encounter is **the work of the heart**. The encounter is the **integration** of the **feminine** (in the male) or the **masculine** (in the female). The encounter is the **opposite of inflation** (the identification with the masculine only, in the male, or with the feminine only, in the female).

The encounter is **necessary**. The anima/animus is the **mediator** between the conscious and the unconscious. The encounter is the **opening of the heart**.

The encounter is **typical in the midlife years** (the 40s and 50s), when the individual has accumulated enough emotional experience to have a **substantial anima/animus**. The encounter can be **triggered** by a major relationship (a marriage, a love affair, a friendship) or by a **psychological crisis** of the heart.

The encounter is **represented** by the alchemical **albedo** (the whitening, the purification), the work with the **mercury**, the **silver**, the **moon**.

### Stage 3: The Encounter with the Self (Citrinitas)

The third stage is the **encounter with the Self** — the **totality archetype**, the **whole person**, the **center of the mandala**. The Self is the **goal** of individuation; the Self is the **archetype** that **organizes** the other archetypes.

The encounter is **the moment of insight**. The encounter is the **illumination**. The encounter is the **dawn** (the citrinitas is the **yellowing**, the color of the dawn).

The encounter is **rare**. The encounter is **only** in the mature individuation process. The encounter is **triggered** by a **peak experience**, a **spiritual opening**, a **deep insight** — or, in the alchemical tradition, by the **mystical vision**.

The encounter is **represented** by the alchemical **citrinitas** (the yellowing, the dawn), the discovery of the **chrysopoeia** (the gold-making), the vision of the **philosopher's stone**.

### Stage 4: The Realization of the Self (Rubedo)

The fourth stage is the **realization of the Self** — the **integration** of the conscious and the unconscious, the **wholeness** of the personality, the **coniunctio oppositorum** (the conjunction of opposites). The realization is the **goal**; the realization is the **end** of the *Opus Magnum*.

The realization is **the final stage**. The realization is the **state** of the integrated personality. The realization is **stable**, not a peak experience. The realization is a **way of being**, not a moment.

The realization is **rare**. The realization is **only** in the **most mature** of individuals. The realization is **the goal** of the entire process.

The realization is **represented** by the alchemical **rubedo** (the reddening, the integration), the **chrysopoeia** (the gold-making, the integration of the opposites), the **philosopher's stone** (the final product).

## The Coniunctio Oppositorum

The **coniunctio oppositorum** (the conjunction of opposites) is the **central symbol** of the individuation process. The opposites are:

- The **conscious** and the **unconscious**
- The **masculine** and the **feminine**
- The **spirit** and the **matter**
- The **light** and the **dark**
- The **above** and the **below**
- The **right** and the **left**
- The **sulfur** and the **mercury**
- The **fixed** and the **volatile**
- The **king** and the **queen**

The conjunction is **not** the **elimination** of one of the opposites. The conjunction is the **integration** of the opposites into a **third thing** — the **coniunctio**, the **reconciliation**, the **marriage** of the opposites.

The coniunctio is **represented** by:

- The **hermaphrodite** (the Rebis, the "two-thing")
- The **chymical wedding** (the Rosarium Philosophorum)
- The **mandorla** (the almond-shaped overlap of the two circles)
- The **gold** (the final product, the integration)

## The Modern Practice

A modern practice of the individuation process requires:

1. **The encounter with the shadow** — identify the **shadow material** in your life, work with it consciously. The practice of **shadow journaling** (recording the things that trigger strong negative reactions) is a good beginning.
2. **The encounter with the anima/animus** — identify the **anima/animus material** in your life, work with it consciously. The practice of **active imagination** (dialog with the inner figures) is a good beginning.
3. **The encounter with the Self** — sit with the **mandala**, the **symbol of the Self**. The practice of **daily mandala drawing** is a good beginning.
4. **The realization of the Self** — the **ongoing practice** of **integration**, the **continual** work of bringing the conscious and the unconscious into alignment.

The individuation process is **lifelong**. The individuation process is **not** a destination; the individuation process is a **way of being**.

## The Practice Note

A modern practitioner of the individuation process requires:

1. **The texts** — read Jung's *Memories, Dreams, Reflections*, *Man and His Symbols*, the *Collected Works* (the relevant volumes)
2. **The dream work** — keep a **dream journal**, record the dreams, work with the symbols
3. **The active imagination** — practice the **active imagination** technique (dialog with the unconscious figures)
4. **The mandala work** — draw a **daily mandala** (the Jungian practice)
5. **The analyst** — for serious work, find a **Jungian analyst** in a recognized institute

The library's category 18 is the **textual foundation**. The practice requires an analyst and a community.
