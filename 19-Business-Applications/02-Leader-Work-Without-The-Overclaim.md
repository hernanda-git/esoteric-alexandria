# The Leader's Work — A Clean Vocabulary

## The Honesty Note

This document **does not** claim that the leader is a magus, that the organization is a subtle body, or that business is theurgy. Those claims are **rhetorical** and the previous drafts of the cat 19 docs overclaimed. This document is **grounded** in the **actual** leadership literature — Drucker, Kotter, Kouzes & Posner, Collins, Heifetz, Goleman, Senge, Charan — and **does not** import the esoteric vocabulary for the **mythic** effect. The vocabulary of leadership is **adequate** to describe the leader's work; the **esoteric** vocabulary is **redundant** for that purpose.

The **esoteric** vocabulary is **useful** in other places (the actual magus grade, the actual theurgic practice, the actual Sufi transmission). The esoteric vocabulary is **not** useful as a **leadership** metaphor. The leader's work can be described **in** the leader's vocabulary. This document does that.

## The Leader's Work — The Three Functions

The **leader's** **work** has **three** **primary** **functions** (per Drucker, Kotter, Kouzes & Posner). The three functions are **not** the magus's vision/embodiment/action; the three functions are **specific** to the **modern** **leadership** **discipline**:

1. **Setting the direction** (per Kotter, *Leading Change*; per Senge, *The Fifth Discipline*; per Collins, *Built to Last*) — the leader **articulates** the **vision** of the **organization**; the leader **sets** the **strategy** that **moves** the **organization** toward the **vision**; the leader **communicates** the **strategy** **consistently** to the **organization**. The **direction** is **not** a **mystic** **insight**; the **direction** is a **specific** **business** **discipline** — the **art** of **deciding** **what** **the** **organization** **should** **do** and **what** **the** **organization** **should** **not** **do** (per Drucker, the **art** of **the** **effective** **decision** is the **art** of **the** **systematic** **abandonment** of **what** **is** **not** **strategic**).

2. **Aligning the organization** (per Kotter, *Leading Change*; per Heifetz, *Leadership Without Easy Answers*; per Charan, et al., *The Leadership Pipeline*) — the leader **aligns** the **organization** with the **direction** through **the** **structure**, the **systems**, the **processes**, the **people**. The **alignment** is **not** a **mystic** **embodiment**; the **alignment** is a **specific** **business** **discipline** — the **art** of **ensuring** **that** **the** **people**, **the** **structure**, the **systems** are **all** **moving** in the **same** **direction**. The **misalignment** is the **silent** **killer** of the **organization**; the **leader** is the **diagnostician** of the **misalignment**; the **leader** is the **therapist** of the **misalignment** (per Heifetz, the **art** of the **adaptive** **leadership** is the **art** of **holding** the **adaptive** **space** for the **organization** to **work** through the **adaptive** **challenge**).

3. **Motivating the people** (per Kouzes & Posner, *The Leadership Challenge*; per Goleman, *Primal Leadership*; per Drucker, *The Effective Executive*) — the leader **motivates** the **people** of the **organization** through **the** **vision**, the **recognition**, the **resonance**, the **growth**. The **motivation** is **not** a **mystic** **charisma**; the **motivation** is a **specific** **business** **discipline** — the **art** of **engaging** the **people** **in** the **work** through the **four** **practices** of the **leadership** (**model** the **way**, **inspire** a **shared** **vision**, **challenge** the **process**, **enable** **others** to **act**, **encourage** the **heart**, per Kouzes & Posner's **five** **practices**).

## The Three Functions Are Simultaneous

The **three** **functions** are **not** **sequential**; the **three** **functions** are **simultaneous**. The **leader** is **always** **setting** the **direction**, **always** **aligning** the **organization**, **always** **motivating** the **people**. The **leader** who **only** **sets** the **direction** **without** **aligning** the **organization** is **a** **strategist**, **not** a **leader**. The **leader** who **only** **aligns** the **organization** **without** **motivating** the **people** is **a** **manager**, **not** a **leader**. The **leader** who **only** **motivates** the **people** **without** **setting** the **direction** is **a** **cheerleader**, **not** a **leader**.

The **great** **leader** is **all** **three** **at** **once**.

## The Great Leader — The Five Practices of Exemplary Leadership

The **Kouzes & Posner** *Leadership Practices Inventory* (LPI) is **the** **most** **cited** **leadership** **assessment** **tool** of the **modern** **era** (the **LPI** has **been** **used** by **over** **5** **million** **managers** **worldwide**). The **LPI** is **based** **on** the **five** **practices** **of** **exemplary** **leadership** (per Kouzes & Posner, *The Leadership Challenge*):

1. **Model the Way** — the leader **clarifies** the **values**; the leader **affirms** the **shared** **values**; the leader **sets** the **example** for the **values**. The **example** is **not** a **mystic** **embodiment**; the **example** is **a** **specific** **practice** of **walking** the **talk** (per Drucker, the **leader** who **does** **not** **walk** the **talk** **is** a **politician**, **not** a **leader**).

2. **Inspire a Shared Vision** — the leader **imagines** the **future**; the leader **appeals** to the **common** **aspiration**; the leader **enlists** the **organization** **in** the **future**. The **vision** is **not** a **mystic** **intuition**; the **vision** is **a** **specific** **practice** of **articulating** **what** the **organization** **could** **be** (per Collins, the **BHAG** — the **Big** **Hairy** **Audacious** **Goal** — is the **most** **powerful** **vision** **tool**).

3. **Challenge the Process** — the leader **searches** for **opportunities**; the leader **experiments**; the leader **takes** **risks**. The **challenge** is **not** a **mystic** **ordeal**; the **challenge** is **a** **specific** **practice** of **questioning** the **status** **quo** (per Grove, the **paranoia** of the **paranoid** **leader** is the **practice** of **questioning** the **assumptions**).

4. **Enable Others to Act** — the leader **fosters** **collaboration**; the leader **strengthens** the **people**; the leader **develops** the **competence** of the **people**. The **enablement** is **not** a **mystic** **transmission**; the **enablement** is **a** **specific** **practice** of **investing** **in** the **people** (per Charan, the **leadership** **pipeline** is the **specific** **framework** for **developing** **leaders** at **every** **level**).

5. **Encourage the Heart** — the leader **recognizes** the **contributions**; the leader** **celebrates** the **values**; the leader **creates** a **community** of **the** **work**. The **encouragement** is **not** a **mystic** **love**; the **encouragement** is **a** **specific** **practice** of **acknowledging** the **human** **dignity** of the **people** (per Greenleaf, the **servant** **leadership** is the **specific** **practice** of **putting** the **people** **first**).

The **five** **practices** are **not** a **mystic** **tradition**; the **five** **practices** are **a** **specific** **leadership** **discipline** **with** **a** **specific** **methodology**, **specific** **assessments**, **specific** **outcomes**. The **LPI** is **a** **specific** **tool** for **diagnosing** the **leader**'s **strengths** and **weaknesses**; the **LPI** is **not** a **mystic** **oracle**.

## The Leader's Three Mistakes

The **leader** **makes** **three** **mistakes** (per the **leadership** **literature**):

1. **The vision mistake** (per Kotter, *Leading Change*; per Collins, *Good to Great*) — the **leader** **does** **not** **set** **the** **direction**; the **leader** **waits** for the **direction** to **emerge**; the **organization** **drifts**; the **organization** **loses** the **focus**. The **mistake** is **remedied** by **the** **discipline** of **the** **vision**.

2. **The alignment mistake** (per Heifetz, *Leadership Without Easy Answers*; per Charan, *The Leadership Pipeline*) — the **leader** **does** **not** **align** the **organization**; the **leader** **leaves** the **people** in **the** **wrong** **positions**; the **leader** **leaves** the **structure** in **the** **wrong** **form**; the **organization** **becomes** **a** **collection** of **silos**; the **organization** **loses** the **synergy**. The **mistake** is **remedied** by **the** **discipline** of **the** **alignment**.

3. **The motivation mistake** (per Goleman, *Primal Leadership*; per Kouzes & Posner, *The Leadership Challenge*) — the **leader** **does** **not** **motivate** the **people**; the **leader** **takes** the **people** for **granted**; the **organization** **loses** the **engagement**; the **organization** **loses** the **resilience**. The **mistake** is **remedied** by **the** **discipline** of **the** **motivation**.

The **three** **mistakes** are **avoidable**. The **leader** who **avoids** the **three** **mistakes** is **a** **good** **leader**; the **leader** who **makes** the **three** **mistakes** **consistently** is **a** **bad** **leader**.

## The Practice

A **modern** **leader** who **wants** the **discipline** of **the** **leadership** **tradition** can:

1. **Read** the **Drucker** (*The Effective Executive*) — the **foundation** of the **modern** **leadership** **discipline**
2. **Read** the **Kotter** (*Leading Change*) — the **most** **practical** **text** on **the** **change** **process**
3. **Read** the **Kouzes** & **Posner** (*The Leadership Challenge*) — the **most** **practical** **text** on **the** **leadership** **practices**
4. **Read** the **Heifetz** (*Leadership Without Easy Answers*) — the **most** **critical** **text** on **the** **adaptive** **leadership**
5. **Read** the **Collins** (*Good to Great*) — the **most** **practical** **text** on **the** **organizational** **transformation**
6. **Take** the **LPI** (the **Leadership Practices Inventory**) — the **most** **practical** **assessment** of **the** **leadership** **strengths** and **weaknesses**
7. **Find** a **mentor** — the **most** **practical** **way** to **develop** the **leadership** **practice**
8. **Maintain** the **practice** **for** **years** — the **leadership** **practice** is **lifelong**; the **practice** is **not** **a** **quick** **fix**

The **leader** who **adopts** the **leadership** **discipline** is **a** **leader** **who** **has** **found** a **specific** **practice** that **informs** **the** **leadership** **work**. The **leadership** **discipline** is **not** **a** **mystic** **tradition**; the **leadership** **discipline** is **a** **specific** **modern** **discipline** **with** **specific** **methodology**, **specific** **assessment**, **specific** **outcomes**.

## The Critical Note

The **leader** **is** **not** **a** **magus**. The **leader** **is** **a** **modern** **business** **role**. The **modern** **leadership** **discipline** **is** **adequate** to **describe** the **leader**'s **work**; the **esoteric** **vocabulary** is **redundant** for **that** **purpose**. The **esoteric** **vocabulary** **is** **useful** **in** **other** **places** (the **actual** **magus** **grade**, the **actual** **theurgic** **practice**, the **actual** **Sufi** **transmission**). The **esoteric** **vocabulary** is **not** **useful** as **a** **leadership** **metaphor**.

The **previous** **draft** **of** this **document** **overclaimed** the **metaphor** as **a** **structural** **identity**. The **current** **draft** **restores** the **clean** **vocabulary** of **the** **leadership** **literature** **without** the **esoteric** **overlay**.
