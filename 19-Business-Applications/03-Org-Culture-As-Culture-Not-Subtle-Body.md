# The Organization as Culture — Not as Subtle Body

## The Honesty Note

The **previous** **draft** of this document mapped the **7 chakras** to **7** **organizational** **centers** (Muladhara = foundation, Svadhisthana = creativity, Manipura = power, Anahata = heart, Vishuddha = expression, Ajna = insight, Sahasrara = purpose). The **mapping** was **poetic**; the **mapping** was **not** **operational**. The **mapping** **did** **not** **help** the **organizational** **practitioner** **diagnose** or **treat** the **organizational** **issue**. The **mapping** is **replaced** **by** a **clean** **organizational** **culture** **vocabulary** **based** on **Schein**, **Handy**, **Hofstede**, **Cameron & Quinn**, and **Laloux**. The **vocabulary** **is** **adequate** to **describe** the **organization** **as** a **culture**; the **esoteric** **vocabulary** is **redundant** for that **purpose**.

The **esoteric** **vocabulary** is **useful** **in** **other** **places** (the **actual** **chakra** **practice** in **the** **Hindu** **tradition**, the **actual** **subtle** **body** **practice** in **the** **Buddhist** **tradition**, the **actual** **qigong** **practice** in **the** **Chinese** **tradition**). The **esoteric** **vocabulary** is **not** **useful** as **an** **organizational** **metaphor**. The **organizational** **culture** **vocabulary** is **a** **specific** **modern** **discipline** **with** **specific** **methodology** and **specific** **outcomes**.

## The Edgar Schein Framework — The Foundational Text

**Edgar Schein** (1928-2023, MIT Sloan School of Management) is the **founder** of the **modern** **organizational** **culture** **discipline**. The **Schein** **framework** in **Organizational Culture and Leadership** (1985) is **the** **standard** **text** **on** the **field**. The **Schein** **framework** **distinguishes** **three** **levels** of **organizational** **culture**:

1. **Artifacts** (the **visible** level) — the **physical** **space**, the **dress** **code**, the **language**, the **rituals**, the **stories**, the **public** **documents**, the **myths**. The **artifacts** are **what** **the** **outsider** **sees**. The **artifacts** are **easy** to **observe** and **easy** to **misread** (the **artifact** **may** **be** **a** **symbol** of **the** **opposite** of the **underlying** **assumption**).

2. **Espoused values** (the **conscious** level) — the **strategies**, the **goals**, the **philosophies**, the **stated** **values**. The **espoused** **values** are **what** the **organization** **says** it **believes**. The **espoused** **values** are **easy** to **articulate** and **easy** to **disconnect** from **the** **underlying** **assumptions** (the **espoused** **values** are **the** **rhetoric**, the **underlying** **assumptions** are **the** **reality**).

3. **Basic underlying assumptions** (the **unconscious** level) — the **taken**-**for**-**granted** **beliefs**, the **unconscious** **perceptions**, the **mental** **models**, the **automatic** **interpretations**. The **basic** **underlying** **assumptions** are **what** the **organization** **actually** **believes**. The **basic** **underlying** **assumptions** are **the** **hardest** **to** **identify** and the **hardest** **to** **change** (the **basic** **underlying** **assumptions** are **the** **implicit** **paradigm** of the **organization**).

The **Schein** **framework** is **a** **diagnostic** **tool**; the **Schein** **framework** is **not** a **mystic** **oracle**. The **Schein** **framework** **is** **used** **by** **organizational** **consultants** **worldwide** to **diagnose** **organizational** **dysfunction**.

## The Charles Handy Framework — The Cultural Types

**Charles Handy** (1932-, the British organizational theorist) is **the** **author** of **Understanding Organizations** (1976), **the** **most** **popular** **British** **text** on **organizational** **theory**. The **Handy** **framework** **identifies** **four** **organizational** **culture** **types** (per **Handy**, **the** **four** **types** **are** **not** **hierarchical**; **the** **four** **types** are **descriptive**):

1. **Power culture** (the **Greek** **god** **Zeus** is the **metaphor**) — the **organization** is **organized** **around** a **central** **power**; the **decisions** are **made** **at** **the** **center**; the **organization** is **responsive** to **the** **center**; the **organization** is **fast** in **the** **center** and **slow** at **the** **edge**. The **power** **culture** is **common** in **the** **startup** **and** in **the** **small** **organization**.

2. **Role culture** (the **Greek** **god** **Apollo** is the **metaphor**) — the **organization** is **organized** **around** a **structure** of **roles**; the **decisions** are **made** **by** **the** **role**; the **organization** is **stable** and **predictable**; the **organization** is **slow** but **reliable**. The **role** **culture** is **common** in **the** **government** and in **the** **bureaucracy**.

3. **Task culture** (the **Greek** **god** **Athena** is the **metaphor**) — the **organization** is **organized** **around** a **task** or **a** **project**; the **decisions** are **made** by **the** **team**; the **organization** is **flexible** and **responsive**; the **organization** is **good** at **the** **novel** and **at** **the** **complex**. The **task** **culture** is **common** in **the** **consulting** **firm** and in **the** **research** **lab**.

4. **Person culture** (the **Greek** **god** **Dionysus** is the **metaphor**) — the **organization** is **organized** **around** the **individual**; the **organization** is **a** **collection** of **individuals**; the **organization** is **loose**; the **organization** is **good** at **the** **expert** **individual** and **at** the **creative** **individual**. The **person** **culture** is **common** in **the** **law** **firm** and in **the** **artistic** **studio**.

The **Handy** **framework** is **a** **typology**; the **Handy** **framework** is **not** a **mystic** **oracle**. The **Handy** **framework** **is** **used** **by** **organizational** **consultants** to **diagnose** the **cultural** **type** of **an** **organization** and to **advise** on the **change** of **the** **cultural** **type**.

## The Hofstede Framework — The Cross-Cultural Types

**Geert Hofstede** (1928-2020, the Dutch organizational anthropologist) is **the** **author** of **Culture's Consequences** (1980), **the** **most** **cited** **cross**-**cultural** **text** of the **modern** **era**. The **Hofstede** **framework** **identifies** **six** **dimensions** of **national** **culture** (per **Hofstede**'s **empirical** **research** **with** **over** **100,000** **IBM** **employees** **worldwide**):

1. **Power distance** — the **extent** to **which** **the** **less** **powerful** **members** of **the** **organization** **accept** **and** **expect** **the** **power** **to** **be** **distributed** **unequally**
2. **Individualism** vs. **collectivism** — the **extent** to **which** **the** **organization** **values** **the** **individual** **achievement** **vs.** the **group** **achievement**
3. **Masculinity** vs. **femininity** — the **extent** to **which** **the** **organization** **values** **the** **assertive** **vs.** the **caring**
4. **Uncertainty** **avoidance** — the **extent** to **which** **the** **organization** **tolerates** **the** **ambiguity** and the **uncertainty**
5. **Long**-**term** **orientation** vs. **short**-**term** **orientation** — the **extent** to **which** **the** **organization** **values** **the** **future** **vs.** the **present**
6. **Indulgence** vs. **restraint** — the **extent** to **which** **the** **organization** **values** **the** **gratification** **vs.** the **control**

The **Hofstede** **framework** is **a** **typology**; the **Hofstede** **framework** is **not** a **mystic** **oracle**. The **Hofstede** **framework** **is** **used** **by** **multinational** **organizations** to **diagnose** the **cultural** **differences** **between** **the** **countries** and to **advise** on **the** **cross**-**cultural** **management**.

## The Cameron & Quinn Framework — The Competing Values

**Kim Cameron** and **Robert Quinn** are **the** **authors** of **Diagnosing and Changing Organizational Culture** (1999, 3rd ed. 2011), **the** **standard** **text** on the **Competing Values Framework** (CVF). The **CVF** **identifies** **four** **organizational** **culture** **types** **based** **on** **two** **axes** (the **internal** **vs.** **the** **external** **focus**; the **flexibility** **vs.** **the** **control**):

1. **Clan culture** (the **family** **metaphor**) — the **internal** **focus**; the **flexibility**; the **organization** is **like** a **family**; the **organization** is **friendly** and **collaborative**
2. **Adhocracy culture** (the **innovation** **metaphor**) — the **external** **focus**; the **flexibility**; the **organization** is **dynamic** and **creative**; the **organization** is **good** at **the** **novel**
3. **Market culture** (the **competitor** **metaphor**) — the **external** **focus**; the **control**; the **organization** is **results**-**oriented**; the **organization** is **good** at **the** **competition**
4. **Hierarchy culture** (the **bureaucracy** **metaphor**) — the **internal** **focus**; the **control**; the **organization** is **structured** and **stable**; the **organization** is **good** at **the** **efficiency**

The **CVF** **is** **operationalized** **through** the **Organizational** **Culture** **Assessment** **Instrument** (**OCAI**), a **validated** **self**-**report** **instrument** **with** **24** **items** **rated** **on** **a** **5**-**point** **scale**. The **OCAI** **produces** **a** **cultural** **profile** of **the** **organization** **and** **compares** the **current** **profile** **to** **the** **preferred** **profile**. The **OCAI** **is** **a** **specific** **tool** **for** **diagnosing** **organizational** **culture**; the **OCAI** is **not** a **mystic** **oracle**.

## The Laloux Framework — The Teal Organization

**Frederic Laloux** is **the** **author** of **Reinventing Organizations** (2014), **a** **popular** **text** **on** the **post**-**bureaucratic** **organization** **types**. The **Laloux** **framework** **identifies** **four** **stages** of **organizational** **evolution** (the **stages** **are** **based** **on** the **stages** of **human** **development** **in** the **Wilber** **spectrum**):

1. **Red** (the **impulsive** **organization**) — the **organization** is **organized** **around** **the** **chief**; the **organization** is **chaotic**; the **organization** **is** **the** **tribal** **organization**
2. **Amber** (the **conformist** **organization**) — the **organization** is **organized** **around** the **hierarchy**; the **organization** is **stable**; the **organization** is **the** **traditional** **church** or **the** **military**
3. **Orange** (the **achievement** **organization**) — the **organization** is **organized** **around** the **goal**; the **organization** is **meritocratic**; the **organization** is **the** **modern** **corporation**
4. **Green** (the **pluralistic** **organization**) — the **organization** is **organized** **around** the **values**; the **organization** is **participative**; the **organization** is **the** **modern** **social** **enterprise**
5. **Teal** (the **evolutionary** **organization**) — the **organization** is **organized** **around** the **purpose**; the **organization** is **self**-**managed**; the **organization** is **the** **post**-**post**-**bureaucratic** **organization**

The **Laloux** **framework** is **a** **typology**; the **Laloux** **framework** is **not** a **mystic** **oracle**. The **Laloux** **framework** **is** **used** **by** **organizational** **consultants** **to** **advise** **on** the **evolution** of the **organization**.

## The Practice

A **modern** **organizational** **practitioner** **who** **wants** **the** **organizational** **culture** **discipline** **can**:

1. **Read** **Schein** — **the** **foundational** **text**
2. **Read** **Handy** — **the** **British** **text**
3. **Read** **Hofstede** — **the** **cross**-**cultural** **text**
4. **Read** **Cameron** & **Quinn** — **the** **validated** **instrument** **text**
5. **Read** **Laloux** — **the** **post**-**bureaucratic** **text**
6. **Take** **the** **OCAI** — **the** **validated** **organizational** **culture** **assessment**
7. **Find** **a** **consultant** **trained** in **the** **Schein** / **Cameron**-**Quinn** **tradition**
8. **Maintain** **the** **practice** for **years** — **the** **organizational** **culture** **discipline** is **lifelong**; **the** **discipline** is **not** **a** **quick** **fix**

The **organizational** **culture** **discipline** is **not** **a** **mystic** **tradition**; the **organizational** **culture** **discipline** is **a** **specific** **modern** **discipline** **with** **specific** **methodology**, **specific** **assessment**, **specific** **outcomes**.

## The Critical Note

The **organization** **is** **not** **a** **subtle** **body**. The **organization** is **a** **modern** **social** **system** **with** **a** **specific** **culture**. The **modern** **organizational** **culture** **discipline** is **adequate** to **describe** the **organization**; **the** **esoteric** **vocabulary** is **redundant** for that **purpose**. The **esoteric** **vocabulary** is **useful** in **other** **places** (the **actual** **chakra** **practice**, the **actual** **qigong** **practice**, the **actual** **subtle** **body** **practice**). The **esoteric** **vocabulary** is **not** **useful** as **an** **organizational** **metaphor**.

The **previous** **draft** of this **document** **overclaimed** the **metaphor** as **a** **diagnostic** **tool**. The **current** **draft** **restores** the **clean** **organizational** **culture** **vocabulary** **without** the **esoteric** **overlay**.
