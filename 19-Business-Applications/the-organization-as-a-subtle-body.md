# The Organization as a Subtle Body

## The Honesty Note

This document uses the **"organization as subtle body"** phrase as a **structural metaphor**, not a literal claim. The category's sister doc — [[03-Org-Culture-As-Culture-Not-Subtle-Body]] — corrects the overclaim that **organizational culture is literally a subtle body**. The mapping below treats the **gross/subtle/causal** triad of the Indian **subtle-body** tradition as a **heuristic** for reading an organization as a **living system** of **structure, flow, and purpose**. The **metaphor** is **useful**; the **identity** is **not** asserted.

## The Subtle-Body Schema

The **Indian** tradition distinguishes three **bodies** (the *triśarīra*): the **gross body** (*sthūla-śarīra*, the physical form), the **subtle body** (*sūkṣma-śarīra*, the energy-information vehicle of **prāṇa**, **nāḍī**, and **chakra**), and the **causal body** (*kāraṇa-śarīra*, the seed of **karma** and **identity**). Abhinavagupta's *Tantrāloka* and Pūrṇānanda's *Ṣaṭ-Cakra-Nirūpaṇa* (Woodroffe trans., 1919) systematize the **subtle** layer as the **dynamic** intermediary between **matter** and **consciousness**.

## The Three Organizational Layers

The **subtle-body** triad maps onto three **organizational** layers:

### The Gross Body — Structure

The **gross body** is the **org chart**, the **processes**, the **physical** and **digital** infrastructure. This is the **explicit** organization — the **anatomy** that can be **drawn** on a whiteboard. In Morgan's *Images of Organization* (1986), this is the **machine** and **organism** image made **concrete**. It is **necessary** but **insufficient**: a body without **breath** is a **corpse**.

### The Subtle Body — Flow

The **subtle body** is the **culture**, the **informal networks**, the **information** and **energy** that **move** through the nāḍī-like **channels** of communication. The **chakras** map to **function hubs** — finance, operations, product, sales, people — where **prāṇa** (resources, attention, trust) is **concentrated** and **distributed**. Senge's *Fifth Discipline* (1990) names this the **learning organization**: the **flow** of **shared** mental models. When the **subtle** flow is **blocked** (siloed, distrustful, signal-starved), the organization **diseases** exactly as the body **diseases** when **prāṇa** is **obstructed**.

### The Causal Body — Purpose

The **causal body** is the **mission**, the **identity**, the **karma** (the accumulated **decisions** and **destiny**) of the organization. Laloux's *Reinventing Organizations* (2014) describes the **teal** organization as one **driven** by **evolutionary purpose** rather than top-down **dictate** — the **causal** layer **authoring** the **gross** and **subtle**. Wheatley's *Leadership and the New Science* (1992) makes the same turn: the organization as a **living system** with an **implicit** order, not a **machine** to be **controlled**.

## The Viable-Systems Parallel

Stafford Beer's **Viable Systems Model** (1972) is the **closest** secular **formalization**: an organization is **viable** only when its **units** (the **sub-systems** analogous to **organs**) **recur** at every **level** and remain **coupled** by **information** flows. The VSM is the **subtle-body** diagram in **engineering** clothing: **structure** (gross), **regulation** through **information** (subtle), **identity/policy** (causal). The **mapping** is **structural**, not **mystical**.

## The Modern Mapping

| Subtle-body layer | Organizational parallel | Modern source |
|---|---|---|
| Gross (sthūla) | Org chart, process, infrastructure | Morgan 1986 |
| Subtle (sūkṣma) | Culture, networks, information flow | Senge 1990; Beer 1972 |
| Causal (kāraṇa) | Mission, identity, evolutionary purpose | Laloux 2014; Wheatley 1992 |

## The Modern Critique

The **skeptic** position: the subtle-body metaphor is a **poetic** overlay that **adds** no **predictive** content beyond **standard** organization theory. The **defender** position (this library's working hypothesis): the metaphor **restores** the **living-system** view that **mechanistic** management **forgets** — it **reminds** leaders that **culture** is **energetic**, not **decorative**, and that **purpose** is the **root**, not the **slogan**. The **honest** reading holds **both**.

## The Practice

A modern leader can **diagnose** an organization as a **body**:

1. **Gross** — is the **structure** **fit** for the **flow** it must carry?
2. **Subtle** — is **prāṇa** (trust, information, attention) **moving** freely, or **stuck** in **silos**?
3. **Causal** — is the **purpose** **alive** in daily **decisions**, or **ossified** in a **deck**?

The **treatment** follows the **layer**: **restructure** the gross, **unblock** the subtle, **reconnect** to the causal.
