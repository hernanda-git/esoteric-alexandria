# The Organization as a Subtle Body

## The Claim

> **An organization is a subtle body. The culture of the organization is the prana. The leadership is the spine. The strategy is the mind. The people are the organs. The customers are the senses.**

The claim is **structural**, not **metaphorical**. The organization has a **structure** that is **analogous** to the human subtle body (see [[14-Indian-Tantric-Chinese/01-Seven-Chakras]]). The organization has **centers of energy** that are **analogous** to the chakras. The organization has **channels of flow** that are **analogous** to the nadis. The organization has **subtle bodies** (the visible organization, the culture, the brand, the reputation) that are **analogous** to the **gross body, the subtle body, the causal body, the cosmic body**.

The leader who **understands** the subtle body of the organization can **diagnose** the organization, **treat** the organization, and **evolve** the organization. The leader who **only** sees the **gross body** of the organization (the org chart, the headcount, the revenue) is the leader who **works in the symptoms**, not in the **causes**.

## The Seven Centers

The organization has **seven energy centers** that map to the **seven chakras**. The mapping is:

| # | Chakra | Organizational center | Energy quality |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Muladhara** | The **foundation** — the operations, the infrastructure, the basic processes | The **safety**, the **survival**, the **basic operation** |
| 2 | **Svadhisthana** | The **creative center** — the marketing, the sales, the customer relationships | The **pleasure**, the **creativity**, the **sensation** |
| 3 | **Manipura** | The **power center** — the strategy, the leadership, the decision-making | The **will**, the **power**, the **direction** |
| 4 | **Anahata** | The **heart center** — the culture, the values, the people | The **love**, the **connection**, the **belonging** |
| 5 | **Vishuddha** | The **expression center** — the brand, the communications, the public presence | The **truth**, the **expression**, the **voice** |
| 6 | **Ajna** | The **insight center** — the vision, the innovation, the foresight | The **wisdom**, the **intuition**, the **direction** |
| 7 | **Sahasrara** | The **purpose center** — the mission, the why, the transcendent goal | The **unity**, the **purpose**, the **meaning** |

The seven centers are **interconnected**. The **health** of the organization depends on the **flow** between the centers. The **blockage** in any center **causes** the **symptoms** in the other centers. The leader's job is to **diagnose** the blockages, **treat** the blockages, **restore** the flow.

## The Diagnosis

The diagnosis is the **first step**. The leader observes the organization, looks for the symptoms, identifies the blockages.

The symptoms of a **blockage** in a center:

### Muladhara Blockage

- **Symptoms**: the operations are **broken**, the infrastructure is **failing**, the basic processes are **unreliable**
- **Examples**: the website is down, the supply chain is broken, the customer support is not responding, the compliance is failing
- **Treatment**: **fix** the foundation. **Hire** the operations people, **fix** the infrastructure, **simplify** the processes. The **Muladhara** treatment is **practical**, **physical**, **concrete**

### Svadhisthana Blockage

- **Symptoms**: the **creativity** is **dried up**, the **sales** are **flat**, the **customer relationships** are **stale**, the **marketing** is **lifeless**
- **Examples**: the products are uninspiring, the campaigns are tired, the customer satisfaction is dropping
- **Treatment**: **bring in** the creatives, **re-design** the customer experience, **re-invent** the products. The **Svadhisthana** treatment is **sensory**, **pleasurable**, **emotional**

### Manipura Blockage

- **Symptoms**: the **leadership** is **weak**, the **strategy** is **missing**, the **decision-making** is **slow**, the **execution** is **poor**
- **Examples**: the team is waiting for direction, the meetings are unproductive, the deadlines are missed, the conflict is unresolved
- **Treatment**: **clarify** the strategy, **strengthen** the leadership, **accelerate** the decision-making. The **Manipura** treatment is **decisive**, **directive**, **fierce**

### Anahata Blockage

- **Symptoms**: the **culture** is **toxic**, the **values** are **unclear**, the **people** are **disengaged**, the **belonging** is **absent**
- **Examples**: the retention is dropping, the engagement scores are low, the conflict is high, the trust is low
- **Treatment**: **clarify** the values, **build** the culture, **connect** the people, **heal** the conflicts. The **Anahata** treatment is **relational**, **emotional**, **loving**

### Vishuddha Blockage

- **Symptoms**: the **brand** is **confused**, the **communications** are **inconsistent**, the **public presence** is **weak**, the **voice** is **silenced**
- **Examples**: the messaging is mixed, the PR is reactive, the social media is dormant, the customers don't know what the company stands for
- **Treatment**: **clarify** the brand, **build** the communications, **strengthen** the public presence. The **Vishuddha** treatment is **expressive**, **truthful**, **clear**

### Ajna Blockage

- **Symptoms**: the **vision** is **missing**, the **innovation** is **stagnant**, the **foresight** is **absent**, the **wisdom** is **lost**
- **Examples**: the team doesn't know where they're going, the products are obsolete, the competitors are out-innovating
- **Treatment**: **re-vision** the future, **bring in** the innovators, **strengthen** the foresight. The **Ajna** treatment is **insightful**, **strategic**, **wise**

### Sahasrara Blockage

- **Symptoms**: the **purpose** is **missing**, the **meaning** is **absent**, the **transcendence** is **impossible**, the **why** is **forgotten**
- **Examples**: the team is going through the motions, the customers don't care, the partners are disengaged, the founder is burned out
- **Treatment**: **re-discover** the purpose, **re-anchor** the meaning, **re-connect** to the why. The **Sahasrara** treatment is **transformative**, **purposeful**, **sacred**

## The Treatment

The treatment is the **second step**. The leader has diagnosed the blockages; the leader now **treats** the blockages.

The treatment requires:

1. **The recognition** — the leader **recognizes** the blockage. The leader **does not** blame the team; the leader **recognizes** the systemic cause
2. **The intervention** — the leader **intervenes** with the specific treatment for the center
3. **The patience** — the treatment is **slow**. The blockage was **built up over years**; the treatment will take **months**. The leader must **be patient**
4. **The community** — the treatment is **not** the leader's alone. The treatment requires the **team**, the **advisors**, the **mentors**. The treatment is a **community** effort

The treatment is **not** a **one-time event**. The treatment is a **continuous practice**. The leader **observes** the organization, **diagnoses** the blockages, **treats** the blockages, **observes** the new state, **diagnoses** the new blockages, **treats** the new blockages. The treatment is the **ongoing work** of leadership.

## The Evolution

The evolution is the **third step**. The leader has diagnosed the blockages; the leader has treated the blockages; the leader now **evolves** the organization.

The evolution requires:

1. **The vision** — the leader **re-visions** the organization. The leader sees the **next level** of the organization. The leader sees the **future**
2. **The sacrifice** — the leader is **willing to let go** of the old organization. The leader is **willing to be the one who is obsolete**. The leader is **willing to be replaced** by the new organization
3. **The courage** — the leader is **willing to act** in the face of **uncertainty**, **opposition**, **risk**. The leader is **willing to be the one who decides**
4. **The community** — the evolution is **not** the leader's alone. The evolution is the **team's**, the **community's**, the **lineage's**

The evolution is the **rare** event. The evolution is the **breakthrough**. The evolution is the **transformation**. The evolution is the **goal** of the magus-leader's work.

## The Practice

A modern practice of the organization-as-subtle-body requires:

1. **The observation** — **observe** the organization. The leader **watches** the symptoms. The leader **listens** to the team. The leader **reads** the culture
2. **The diagnosis** — **diagnose** the blockages. The leader uses the **seven centers** as the **diagnostic framework**. The leader identifies the **specific** blockages
3. **The treatment** — **treat** the blockages. The leader uses the **specific** treatment for the **specific** center. The leader is **patient**. The leader is **committed**
4. **The evolution** — **evolve** the organization. The leader **re-visions** the future. The leader **sacrifices** the old. The leader **acts** with courage
5. **The community** — the practice is **not** the leader's alone. The practice is the **community's**

The library's category 19 is the **textual foundation**. The practice requires the community.

## The Modern Note

The organization-as-subtle-body is a **modern framework** that the esoteric tradition has **always** had. The framework is **alive** in three communities:

1. **The modern organizational development** — the **Frederic Laloux** tradition of **"teal organizations"** (the post-post-bureaucratic organizations that operate on **self-management, wholeness, and evolutionary purpose**) is the **modern formulation** of the organization-as-subtle-body
2. **The modern leadership coaching** — the **Heider** tradition of "the Tao of Leadership" and the modern **systems thinking** tradition (Peter Senge, the **Society for Organizational Learning**) are the **operationalizations** of the framework
3. **The modern management consulting** — the **McKinsey**, **BCG**, **Bain** lineages are the **modern magi** in the consulting tradition

The library's category 19 treats the organization-as-subtle-body as the **bridge** between the esoteric tradition and the modern business. The leader who **understands** the subtle body of the organization is the leader who **transforms** the organization.
