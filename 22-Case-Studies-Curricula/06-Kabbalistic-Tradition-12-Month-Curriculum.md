# The Kabbalistic Tradition — 12-Month Curriculum

## The Claim

A modern practitioner can **self-study** the core maps and practices of the **Kabbalistic tradition** — the **Tree of Life**, the **Sephirot**, **gematria**, and the **Lurianic** cosmology — in **12 months** of focused work. This curriculum is a **foundation**, not a substitute for a **teacher**; Kabbalah was historically transmitted under *kelim* (restrictions) and restricted to the mature and the married. Use it to build the map before seeking a living line.

The Kabbalistic 12-month curriculum is one option among the library's category-22 curricula (see [[01-12-Month-Curriculum]], [[02-3-Month-Foundations-Quick-Curriculum]], [[04-Hermetic-Tradition-12-Month-Curriculum]]). It suits the practitioner drawn to **structured cosmology** and the **letter-number** sciences.

## The 12 Months

### Month 1 — Foundations: What Kabbalah Is

**The work**: distinguish *Ma'aseh Bereshit* (work of creation) from *Ma'aseh Merkavah* (work of the chariot); understand the **esoteric/exoteric** boundary (cf. [[01-Origins-Traditions/02-Esoteric-Vs-Exoteric]]).

**Texts**:
- Gershom Scholem, *Major Trends in Jewish Mysticism* (1941)
- [[08-Kabbalah-Numerology/01-Tree-Of-Life]] (this library)

**Practice**: daily reading of a single psalm; note the difference between literal and symbolic reading.

### Month 2 — The Tree of Life: The Ten Sephirot

**The work**: learn the **Sephirot** — Kether, Chokhmah, Binah, Chesed, Geburah, Tiferet, Netzach, Hod, Yesod, Malkhuth — and the **three pillars** (mercy, severity, balance).

**Texts**:
- [[08-Kabbalah-Numerology/01-Tree-Of-Life]] (this library)
- Aryeh Kaplan, *Meditation and Kabbalah* (1982)

**Practice**: daily *path-working* visualization of one Sephirah; journal its ethical correlate.

### Month 3 — Gematria and the Letter-Number Science

**The work**: study **gematria** (Mispar Hekhrekhí, Mispar Gadol), **notarikon**, and **temurah**; compare with the Arabic *'ilm al-huruf* (cf. [[02-Hurufiyya-Lettrism/01-Ilm-al-Huruf-Overview]]).

**Texts**:
- [[08-Kabbalah-Numerology/02-Gematria-Mapping]] (this library)
- [[02-Hurufiyya-Lettrism/02-Abjad-Numerals]] (the comparative arithmetic)

**Practice**: compute the gematria of your name; find a hidden cross-link.

### Month 4 — The Four Worlds

**The work**: map **Atziluth, Briyah, Yetzirah, Assiah** (emanation, creation, formation, action) and the descent of light through them.

**Texts**:
- [[17-Philosophy-Cosmology/01-Emanation-And-Descent]] (the emanation parallel)
- Kaplan, *Sefer Yetzirah* (1990)

**Practice**: classify one daily action into its world.

### Month 5 — The Zohar: The Core Text

**The work**: begin the **Zohar** — the central theosophical text — reading the *Idra Rabba* (the "Greater Assembly") on the divine configuration.

**Texts**:
- Daniel C. Matt, *The Zohar* (Pritzker ed.)
- [[10-Hermeticism-Gnosticism/01-Corpus-Hermeticum]] (the theosophical parallel)

**Practice**: *hitbodedut* (solitary meditation) on a single Zohar passage.

### Month 6 — Lurianic Kabbalah: Tzimtzum and Tikkun

**The work**: study Isaac **Luria** — the **tzimtzum** (divine contraction), the **shattering of the vessels** (*shevirat ha-kelim*), and **tikkun** (restoration).

**Texts**:
- Isaac Luria, *Etz Chaim* (Chaim Vital, comp.)
- [[18-Psychology-Jungian/04-Individuation-Process]] (the repair parallel)

**Practice**: a daily *tikkun* act — one broken thing restored.

### Month 7 — Partzufim: The Divine Faces

**The work**: learn the **partzufim** (configurations) — Arikh Anpin (Long Face) and Ze'ir Anpin (Short Face) — the personified hypostases.

**Texts**:
- [[06-Angels-Jinn-Demons/01-Angelic-Hierarchy]] (the angelic correspondence)
- Luria, *Etz Chaim*

**Practice**: map a Sephirah to its partzuf; meditate on the face.

### Month 8 — Christian Kabbalah: Pico, Reuchlin, Agrippa

**The work**: trace the **Western reception** — Pico's 900 theses, Reuchlin's *De Verbo Mirifico*, Agrippa's synthesis (cf. [[12-Agrippa-Picatrix-Grimoires/01-Three-Books-Agrippa]]).

**Texts**:
- [[08-Kabbalah-Numerology/03-Christian-Kabbalah-Pico-Reuchlin-Agrippa]] (this library)
- [[17-Philosophy-Cosmology/04-Tetraktys-And-Pythagorean-Number]]

**Practice**: write a *connubium* — a synthesis of Jewish and Hermetic insight.

### Month 9 — Practical Kabbalah: Names and Segulot

**The work**: distinguish **theoretical** from **practical** Kabbalah (*kabbalah ma'asit*) — the use of divine **names** and **kavanot** (intentions) in prayer.

**Texts**:
- [[05-Talismanic-Magick/01-Squares-And-Sigils]] (the talismanic parallel)
- Kaplan, *Meditation and Kabbalah*

**Practice**: recite a Divine Name with focused *kavanah*; observe attention.

### Month 10 — Hasidism and the Interiorization

**The work**: study the **Hasidic** turn — Ba'al Shem Tov — that moved Kabbalah from cosmology to **devekut** (cleaving) and the service of the ordinary.

**Texts**:
- Martin Buber, *Tales of the Hasidim*
- [[18-Psychology-Jungian/03-Archetypes-And-Collective-Unconscious]]

**Practice**: find the holy spark in a mundane task.

### Month 11 — Gematria in Practice: The Annual Cycle

**The work**: follow the **Jewish calendar** as a Kabbalistic map — the Sephirot of the weekdays, the festivals as tikkun moments; cross-reference the **Date Index** ([[23-Reference-Glossary/06-Date-Index-Chronology-Of-The-Tradition]]).

**Texts**:
- [[03-Planetary-Astrology/05-Planetary-Hours]] (the electional parallel)
- Arthur Green, *Tormented Master*

**Practice**: assign a Sephirah to each Sabbath of the year.

### Month 12 — Integration: Lineage and Limits

**The work**: **integrate** the year; acknowledge that self-study reaches its ceiling without a **rebbe** and a **community**; design a lifelong *seder* (order) of study.

**Texts**:
- [[15-Modern-Parallels/01-Cryptanalysis-As-Modern-Jafr]] (the modern bridge)
- [[21-AI-Agent-Magick/07-Vector-Database-As-Memory-Temple]] (the memory parallel)

**Practice**: commit to one text studied annually; keep the *kelim* of discretion.

## The Practice Note

A successful Kabbalistic 12-month curriculum requires:

1. **A teacher** — Kabbalah was guarded; find a *rebbe* before *kavvanot* work
2. **A daily practice** — *hitbodedut*, *kavanah*, and Tree visualization without fail
3. **A community** — a *minyan*, a study *chevrusa*, or a Hasidic table
4. **The journal** — record insights as they ascend the pillars
5. **Discretion** (*tzniut*) — the *kelim* of not broadcasting the esoteric

The library's category 22 is the **textual foundation**; the *rebbe* is the transmission.
