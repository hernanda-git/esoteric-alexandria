# Deep-Dive Alchemy — 2-Year Curriculum

## The Claim

A modern practitioner can **master** the **Western alchemical tradition** in **2 years** of focused work. The 2-year curriculum is **not** a substitute for a **lineage teacher** (the alchemical tradition is one of the most demanding of the esoteric traditions, and the practice without a teacher is dangerous), but the 2-year curriculum is the **right size** for the **serious practitioner** who has **decided** to **commit** to the alchemical tradition.

The 2-year curriculum is **deep**: the practitioner reads **the entire corpus** of the Western alchemical tradition, **practices the symbolic operations** of the alchemical work, **integrates the tradition** with the modern psychological and philosophical traditions, and **establishes a daily practice** that continues for the rest of the practitioner's life.

## The 2 Years

### Year 1 — The Foundation: The Western Alchemical Tradition

**The work**: establish the **foundation** of the Western alchemical tradition. The practitioner reads the **major texts**, learns the **symbolic vocabulary**, practices the **basic operations**.

**The 12 months**:

#### Months 1-2 — The Hellenistic Sources

**Texts**:
- The *Corpus Alchemicum Graecum* (the foundational Greek texts, 1st-3rd c. CE)
- Zosimos of Panopolis, *On the Letter Omega* and other fragments
- Mary the Jewess, the inventor of the water bath
- Bolus of Mendes (Pseudo-Democritus)
- *The Emerald Tablet* (Tabula Smaragdina, attributed to Hermes Trismegistus)

**Practice**:
- **Read** the *Emerald Tablet* every morning for a month
- **Journal** the experience
- **Establish** the daily meditation practice

#### Months 3-4 — The Arabic-Islamic Reception

**Texts**:
- Jabir ibn Hayyan (the Latin "Geber"), the *Kitab al-Ahjar* and the *Kitab al-Azan*
- Al-Razi (Rhazes), the *Kitab al-Asrar* (Book of Secrets)
- Al-Majriti / Picatrix, the *Ghayat al-Hakim* (the Picatrix)
- Al-Buni, the *Shams al-Ma'arif* (13th c.)
- The *Book on the Nines* (an alchemical manual from the same period)

**Practice**:
- **Continue** the daily meditation
- **Begin** the dream journal (the alchemists used the dreams as the inner guidance)
- **Calculate** the abjad value of the *Emerald Tablet* and the *basmala*

#### Months 5-6 — The Latin Middle Ages

**Texts**:
- Roger Bacon, the alchemical works
- Albertus Magnus, *De Alchemia* (attributed)
- Thomas Aquinas (attributed), the *Aurora Consurgens*
- Arnold of Villanova, the alchemical works
- Ramon Llull (Pseudo-Llull), the alchemical works
- The *Rosarium Philosophorum* (1550) — the master text of the medieval alchemical tradition

**Practice**:
- **Continue** the daily meditation and the dream journal
- **Begin** the mandala drawing (the alchemical symbol of the Self)
- **Study** the illustrations of the *Rosarium*

#### Months 7-8 — The Renaissance and the New World

**Texts**:
- Paracelsus, the alchemical works
- John Dee, the alchemical works and the *Monas Hieroglyphica*
- Isaac Newton, the alchemical manuscripts (the Princeton Chymistry of Isaac Newton project)
- Michael Maier, the *Atalanta Fugiens* (1618)
- Thomas Vaughan (Eugenius Philalethes), the alchemical works
- George Starkey, the alchemical works (the mentor of Newton)

**Practice**:
- **Continue** the daily meditation, the dream journal, the mandala drawing
- **Begin** the alchemy of the body (the **outer** chemistry, the basic lab work — only with a teacher, the lab is dangerous)
- **Begin** the alchemy of the mind (the **inner** psychology, the work with the archetypes)

#### Months 9-10 — The Alchemy and the Psychology

**Texts**:
- Carl Jung, *Psychology of the Transference* (CW 16, 1946)
- Carl Jung, *Mysterium Coniunctionis* (CW 14, 1955/56)
- Carl Jung, *Aion* (CW 9ii, 1951)
- Marie-Louise von Franz, *Aurora Consurgens* (the commentary, posthumous, 2002)
- Edward Edinger, *Anatomy of the Psyche* (1985)
- James Hollis, *The Archetypal Imagination* (2000)

**Practice**:
- **Continue** the daily meditation, the dream journal, the mandala drawing
- **Begin** the shadow work — the **first** stage of the alchemical / Jungian process (the nigredo)
- **Begin** the active imagination — the **central** practice of the Jungian alchemy

#### Months 11-12 — The Modern Synthesis

**Texts**:
- William Newman, *Atoms and Alchemy* (2000) — the history of science
- Stanislas Klossowski de Rola, *The Golden Game* (1988) — the alchemical art
- Titus Burckhardt, *Alchemy* (1967) — the **best modern introduction** to the alchemical tradition
- The *Splendor Solis* (1532, attributed to Salomon Trismosin) — the most beautiful alchemical manuscript

**Practice**:
- **Continue** all the practices
- **Design** the **personal alchemical practice** — the synthesis of the 12 months
- **Establish** the **year-2 plan** — the deep-dive plan

### Year 2 — The Practice: The Alchemical Work in the Daily Life

**The work**: **live** the alchemical tradition in the daily life. The Year 2 is **not** a reading year; the Year 2 is a **practice year**. The practitioner has the **textual foundation**; the practitioner now **works** the tradition.

**The 12 months**:

#### The Daily Practice

The daily practice has **5 elements**:

1. **The morning reading** (15-30 minutes) — read one chapter or one passage from the alchemical tradition. The reading is **slow**; the reading is **meditative**; the reading is **contemplative**
2. **The morning meditation** (15-30 minutes) — sit with the alchemical symbol of the day. The symbol is **one** of the 12 main alchemical symbols (lead, gold, mercury, sulfur, salt, the king, the queen, the dragon, the rebis, the nigredo, the albedo, the rubedo). The meditation is **silent**; the meditation is **with** the symbol
3. **The day's action** (1 specific action) — perform **one** specific alchemical action in the day. The action is **small**; the action is **concrete**; the action is **symbolic** (e.g., "today I will treat a small conflict as the nigredo, the putrefaction, the dissolution")
4. **The evening journal** (15-30 minutes) — record the day's experience, the dream (if any), the meditation, the action
5. **The weekly review** (1 hour on the weekend) — review the week, identify the pattern, plan the next week

The daily practice is the **foundation**. The daily practice is the **lifelong** work.

#### The Quarterly Retreat

Every **3 months**, the practitioner takes a **3-day weekend retreat**. The retreat is **silent**, **solitary**, and **focused on one alchemical stage**. The 4 retreats are:

- **Q1 retreat (March)** — the *nigredo*. The 3 days are **dark**, **silent**, **inward**. The practitioner sits with the shadow, the suppressed, the rejected. The practitioner does **not** speak, does **not** read, does **not** act. The practitioner **is** with the dark
- **Q2 retreat (June)** — the *albedo*. The 3 days are **light**, **clear**, **discerning**. The practitioner sits with the clarity, the purity, the separation of the true from the false. The practitioner **reviews** the Q1 retreat
- **Q3 retreat (September)** — the *citrinitas*. The 3 days are **illuminating**, **insightful**, **dawning**. The practitioner sits with the insight, the illumination, the vision. The practitioner **sees** the goal
- **Q4 retreat (December)** — the *rubedo*. The 3 days are **integrating**, **conjoining**, **completing**. The practitioner sits with the integration, the conjunction, the completion. The practitioner **becomes** the philosopher's stone

The 4 retreats are the **alchemical cycle** of the year. The 4 retreats are the **seasonal** practice.

#### The Annual Synthesis

At the end of Year 2, the practitioner **synthesizes** the 2 years. The synthesis has 3 elements:

1. **The personal alchemical thesis** — a 5,000-10,000 word document that **describes** the practitioner's 2 years of work. The thesis is **the** document of the practitioner's individuation
2. **The personal alchemical practice** — a **lifelong** practice that **continues** the alchemical work
3. **The personal alchemical lineage** — a **lineage teacher** (the chain tradition) that **confers** the alchemical authority

The synthesis is the **end** of the 2-year curriculum. The synthesis is the **beginning** of the **lifelong** practice.

## The 2-Year Discipline

A successful 2-year curriculum requires **discipline**. The minimum:

- **2-3 hours per day** of reading, practice, journaling
- **1 day per week** of silent retreat (Saturday or Sunday)
- **1 weekend per quarter** of 3-day retreat
- **1 week per year** of 7-day extended retreat
- **Year-2 monthly check-in** with a teacher (in person, by video, or by phone)

The discipline is **intense**. The discipline is the **right size** for the **serious practitioner**.

## The Practice Note

A modern practitioner of the 2-year curriculum requires:

1. **The texts** — read the texts in the **order given**. The order is **deliberate**
2. **The practice** — practice **the discipline**. The practice is the **lifelong** work
3. **The teacher** — find a **lineage teacher** in the alchemical tradition. The 2-year curriculum is **not safe** without a teacher
4. **The community** — find a **community** of alchemical practitioners (a local group, an online group, a school)
5. **The journal** — keep a **lifelong journal** of the alchemical work

The library's category 22 is the **textual foundation**. The practice requires the chain.
