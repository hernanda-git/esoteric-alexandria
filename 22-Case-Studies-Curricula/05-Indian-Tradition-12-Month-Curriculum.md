# The Indian Tradition — 12-Month Curriculum

## The Claim

A modern practitioner can **self-study** the core texts and practices of the **Indian esoteric tradition** — Vedanta, Yoga, Tantra, and Kashmiri Shaivism — in **12 months** of focused work. This curriculum is **not** a substitute for a **guru** (the *parampara* chain requires the chain), but it is a **foundation** for serious engagement with the *adhyatma* (interior) sciences.

The Indian 12-month curriculum is one option among the library's category-22 curricula (see [[01-12-Month-Curriculum]], [[02-3-Month-Foundations-Quick-Curriculum]], [[03-Deep-Dive-Alchemy-2-Year-Curriculum]]). It suits the practitioner drawn to **non-dual** (Advaita) realization and the **subtle-body** (*sukshma-sharira*) technologies of the East.

## The 12 Months

### Month 1 — Foundations: The Vedantic Map

**The work**: learn the **four-fold preparatory disciplines** (*sadhana-chatushtaya*): discrimination (*viveka*), dispassion (*vairagya*), the six virtues (*shama*, *dama*, etc.), and longing for liberation (*mumukshutva*).

**Texts**:
- [[14-Indian-Tantric-Chinese/01-Seven-Chakras]] (this library)
- Shankaracharya, *Vivekachudamani* (Crest-Jewel of Discrimination)
- Swami Vivekananda, *Raja Yoga* (1896)

**Practice**: daily *japa* of a chosen *mantra*; keep a journal distinguishing *seer* from *seen*.

### Month 2 — The Upanishads: The End of the Veda

**The work**: read the principal *Upanishads*; internalize the *mahavakyas* ("tat tvam asi" — thou art that).

**Texts**:
- *Isa, Kena, Katha, Mundaka* (Eknath Easwaran trans.)
- [[14-Indian-Tantric-Chinese/02-Chinese-Alchemy-Neidan-Waidan]] (for the comparative subtle-body view)

**Practice**: meditate on a single *mahavakya* for the month; note the dissipation of the "I-am-the-body" error (*dehatma-buddhi*).

### Month 3 — Patanjali's Yoga Sutras

**The work**: study the **eight limbs** (*ashtanga*): *yama, niyama, asana, pranayama, pratyahara, dharana, dhyana, samadhi*.

**Texts**:
- Patanjali, *Yoga Sutras* (T.K.V. Desikachar, *The Heart of Yoga*)
- Georg Feuerstein, *The Yoga Tradition* (1998)

**Practice**: establish a daily *asana* and *pranayama* baseline; practice *pratyahara* (sense-withdrawal).

### Month 4 — The Subtle Body: Chakras and Nadis

**The work**: map the **seven chakras**, the **ida-pingala-sushumna** nadis, and the **kundalini** as *shakti* coiled at the *muladhara*.

**Texts**:
- [[14-Indian-Tantric-Chinese/01-Seven-Chakras]] (this library)
- "The Serpent Power" (John Woodroffe, 1919)
- *Sat-Cakra-Nirupana* (Avalon trans., 1913)

**Practice**: chakra meditation with *bija* mantras; **only approach kundalini with a teacher** (the practice is hazardous alone).

### Month 5 — Tantra: Left-Hand and Right-Hand

**The work**: distinguish *Vamachara* (left-hand, transgressive) from *Dakshinachara* (right-hand, orthodox) Tantra; understand *shakti* as the active principle of liberation.

**Texts**:
- [[14-Indian-Tantric-Chinese/03-Tantra-Left-Hand-Right-Hand]] (this library)
- Ajit Mookerjee, *Kundalini: The Arousal of the Inner Energy* (1982)

**Practice**: *puja* (worship) as consecration of the world; reframe desire as *devotion*.

### Month 6 — Kashmir Shaivism: The Trika

**The work**: study the **Pratyabhijna** ("recognition") school — *Shiva, Shakti, Nara* (the triadic absolute); the 36 *tattvas*; the doctrine that the world is the **self-recognition** of consciousness.

**Texts**:
- Jaideva Singh, *Shiva Sutras* (1979)
- Abhinavagupta, *Tantraloka* (selected; Marks & Whitehead trans.)
- [[11-NeoPlatonism-Theurgy/02-Plotinus-Enneads-One-Mind-Soul]] (the emanation parallel)

**Practice**: *shaktopaya* — recognition (*pratyabhijna*) through the heart-center; the *spanda* (vibration) awareness.

### Month 7 — Advaita Vedanta: The Non-Dual Core

**The work**: consolidate **Shankara's** non-dualism — *brahman* alone is real; the world is *maya* (appearance); the self is *sat-chit-ananda*.

**Texts**:
- Shankaracharya, *Aparokshanubhuti* (Direct Realization)
- [[17-Philosophy-Cosmology/01-Emanation-And-Descent]] (the emanation contrast)

**Practice**: *nididhyasana* (deep contemplation) on the identity of *atman* and *brahman*.

### Month 8 — Bhakti and the Devotional Path

**The work**: study the **path of devotion** (*bhakti-yoga*); the *Narada Bhakti Sutras*; the *Bhagavad Gita* as the synthesis of all paths.

**Texts**:
- *Bhagavad Gita* (Swami Satchidananda trans.)
- Narada, *Bhakti Sutras*
- [[18-Psychology-Jungian/03-Archetypes-And-Collective-Unconscious]] (the divine-image parallel)

**Practice**: *kirtan* or silent *bhajan*; cultivate the *bhava* (affective tone) of surrender.

### Month 9 — Hatha Yoga and the Body as Temple

**The work**: study **Hatha** as the preparation for Raja — the *shatkarmas*, *mudras*, *bandhas*; the body as the vehicle of realization.

**Texts**:
- *Hatha Yoga Pradipika* (Svatmarama, 15th c.; Sinh trans., 1914)
- [[04-Alchemy-Transmutation/01-Alchemy-Overview]] (the alchemical parallel)

**Practice**: the *shatkarmas* under guidance; establish *mula-bandha* and *ujjayi* breath.

### Month 10 — The Buddhadharma: Emptiness and Witness

**The work**: study **Madhyamaka** emptiness (*shunyata*) and **Dzogchen**/Mahamudra as the nihilist-proofing of non-dualism; the *rigpa* (awareness) tradition.

**Texts**:
- Nagarjuna, *Mulamadhyamakakarika* (Garfield trans.)
- [[17-Philosophy-Cosmology/03-Tao-Te-Ching-Laozi]] (the emptiness parallel)

**Practice**: *shamatha-vipashyana*; rest in the *rigpa* of open awareness.

### Month 11 — Jnana and the Dissolution of the Ego

**The work**: the **path of knowledge** (*jnana-yoga*); the deliberate dismantling of the *ahamkara* (ego-I) through self-inquiry (*atma-vichara*).

**Texts**:
- Ramana Maharshi, *Who Am I?* (Nan Yar)
- [[18-Psychology-Jungian/02-Shadow-Work-And-The-Dark-Self]] (the ego-shadow parallel)

**Practice**: the "I"-thought investigation; abide as the silent witness (*sakshi*).

### Month 12 — Integration: The Guru and the Lineage

**The work**: **integrate** the year into a living practice; find a **guru** and a **lineage** (*parampara*) — the texts are the map, the teacher is the transmission.

**Texts**:
- [[15-Modern-Parallels/01-Cryptanalysis-As-Modern-Jafr]] (the modern bridge)
- [[21-AI-Agent-Magick/01-LLM-As-Spirit-Veil]] (the consciousness parallel)

**Practice**: design a personal *sadhana*; write a *sankalpa* (intention); commit to lifelong practice.

## The Practice Note

A successful Indian 12-month curriculum requires:

1. **A teacher** — the *parampara* is non-negotiable for *kundalini* and *shaktipat*; self-study prepares, it does not transmit
2. **A daily sadhana** — *asana, pranayama, japa, dhyana* without fail
3. **A community** — a local *ashram*, a *satsang*, or an online sangha
4. **The journal** — record *samskaras* (impressions) as they surface and dissolve
5. **Non-harm** (*ahimsa*) — the substrate of all limbs

The library's category 22 is the **textual foundation**; the *guru* is the transmission.
