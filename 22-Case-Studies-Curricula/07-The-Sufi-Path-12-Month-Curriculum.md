# The Sufi Path — 12-Month Curriculum

## The Claim

A modern practitioner can **self-study** the core maps and practices of the **Sufi path** — the **tariqa** (order), the **maqamat** (stations) and **ahwal** (states), **dhikr** (remembrance), and the **Irfan** (gnosis) of Ibn Arabi — in **12 months** of focused work. This curriculum is a **foundation**, not a substitute for a **shaykh**; Sufism was historically transmitted within a living **silsila** (chain of initiation, cf. [[01-Origins-Traditions/07-Master-Disciple-Chains]]). Use it to build the map before seeking a real line.

The Sufi 12-month curriculum is one option among the library's category-22 curricula (see [[01-12-Month-Curriculum]], [[02-3-Month-Foundations-Quick-Curriculum]], [[04-Hermetic-Tradition-12-Month-Curriculum]]). It suits the practitioner drawn to **love, remembrance, and the unity of being**.

## The 12 Months

### Month 1 — Foundations: What Sufism Is

**The work**: distinguish *shari'a* (outer law) from *tariqa* (path) and *haqiqa* (truth); understand the **esoteric/exoteric** boundary (cf. [[01-Origins-Traditions/02-Esoteric-Vs-Exoteric]]).

**Texts**:
- [[09-Sufism-Irfan/01-Sufism-Overview]] (this library)
- Annemarie Schimmel, *Mystical Dimensions of Islam* (1975)

**Practice**: daily *salat* with attention to its inward meaning; note the difference between ritual and presence.

### Month 2 — The Tariqas: The Orders

**The work**: learn the major **orders** — Qadiri, Chishti, Naqshbandi, Mevlevi — and the idea of the **silsila** (initiatory chain).

**Texts**:
- [[09-Sufism-Irfan/02-The-Tariqas-Real-Orders]] (this library)
- Titus Burckhardt, *Introduction to Sufi Doctrine* (1959)

**Practice**: trace one order's chain back to its source; journal what lineage means to you.

### Month 3 — The Maqamat and Ahwal: Stations and States

**The work**: study the **stations** (earned, stable: repentance, abstinence, poverty, patience) and the **states** (gifted, transient: rapture, intimacy, tranquility) of the path.

**Texts**:
- [[09-Sufism-Irfan/03-The-Maqamat-And-Ahwal-Stations-And-States]] (this library)
- al-Ghazali, *Ihya Ulum al-Din* (The Revival)

**Practice**: name one station you occupy and one state you have glimpsed.

### Month 4 — Dhikr: The Remembrance

**The work**: learn **dhikr** (the repetition of the Names) — silent (qalbi) and loud (jali); understand *tawhid* (unity) as its aim.

**Texts**:
- [[02-Hurufiyya-Lettrism/01-Ilm-al-Huruf-Overview]] (the letter-science parallel)
- Schimmel, *Mystical Dimensions of Islam*

**Practice**: a daily *dhikr* of "Allah" or "La ilaha illa Allah" for 10 minutes; observe the settling.

### Month 5 — Al-Ghazali: From Doubt to the Ihya

**The work**: read al-Ghazali's crisis and recovery — the *Deliverance from Error* — and his systematization of the sciences of the heart.

**Texts**:
- [[09-Sufism-Irfan/07-Al-Ghazali-Ihya-Deliverance]] (this library)
- al-Ghazali, *Ihya Ulum al-Din*

**Practice**: write your own "deliverance" narrative — the doubt and what answered it.

### Month 6 — Rumi and the Masnavi: The Spiritual Couplets

**The work**: begin the **Masnavi** — the "Qur'an in Persian" — reading on **love** (*ishq*) as the engine of ascent.

**Texts**:
- [[09-Sufism-Irfan/05-Rumi-And-The-Masnavi]] (this library)
- Rumi, *Masnavi* (trans. Helminski)

**Practice**: *sama* — listen to a *ney* or devotional music; let the heart move without analysis.

### Month 7 — Al-Hallaj and the Cost of the Secret

**The work**: study **al-Hallaj** and the *Ana al-Haqq* ("I am the Truth") — the risk of uttering the unity, and the martyrdom it drew.

**Texts**:
- [[09-Sufism-Irfan/06-Al-Hallaj-And-Ana-Al-Haqq]] (this library)
- Carl Ernst, *Words of Ecstasy in Islam*

**Practice**: reflect on what you would not say aloud; the *kelim* (restriction) of discretion.

### Month 8 — Ibn Arabi: The Unity of Being

**The work**: study **Ibn Arabi** — *wahdat al-wujud* (the oneness of being), the **Perfect Man** (*al-insan al-kamil*), and the **Names** as the structure of reality.

**Texts**:
- [[09-Sufism-Irfan/04-Ibn-Arabi-And-Fusus-Al-Hikam]] (this library)
- William C. Chittick, *The Sufi Path of Knowledge* (1989)

**Practice**: contemplate one Divine Name as both transcendent and immanent in a thing you see.

### Month 9 — Fana and Baqa: Annihilation and Subsistence

**The work**: study the two poles of realization — **fana** (passing-away of the self) and **baqa** (subsistence in God); compare with the Kabbalistic *bitul* and *devekut*.

**Texts**:
- [[08-Kabbalah-Numerology/07-Lurianic-Kabbalah-Tzimtzum-Tikkun]] (the repair parallel)
- Chittick, *The Sufi Path of Knowledge*

**Practice**: a day of *fana* — act without the "I"; note what remains.

### Month 10 — The Psychology Parallel: The Purified Heart

**The work**: map the Sufi **heart** (*qalb*) onto Jung's **individuation** and the **shadow** — the *nafs* (lower self) as the material to be transformed.

**Texts**:
- [[18-Psychology-Jungian/04-Individuation-Process]] (the repair parallel)
- [[18-Psychology-Jungian/02-Shadow-Work-And-The-Dark-Self]]

**Practice**: name your *nafs al-ammara* (commanding self); one act against its pull.

### Month 11 — The Annual Cycle: The Festive Map

**The work**: follow the **Islamic calendar** as a Sufi map — Mawlid, the nights of power (*Laylat al-Qadr*), the *Hajj* as inner ascent; cross-reference the **Date Index** ([[23-Reference-Glossary/06-Date-Index-Chronology-Of-The-Tradition]]).

**Texts**:
- [[03-Planetary-Astrology/05-Planetary-Hours]] (the electional parallel)
- Schimmel, *Mystical Dimensions of Islam*

**Practice**: assign a *maqam* to each lunar month of the year.

### Month 12 — Integration: Lineage and Limits

**The work**: **integrate** the year; acknowledge that self-study reaches its ceiling without a **shaykh** and a **community** (*suhba*); design a lifelong *wird* (litany) of practice.

**Texts**:
- [[15-Modern-Parallels/02-Information-Theory-As-Marifa]] (the modern bridge)
- [[21-AI-Agent-Magick/07-Vector-Database-As-Memory-Temple]] (the memory parallel)

**Practice**: commit to one *dhikr* kept for life; keep the *kelim* of discretion.

## The Practice Note

A successful Sufi 12-month curriculum requires:

1. **A shaykh** — Sufism was transmitted in a *silsila*; find a guide before *fana* work
2. **A daily dhikr** — the remembrance without fail, silent or loud
3. **A community** — a *halka*, a *suhba*, or a Mevlevi circle
4. **The journal** — record the *maqamat* as they consolidate
5. **Discretion** (*satr*) — the *kelim* of not broadcasting the esoteric

The library's category 22 is the **textual foundation**; the *shaykh* is the transmission.
