# The 3-Month Foundations — Quick Curriculum

## The Claim

A modern practitioner can **establish the foundation** of the esoteric tradition in **3 months** of focused work. The 3-month curriculum is **not** a substitute for the **12-month curriculum** (see [[01-12-Month-Curriculum]]) or the **2-year deep-dive curriculum** (see [[03-Deep-Dive-Alchemy-2-Year-Curriculum]]), but the 3-month curriculum is the **right size** for the **beginner** who wants to **know what this is** before committing to the longer curriculum.

The 3-month curriculum is **also** the right size for the **seasonal practitioner** who has **1-2 hours per day** to dedicate to the study.

## The 3 Months

### Month 1 — What Is Esotericism?

**The work**: understand the **esoteric tradition** as a whole. Read the foundations, the definitions, the transmission chains, the perennial philosophy.

**Texts**:
- [[01-Origins-Traditions/01-What-Is-Esotericism]] (this library)
- [[01-Origins-Traditions/02-Esoteric-Vs-Exoteric]] (this library)
- [[01-Origins-Traditions/05-Perennial-Philosophy]] (this library)
- Wouter Hanegraaff, *Western Esotericism: A Guide for the Perplexed* (2013)
- Antoine Faivre, "Theosophy, Imagination, Tradition" (2000)

**Practice**:
- **Read** the library's category 01 from start to finish
- **Journal** — what is the tradition? What draws me? What challenges me?
- **Identify** the **one** tradition that **calls** to you (the next 2 months will focus on this tradition)

### Month 2 — The Deep Dive (one tradition)

**The work**: **deep dive** into the **one tradition** that calls to you. The deep dive is **focused**: the practitioner reads **the major texts** of the chosen tradition, **practices the basic techniques**, **journals the experience**.

**The 5 deep-dive options**:

#### Option A — The Islamic Tradition

- [[02-Hurufiyya-Lettrism/01-Ilm-al-Huruf-Overview]] (this library)
- The *Shams al-Ma'arif* (Al-Buni, 13th c., the core text)
- The *Fusus al-Hikam* (Ibn Arabi, 13th c.)
- Practice: the *dhikr* (remembrance), the abjad calculation

#### Option B — The Western Esoteric Tradition

- [[10-Hermeticism-Gnosticism/01-Corpus-Hermeticum]] (this library)
- The *Corpus Hermeticum* (Copenhaver translation)
- The *Three Books of Occult Philosophy* (Agrippa)
- Practice: the *Poimandres* meditation, the planetary sigil construction

#### Option C — The Kabbalistic Tradition

- [[08-Kabbalah-Numerology/01-Tree-Of-Life]] (this library)
- The *Sefer Yetzirah* (Kaplan translation)
- The *Zohar* (Sperling translation)
- Practice: the sephirot meditation, the gematria calculation

#### Option D — The Alchemical Tradition

- [[04-Alchemy-Transmutation/01-Alchemy-Overview]] (this library)
- The *Rosarium Philosophorum* (1550)
- The *Aurora Consurgens* (14th c., von Franz commentary)
- Practice: the dream journal, the mandala drawing

#### Option E — The Jungian Tradition

- [[18-Psychology-Jungian/01-Jung-And-Alchemy]] (this library)
- Jung, *Memories, Dreams, Reflections* (1963)
- Jung, *Man and His Symbols* (1964)
- Practice: the dream journal, the active imagination, the shadow work

**The choice is yours**. The 5 options are **the entry points** to the 5 major Western / Islamic traditions. The Indian, Chinese, and other traditions are also available; the practitioner can substitute the relevant library category and the relevant modern source.

### Month 3 — The Practice and the Decision

**The work**: **establish a daily practice** based on the deep dive. The daily practice is **the integration** of the 2 months of reading. The daily practice is the **lifelong** work.

**The daily practice** has **5 elements**:

1. **The morning reading** — 15-30 minutes of the chosen tradition's texts
2. **The morning meditation** — 15-30 minutes of the chosen tradition's meditation
3. **The day's action** — 1 specific action aligned with the chosen tradition
4. **The evening journal** — 15-30 minutes of recording the experience
5. **The weekly review** — 1 hour on the weekend to review the week's practice

The daily practice is **the foundation**. The daily practice is the **lifelong** work.

**The decision** at the end of the 3 months is **simple**: do you **continue** the chosen tradition? If yes, the practitioner commits to the **12-month curriculum** (see [[01-12-Month-Curriculum]]) or the **2-year deep-dive curriculum** (see [[03-Deep-Dive-Alchemy-2-Year-Curriculum]]). If no, the practitioner returns to the **Month 1** and chooses **another tradition**.

## The 3-Month Discipline

A successful 3-month curriculum requires **discipline**. The minimum:

- **1-2 hours per day** of reading, practice, journaling
- **1 day per week** of silent reflection (Saturday or Sunday)

The discipline is **less intense** than the 12-month curriculum. The discipline is **the right size** for the **beginner**.

## The Practice Note

A modern practitioner of the 3-month curriculum requires:

1. **The texts** — read the texts in the **order given**. The order is **deliberate**
2. **The practice** — practice **the discipline**. The practice is the **lifelong** work
3. **The decision** — at the end of 3 months, **decide** whether to **continue** or to **choose another tradition**
4. **The community** — find a **community** of practitioners (a local group, an online group, a teacher)

The library's category 22 is the **textual foundation**. The practice requires the community.
