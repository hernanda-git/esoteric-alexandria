# The Hermetic Tradition — 12-Month Curriculum

## The Claim

A practitioner can study the **Hermetic stream** of Western esotericism — the Hermetica, the Renaissance revival, and the operative orders — in **12 months** of focused work. This curriculum is **one path** among the library's category 22 offerings (see [[01-12-Month-Curriculum]] for the cross-tradition plan). It is a **foundation**, not a substitute for a **lineage teacher** (the chain tradition requires the chain).

The Hermetic path is the **backbone** of Western esotericism: it connects late-antique Alexandria to the Renaissance, to the grimoire tradition ([[12-Agrippa-Picatrix-Grimoires]]), to alchemy ([[04-Alchemy-Transmutation]]), and to modern magic ([[21-AI-Agent-Magick]]).

## The 12 Months

### Month 1 — The Hermetica: The Ancient Sources

**The work**: read the **Corpus Hermeticum** — the 17 Greek treatises attributed to Hermes Trismegistus — and the **Asclepius**. Grasp the core doctrines: the **unity of the All**, the **divine mind (Nous)**, the **ascent of the soul**, and the **recapitulation** ("thou art a little cosmos").

**Texts**:
- [[10-Hermeticism-Gnosticism/01-Corpus-Hermeticum]] (this library)
- Brian Copenhaver, *Hermetica* (1992)
- Garth Fowden, *The Egyptian Hermes* (1986)

**Practice**: daily contemplative reading of one tractate; journal the *Poimandres* cosmogony.

### Month 2 — The Perennial Frame

**The work**: place Hermes inside the **perennial philosophy** and the **esoteric/exoteric** distinction.

**Texts**:

### Month 12 — Synthesis and the Ascent

**The work**: re-read the *Asclepius*; perform a personal **recapitulation**; write a one-page **Hermetic credo**.

**Practice**: a weekly contemplative ascent (the *Ascent of the Soul* from Poimandres); a final synthesis essay mapping the 11 months onto the **emanation** structure.

## The Note

This curriculum is a **reading and contemplative** plan, not an initiation. It follows the library's house rule: map the tradition onto **modern categories** where useful (mechanism, system, agent), but never overclaim identity. Hermes is a **real historical current**; the curriculum is a **real study plan**.
