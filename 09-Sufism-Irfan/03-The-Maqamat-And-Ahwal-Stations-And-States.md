# The Maqamat and the Ahwal — Stations and States

## The Definition

A **maqam** (Arabic مقام, "station", "place", "standing-place") is a **stable** **spiritual station** that the **practitioner** **attains** through **effort**, **discipline**, and **divine grace**. The maqam is **permanent**; the maqam is **achieved**; the maqam is **maintained** through **practice**.

A **hal** (Arabic حال, "state") is a **transient** **spiritual state** that the **practitioner** **receives** through **divine gift** alone. The hal is **temporary**; the hal is **uncontrolled**; the hal is **not** **maintained** through **effort** but **received** through **grace**.

The **distinction** is **fundamental** to the Sufi tradition. The **distinction** is **formulated** by **al-Hakim al-Tirmidhi** (c. 880 CE), **al-Qushayri** (1027 CE), **al-Sarraj** (c. 950 CE), and **al-Kalabadhi** (c. 990 CE). The **distinction** is **not** the **same** as the **distinction** between **active** and **passive** (the **active** is the **maqam**, the **passive** is the **hal**, but the **distinction** is **deeper** than the **active/passive** distinction — the **maqam** is **both** the **effort** and the **grace**, the **hal** is **purely** the **grace**).

The **maqam** is **stable**, the **hal** is **transient**. The **practitioner** **works** the **maqam**; the **practitioner** **receives** the **hal**. The **maqam** is the **stable** **result**; the **hal** is the **transient** **gift**. The **practitioner** **cannot** **manufacture** the **hal**; the **practitioner** can **prepare** the **ground** for the **hal** through the **maqam**.

## The Classical Sequence of the Maqamat

The **classical** Sufi tradition (the **Qushayri**, the **Sarraj**, the **Kalabadhi**, the **Sulami**) gives a **standard** **sequence** of the **maqamat** (the **sequence** is **not** **universal** — the **order** of the **maqamat** is **disputed** among the **Sufi** scholars — but the **classical** **sequence** is **consistent** in the **Qushayri** and the **Sarraj**).

The **classical** **sequence** has **seven** **maqamat** (the **seven** is **not** **universal** — the **Kharraz** (c. 890 CE) has **six**, the **Tirmidhi** has **eight**, the **Makki** has **ten**, the **Ibn Arabi** tradition has **fifteen**):

1. **Tawba** (التوبة, "repentance") — the **first** **maqam**. The **practitioner** **turns** from the **world** to **God**. The **tawba** is the **return** to the **origin**, the **U-turn** of the **soul**, the **first** **step** of the **path**. The **tawba** is **not** **regret** (the **tawba** is **not** the **Western** "regret"); the **tawba** is **conversion**, the **change** of the **direction** of the **life** from the **world** to **God**.
2. **Zuhd** (الزهد, "asceticism", "renunciation") — the **practitioner** **renounces** the **world**. The **zuhd** is **not** the **rejection** of the **world** (the **zuhd** is **not** the **Christian** asceticism); the **zuhd** is the **right** **use** of the **world** (the **zuhd** is the **non-attachment** to the **world** while **using** the **world** for the **purpose** of **God**).
3. **Sabr** (الصبر, "patience", "endurance") — the **practitioner** **endures** the **trial** and the **ordeal**. The **sabr** is the **patience** in the **face** of the **trial**; the **sabr** is the **endurance** of the **suffering**; the **sabr** is the **perseverance** in the **practice**. The **sabr** is **not** the **passive** **resignation** (the **sabr** is **not** the **Western** "patience"); the **sabr** is the **active** **endurance**, the **work** of the **soul** in the **face** of the **ordeal**.
4. **Faqr** (الفقر, "poverty") — the **practitioner** **becomes** **poor** in the **sight** of **God**. The **faqr** is **not** the **material** **poverty** (the **faqr** is **not** the **dervish** **beggar**); the **faqr** is the **spiritual** **poverty**, the **recognition** that the **practitioner** **has** **nothing** of **himself** and **everything** of **God**. The **faqr** is the **first** **maqam** of the **practitioner** in the **technical** **Sufi** **sense** (the **practitioner** is the **faqir**).
5. **Tawakkul** (التوكل, "reliance") — the **practitioner** **relies** on **God** **alone**. The **tawakkul** is **not** the **rejection** of the **means** (the **tawakkul** is **not** the **neglect** of the **means**); the **tawakkul** is the **reliance** on **God** **through** the **means**, the **trust** that the **means** are **from** **God** and the **end** is **from** **God**. The **tawakkul** is **especially** associated with the **Qadiriyya** (the **Qadiri** tariqa is the **tariqa** of the **tawakkul**).
6. **Rida** (الرضا, "satisfaction", "contentment") — the **practitioner** is **satisfied** with **what** **God** **gives**. The **rida** is **not** the **stoic** **acceptance** (the **rida** is **not** the **Western** "stoicism"); the **rida** is the **satisfaction** with the **divine** **decree**, the **acceptance** of the **divine** **will** in **all** **circumstances**, the **contentment** with the **divine** **gift**. The **rida** is **especially** associated with the **Shadhiliyya** (the **Shadhili** tariqa is the **tariqa** of the **rida**).
7. **Tawhid** (التوحيد, "unity", "the affirmation of the oneness of God") — the **practitioner** **realizes** the **unity** of **God**. The **tawhid** is **not** the **intellectual** **affirmation** of the **oneness** of **God** (the **tawhid** is **not** the **theological** **doctrine**); the **tawhid** is the **experiential** **realization** of the **oneness** of **God**, the **mystical** **unification** of the **practitioner** with the **divine**. The **tawhid** is the **goal** of the **path**.

The **seven** **maqamat** are the **standard** **sequence**. The **practitioner** **progresses** through the **seven** **maqamat** in **order** (the **order** is **not** **strict** — the **practitioner** can **revisit** a **maqam** at a **deeper** **level**). The **practitioner** **attains** the **maqam** through **effort** and **grace**. The **practitioner** **maintains** the **maqam** through **practice**. The **practitioner** **receives** the **hal** at the **maqam** through **grace** alone.

## The Ahwal (States)

The **ahwal** (Arabic أحوال, "states", plural of **hal**) are the **transient** **spiritual** **states** that the **practitioner** **receives** at the **maqam**. The **classical** Sufi tradition gives **four** **ahwal** (the **four** is **not** **universal** — the **Qushayri** has **ten**, the **Sarraj** has **seven**, the **Ibn Arabi** tradition has **many**):

1. **Muraqaba** (المراقبة, "vigilance", "watchfulness") — the **state** of **watchfulness**, the **state** of **awareness** of the **presence** of **God**. The **muraqaba** is the **state** of the **practitioner** who is **aware** of **God** at **every** **moment**. The **muraqaba** is **especially** associated with the **Chishtiyya** (the **Chishti** tariqa is the **tariqa** of the **muraqaba**).
2. **Qurb** (القرب, "nearness") — the **state** of **nearness** to **God**. The **qurb** is the **state** of the **practitioner** who is **near** to **God** in the **experience** of the **practitioner**. The **qurb** is **especially** associated with the **Naqshbandiyya** (the **Naqshbandi** tariqa is the **tariqa** of the **qurb**).
3. **Mahabba** (المحبة, "love") — the **state** of **love** of **God**. The **mahabba** is the **state** of the **practitioner** who **loves** **God** with the **whole** **heart**. The **mahabba** is **especially** associated with the **Mawlawiyya** (the **Mawlawi** tariqa is the **tariqa** of the **mahabba**, of the **'ishq** of Rumi).
4. **Yaqin** (اليقين, "certainty") — the **state** of **certainty** in **God**. The **yaqin** is the **state** of the **practitioner** who **knows** **God** with **certainty**, who **does** **not** **doubt** the **existence** of **God**, who **does** **not** **doubt** the **decree** of **God**, who **does** **not** **doubt** the **mercy** of **God**. The **yaqin** is **especially** associated with the **Khalwatiyya** (the **Khalwati** tariqa is the **tariqa** of the **yaqin**).

The **four** **ahwal** are the **classical** **sequence**. The **practitioner** **receives** the **ahwal** at the **maqam**. The **practitioner** **does** **not** **control** the **ahwal**; the **practitioner** **receives** the **ahwal** through the **grace** of **God**.

## The Fana' and the Baqa'

Two **additional** **states** are the **fana'** and the **baqa'**. The **fana'** (الفناء, "annihilation") is the **state** of the **annihilation** of the **ego** in the **divine**. The **fana'** is the **state** of the **practitioner** who **loses** the **sense** of the **self** in the **presence** of **God**. The **fana'** is the **goal** of the **Sufi** path (the **fana'** is the **technical** **term** for the **unitive** **state**).

The **baqa'** (البقاء, "subsistence", "abiding") is the **state** of the **subsistence** of the **practitioner** in **God** **after** the **fana'**. The **baqa'** is the **state** of the **practitioner** who **returns** to the **world** **after** the **fana'** and **lives** in the **world** **as** a **manifestation** of **God**. The **baqa'** is the **final** **state** of the **practitioner**; the **baqa'** is the **goal** of the **Sufi** path **after** the **fana'**.

The **distinction** between the **fana'** and the **baqa'** is **fundamental** to the **Sufi** tradition. The **distinction** is **formulated** by **al-Qushayri** (the **fana'** is the **state** of the **annihilation**; the **baqa'** is the **state** of the **subsistence**), by **al-Hakim al-Tirmidhi** (the **fana'** is the **state** of the **fana'** of the **ego**; the **baqa'** is the **state** of the **fana'** of the **fana'**), by **Ibn Arabi** (the **fana'** is the **state** of the **fana'** of the **ego**; the **baqa'** is the **state** of the **fana'** of the **ego** in the **divine**). The **distinction** is **not** the **same** as the **distinction** between the **fana'** of the **ego** and the **fana'** of the **divine** (the **distinction** is **deeper** than the **ego/divine** distinction — the **distinction** is **between** the **fana'** as **state** and the **baqa'** as **state**).

## The Tasawwuf and the Tariqa

The **tasawwuf** (Arabic تصوف, "Sufism") is the **Sufi** tradition as a **whole**. The **tariqa** is the **Sufi** tradition as a **chain**. The **tasawwuf** is the **theory**; the **tariqa** is the **practice**. The **tasawwuf** is the **formulation** of the **maqamat** and the **ahwal**; the **tariqa** is the **transmission** of the **maqamat** and the **ahwal**.

The **tasawwuf** is **not** the **same** as the **tariqa**. A **mutasawwif** (a **Sufi** at **large**) is **not** a **member** of a **tariqa**. A **Sufi** in the **technical** **sense** is a **member** of a **tariqa**. The **technical** **sense** is the **Sufi** of the **chain** (the **Sufi** of the **transmission**). The **non-technical** **sense** is the **Sufi** of the **practice** (the **Sufi** of the **tasawwuf**).

The **tasawwuf** is **preserved** in the **books** (the **Qushayri**, the **Sarraj**, the **Kalabadhi**, the **Makki**, the **Sulami**, the **Hujwiri**, the **Qalandariyya**, the **Ibn Arabi**, the **Jili**, the **Rumi**, the **Hakim Sanai**, the **'Attar**, the **Jami**). The **tariqa** is **preserved** in the **chains** (the **Qadiri**, the **Naqshbandi**, the **Shadhili**, the **Mawlawi**, the **Chishti**, the **Tijani**, the **Rifai**, the **Khalwati**). The **books** are the **theory**; the **chains** are the **practice**. The **theory** **informs** the **practice**; the **practice** **informs** the **theory**.

## The Maqamat in the Tariqa

The **maqamat** are **transmitted** in the **tariqa**. The **tariqa** **transmits** the **specific** **sequence** of the **maqamat**. The **Qadiriyya** **transmits** the **seven** **maqamat** of the **classical** **sequence** (the **Qadiri** tariqa is the **most** **conservative** of the **tariqas** in the **transmission** of the **maqamat**). The **Naqshbandiyya** **transmits** the **specific** **Naqshbandi** **sequence** (the **Naqshbandi** tariqa is **known** for the **emphasis** on the **silent** **dhikr** at the **maqam** of the **tawhid**). The **Shadhiliyya** **transmits** the **specific** **Shadhili** **sequence** (the **Shadhili** tariqa is **known** for the **emphasis** on the **sabr** at the **maqam** of the **faqr**). The **Mawlawiyya** **transmits** the **specific** **Mawlawi** **sequence** (the **Mawlawi** tariqa is **known** for the **emphasis** on the **mahabba** at the **maqam** of the **zuhd**). The **Chishtiyya** **transmits** the **specific** **Chishti** **sequence** (the **Chishti** tariqa is **known** for the **emphasis** on the **muraqaba** at the **maqam** of the **sabr**).

The **tariqa** is the **transmission** of the **maqamat**. The **tariqa** is **not** the **same** as the **maqamat**. The **tariqa** is the **chain**; the **maqamat** are the **stations** of the **chain**. The **practitioner** **progresses** through the **maqamat** in the **chain**. The **practitioner** **attains** the **maqam** through the **chain**. The **practitioner** **maintains** the **maqam** through the **chain**.

## The Practice Today

The **modern** **practice** of the **maqamat** is **preserved** in the **tariqas**. The **practitioner** **works** the **maqamat** in the **chain** of the **tariqa**. The **practitioner** **receives** the **ahwal** at the **maqam** through the **chain** of the **tariqa**. The **practitioner** **attains** the **fana'** and the **baqa'** through the **chain** of the **tariqa**.

The **modern** **Sufi** **scholars** of the **maqamat** are **few** (the **modern** **Sufi** **scholarship** is **decline** in the **20th** **century**, the **modern** **reformist** Islam has **rejected** the **Sufi** **scholarship**). The **modern** **Sufi** **practitioners** are **many** (the **modern** **Sufi** **tariqas** are **active** in **the** **modern** **world**, the **modern** **Sufi** **practitioners** are **tens** of **millions**).

The **modern** **practitioner** of the **maqamat** should:

1. **Find** a **shaykh** of a **specific** tariqa
2. **Take** the **baya** at the **specific** form of the **specific** tariqa
3. **Work** the **maqamat** at the **specific** form of the **specific** tariqa
4. **Receive** the **ahwal** at the **maqam** of the **specific** tariqa
5. **Attain** the **fana'** and the **baqa'** through the **chain** of the **specific** tariqa

The **maqamat** are **not** **self-help** **techniques**. The **maqamat** are **stations** of the **chain**. The **chain** is the **transmission**. The **transmission** is the **practice**. The **practice** is the **lifelong** **work**.
