# Quantum Biology — Coherent Vibration in Living Systems

## The Claim

**Quantum biology** is the study of **non-trivial quantum phenomena** — coherence, superposition, tunneling, and entanglement — in **living organisms**. For most of the 20th century the consensus held that warm, wet, noisy biology **destroys** quantum effects before they can matter. The discovery, in **2007**, that photosynthetic complexes maintain **quantum coherence at room temperature** overturned that assumption and revealed the **cell as a vibratory, resonant system** — an experimental echo of the esoteric claim that *life is ordered vibration* (see [[01-Cymatics-And-Vibration]]).

## Photosynthesis — The Coherent Exciton

In **2007**, Gregory Engel and colleagues at the University of Chicago fired **femtosecond** (10^-15 s) laser pulses at the **Fenna-Matthews-Olson (FMO) complex** of green sulfur bacteria and observed **quantum beats** — oscillations proving that the excitation energy explores **multiple pathways simultaneously** (a superposition) before settling on the most efficient route to the reaction center.

The mechanism:

1. A photon excites a **chlorophyll** molecule; the energy becomes an **exciton** (a delocalized quasiparticle)
2. The exciton **coherently samples** all paths through the antenna complex as a wave, not a particle
3. **Vibrational modes** of the protein scaffold "tune" the pathways — a phenomenon called **vibronic** coupling
4. The energy arrives at the **reaction center** with near-unity efficiency (~95%), far above any classical random walk

Crucially, the coherence is **environment-assisted**: the same thermal noise that classical theory said would kill it actually **protects and directs** the transport (the "ENAQT" model of Mohseni et al., 2008). The organism **uses** its warm, wet noise as a resonance chamber.

## Magnetoreception — The Quantum Compass

Many migratory birds (European robin, *Erithacus rubecula*) navigate by the **Earth's magnetic field** using a **quantum sensor** in the eye:

1. Blue light hits a **cryptochrome** flavoprotein, creating a **spin-correlated radical pair** (two unpaired electrons)
2. The **singlet-triplet interconversion** of the pair is **modulated by the magnetic field** — a genuinely quantum, spin-entangled state
3. The bird's visual system reads the **spatial pattern** of cryptochrome activation as a **magnetic map**

This is **entanglement in a living retina** (Schulten's hypothesis, 1978; experimentally supported by Mouritsen & Hore, 2012). The bird *sees* the magnetic field as a faint overlay on its vision — a literal **resonant coupling** between organism and planet.

## Other Quantum-Living Phenomena

- **Enzyme catalysis** — **proton and electron tunneling** short-circuits the classical activation barrier, accelerating reactions 10^3-10^6× (the H-transfer in alcohol dehydrogenase)
- **DNA mutation** — **proton tunneling** at the G-C and A-T bonds permits spontaneous, coherent base flips
- **Olfaction** — the **Turin hypothesis** (1996, 2001) proposes that receptors detect odor by the **vibrational frequency** of the molecule via inelastic electron tunneling — smell as **spectroscopy**, not shape (see [[03-Harmonics-Resonance-Fourier]])
- **Avian navigation timing** — cryptochrome is also gated by the **circadian clock**, linking the quantum sensor to the organism's timekeeping

## The Caveat

Quantum biology is **young and contested**. Not every claimed effect survives replication:

- The **original 77 K** FMO coherence (Engel 2007) was later shown to be **more robust at higher temperature** (Collini 2010), but the *functional* role of coherence remains debated
- The ** olfactory vibration** hypothesis lacks decisive proof; the **shape** theory still competes
- Many "quantum" effects may be **classical correlations** misread as coherence

The honest claim is therefore: **some** living systems exploit **some** quantum coherence, **some** of the time, in **warm, wet** conditions. That alone dissolves the old dogma that life is purely classical.
