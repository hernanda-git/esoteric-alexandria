# Christian Kabbalah — Pico, Reuchlin, Agrippa

## The Move

**Christian Kabbalah** is the **integration of the Kabbalistic tradition into Christian theology**. It is the major intellectual achievement of the Italian Renaissance, and the foundation of the Western esoteric tradition.

The basic move: the Christian theologian reads the **Kabbalah** (the Jewish esoteric tradition) and finds in it **proof of Christian truth**. Pico della Mirandola was the first to do this systematically (1486). The argument:

- The Kabbalah is the **oldest** of all esoteric traditions (claimed to go back to Moses at Sinai)
- The Kabbalah is the **purest** (because it never went through the Islamic mediation)
- The Kabbalah, when read correctly, **proves** the Christian doctrines: the Trinity, the Incarnation, the divinity of Christ, the sacraments
- Therefore, the **prisca theologia** (the original theology) was the Kabbalah, and the Christian tradition is its fullest expression

The move was **brilliant** and **controversial**. The Christian Kabbalah gave the West a sophisticated esoteric vocabulary that the Latin tradition had lacked. The Christian Kabbalists (Pico, Reuchlin, Agrippa, the Florentine Academy under Ficino) were the **most serious intellectuals** of the Renaissance.

## The Three Founders

### Giovanni Pico della Mirandola (1463-1494)

The **first Christian Kabbalist**. His 1486 *900 Theses* (dedicated to Pope Innocent VIII) included 47 theses drawn from the Kabbalah. The Kabbalah, Pico argued, was the **original theology** of humanity, given to Moses at Sinai, transmitted through the Essenes and the Hellenistic Jewish philosophers (Philo of Alexandria), and recovered by the Renaissance from the Jewish sources.

Pico's *Oration on the Dignity of Man* (1496) is the **founding text of the Renaissance humanism**. The central claim: man is the **chained god**, the **knot of the world**, the **great miracle** that can become what he chooses. The Kabbalistic doctrine of *tikkun* (repair, restoration) is the philosophical core.

Pico died young (31) in a politically complicated situation (he had been imprisoned by Pope Innocent VIII and only released through the intervention of Lorenzo de' Medici). His work was continued by his nephew Gianfrancesco Pico, who published the *Opera Omnia* in 1496.

### Johannes Reuchlin (1455-1522)

The **first scholar to read the Kabbalah in Hebrew**. Reuchlin's *De Arte Cabbalistica* (1517) is the first Christian book on the Kabbalah written by someone who actually read the Jewish sources. Reuchlin learned Hebrew from the Jewish scholar **Obadiah Sforno** of Rome.

The work is structured as a **dialogue** between three characters: a Christian (Philolaus), a Jewish scholar (Baruchias), and a polymath (Capnion). The Jewish scholar **wins** the dialogue on most points — the Kabbalah is presented as the **superior** tradition, the **most ancient**, the **most pure**.

Reuchlin was involved in a famous **anti-Jewish** controversy — the *Judenbuch* (1510) — in which he defended the Jewish books (including the Talmud) against a proposed confiscation and burning. His position was: the Jewish books contain **truths** that Christians need to know. The controversy is the seed of the **Reformation**, with Reuchlin's defense of free inquiry aligning with the Lutheran position.

### Heinrich Cornelius Agrippa (1486-1535)

The **systematizer**. Agrippa's *Three Books of Occult Philosophy* (1533) is the **summa** of the Christian Kabbalistic tradition. The work integrates the Kabbalah, the Hermetica, the Neoplatonic tradition, the astrological tradition, the grimoire tradition, into a single structured system.

Agrippa's central move is the **chain of being** (see [[12-Agrippa-Picatrix-Grimoires/01-Three-Books-Agrippa]]). The chain goes from God (Kether in the Kabbalistic map) through the angelic choirs, through the planetary spheres, through the elements, to the material world. The chain is the **operating system** of the Western esoteric tradition.

The *Three Books* was placed on the **Index of Forbidden Books** in 1559. It was read anyway. It is still in print.

## The Reception

The Christian Kabbalah was the **founding move** of the Western esoteric tradition. The major later developments:

- **The Florentine Academy** (Marsilio Ficino, Pico's circle) — the Hermetic-Cabalistic synthesis
- **The Rosicrucian manifestos** (1614-1616) — the Christian Kabbalistic tradition gone public
- **The Golden Dawn** (1888-1903) — the most systematic modern revival
- **The OTO and Thelema** (Crowley, 1904-) — the Thelemic reformulation
- **The modern chaos magic** (Spare, Carroll, 1970s-) — the lightest, most pragmatic reformulation

The chain is **clear** and **direct**. The Christian Kabbalah is the **node** in the chain; without it, the modern Western esoteric tradition would not exist.

## The Modern Note

Christian Kabbalah is **alive** in three communities:

1. **The traditional Christian esoteric orders** — the **Societas Rosicruciana in Anglia** (SRIA, founded 1866), the **Societas Rosicruciana in America** (SRIA, 1878), the **Ordo Rosae Rubeae et Aureae Crucis** (the Golden Dawn), the **A∴A∴** (Crowley's reformed Golden Dawn), the **Builders of the Adytum** (BOTA, founded 1922) — all use the Christian Kabbalistic framework
2. **The academic study** — the modern scholarship of **Frances Yates**, **Antoine Faivre**, **Wouter Hanegraaff**, **Allison Coudert** has recovered the Christian Kabbalistic tradition as a serious object of study
3. **The popular spirituality movement** — the **Kabbalah Centre** (founded 1922, modernized 1965-) teaches a simplified version of the Christian Kabbalistic framework to a wide audience, including Madonna and other celebrities

The library's category 08 treats Christian Kabbalah as the **bridge** between the Jewish Kabbalistic tradition and the modern Western esoteric tradition. Without the Christian Kabbalists, the West would not have a serious esoteric vocabulary.

## The Practice

A modern Christian Kabbalistic practice requires:

1. **Learn the Tree of Life** — the 10 sephirot, the 22 paths, the 3 pillars (see [[01-Tree-Of-Life]])
2. **Learn the gematria** — the number-letter system (see [[02-Gematria-Mapping]])
3. **Learn the divine names** — the 4-letter name, the 72-fold name, the names of the sephirot
4. **Learn the source texts** — Pico, Reuchlin, Agrippa, the *Zohar*, the *Sefer Yetzirah*
5. **Find a tradition** — the operative Christian Kabbalah is found in the modern esoteric orders
6. **Find a teacher** — the tradition is oral, the technical details are best learned in person
