# The Tree of Life — 10 Sephirot, 22 Paths

## The Diagram

The **Tree of Life** (עץ החיים, *Etz Chaim*) is the master diagram of the Kabbalistic tradition. It depicts the **10 sephirot** (singular *sephirah*) and the **22 paths** that connect them.

The 10 sephirot are the **10 divine attributes** through which the Infinite (*Ein Sof*) manifests in the created world. They are arranged in 3 vertical columns (the **three pillars**):

| Pillar | Position | Character |
|---|---|---|
| **Pillar of Severity** (left) | The left side | The form, the strict, the judgment (Boaz in Solomon's Temple) |
| **Pillar of Mercy** (right) | The right side | The expansion, the gentle, the loving (Jachin) |
| **Pillar of Balance** (middle) | The center | The equilibrium, the synthesis, the path |

The 10 sephirot, from top to bottom:

| # | Sephirah | Meaning | Pillar | Color | Body | World | Divine Name |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Kether** | Crown | Middle | White brilliance | Crown of the head | Atziluth (Emanation) | Eheieh (אהיה) |
| 2 | **Chokhmah** | Wisdom | Right (mercy) | Grey | Right hemisphere of the brain | Atziluth | Yah (יה) |
| 3 | **Binah** | Understanding | Left (severity) | Black | Left hemisphere of the brain | Atziluth | YHVH Elohim (יהוה אלהים) |
| 4 | **Chesed** | Mercy | Right (mercy) | Blue | Left arm | Beriah (Creation) | El (אל) |
| 5 | **Geburah** | Severity | Left (severity) | Red | Right arm | Beriah | Elohim Gibbor (אלהים גיבור) |
| 6 | **Tiphareth** | Beauty | Middle | Yellow | Heart / Chest | Yetzirah (Formation) | YHVH Eloah Va-Daath (יהוה אלוה ודעת) |
| 7 | **Netzach** | Victory | Right (mercy) | Green | Left leg / kidney | Yetzirah | YHVH Tzabaoth (יהוה צבאות) |
| 8 | **Hod** | Splendor | Left (severity) | Orange | Right leg / kidney | Yetzirah | Elohim Tzabaoth (אלהים צבאות) |
| 9 | **Yesod** | Foundation | Middle | Violet | Reproductive organs | Yetzirah | Shaddai El Chai (שדי אל חי) |
| 10 | **Malkuth** | Kingdom | Middle | Earth colors (4) | Feet | Assiah (Action) | Adonai Ha-Aretz (אדני הארץ) |

The 22 paths (in the classical system) are the **connections** between the sephirot — 22 in number, because they correspond to the 22 letters of the Hebrew alphabet. The most famous of the 22 paths is the **Lightning Flash** (the path from Kether to Malkuth in the order 1→2→3→6→7→9→10).

## The Four Worlds

The Tree of Life maps to the **Four Worlds** of the Kabbalistic cosmology:

| World | Hebrew | Meaning | Sephirotic Association | What lives here |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Atziluth** (אצילות) | Emanation | The world of pure divine light | Kether, Chokhmah, Binah | The divine names, the highest angels |
| **Beriah** (בריאה) | Creation | The world of the throne | Chesed, Geburah | The cherubim, the thrones, the divine ideas |
| **Yetzirah** (יצירה) | Formation | The world of the angelic | Tiphareth, Netzach, Hod | The angels, the souls, the archetypal forms |
| **Assiah** (עשיה) | Action | The world of the material | Malkuth | The physical world, the embodied spirits |

The **descent** from Atziluth to Assiah is the **process of creation** itself — the Infinite becomes more and more concrete as it descends. The **ascent** from Assiah to Atziluth is the path of the mystic — the practitioner returns to the source by ascending through the worlds.

The four worlds are **simultaneously real**. The Kabbalist does not say "the material world is an illusion". The Kabbalist says "the material world is **the densest expression** of the same reality that is the most rarefied in Atziluth". The physical body is **as real as** the highest seraph — both are modes of the divine.

## The Ein Sof

The **Ein Sof** (אין סוף, "Without End", "Infinite") is the **source** of the 10 sephirot. The Ein Sof is not a sephirah itself; it is the **pre-sephirotic** state, the undifferentiated divine, the **infinite that has not yet emerged** into the 10 attributes.

The Ein Sof is **unknowable** in itself. The Kabbalists were unanimous that we cannot know the Ein Sof; we can only know the sephirot through which the Ein Sof has chosen to be known. The 10 sephirot are the **10 modes of divine self-presentation**, the 10 ways God is knowable.

The **Christian Kabbalistic** tradition (Pico, Reuchlin, Agrippa) used the diagram extensively. See [[03-Christian-Kabbalah-Pico-Reuchlin-Agrippa]].

## The Correspondence to Other Systems

The Tree of Life maps to many other systems:

| System | Mapping |
|---|---|
| **9 choirs of angels** (Pseudo-Dionysius) | Each sephirah has a corresponding choir (see [[06-Angels-Jinn-Demons/01-Angelic-Hierarchy]]) |
| **9 planets** (Ptolemy / al-Buni) | The planetary spheres correspond to the lower sephirot |
| **4 elements** | Fire = Netzach, Water = Hod, Air = Tiphareth, Earth = Malkuth (in the Western esoteric reformulation) |
| **4 humors** | Sanguine = Tiphareth, Choleric = Geburah, Melancholic = Binah, Phlegmatic = Hod |
| **7 chakras** (Hindu/Tantric) | Kether = Sahasrara, Chokhmah = Ajna, Tiphareth = Anahata, Yesod = Svadhisthana, Malkuth = Muladhara (in the Golden Dawn synthesis) |
| **4 stages of the alchemical work** | Nigredo = Malkuth, Albedo = Yesod, Citrinitas = Tiphareth, Rubedo = Kether (in the Jungian reading) |
| **3 stages of the mystical path** | Purgation = Geburah/Hod, Illumination = Chesed/Netzach, Union = Tiphareth/Kether (in the Christian contemplative tradition) |

The Tree of Life is the **master cross-tradition concordance diagram**. Every major tradition has a system that can be mapped onto it, and the mappings are remarkably consistent.

## The Practical Note

The Tree of Life is **not just a diagram**. In the operative Kabbalah, the practitioner:

1. **Visualizes** the Tree of Life in the appropriate color
2. **Invokes** the appropriate sephirah at the appropriate time
3. **Combines** sephirot in ritual formulas (e.g., "Kether-Chokhmah-Binah" — the supernal triad)
4. **Creates** talismans and seals for the specific sephirah (see the **kamea** tradition in [[02-Hurufiyya-Lettrism/04-Wafq-Magic-Squares]])
5. **Meditates** on the sephirah for a specific purpose (e.g., meditating on Tiphareth for healing, on Netzach for victory, on Hod for clarity)

The Tree of Life is also the **framework for dream interpretation** in the modern Kabbalistic tradition. The sephirot are the **10 levels of meaning** that any dream can have.

## The Modern Note

The Tree of Life is alive in three communities:

1. **The Orthodox Jewish tradition** — the contemporary Kabbalistic revival (centered in Bnei Brak and Brooklyn) studies the Tree intensively. The **Arizal** (Isaac Luria, 16th c.) is the most studied master.
2. **The Western esoteric tradition** — the Golden Dawn, the OTO, the modern Thelemic and ceremonial orders all use the Tree as the master diagram.
3. **The popular Jewish spirituality movement** — the **Kabbalah Centre** (founded 1922 by Rav Ashlag, now centered in LA and NY) teaches the Tree to a wider audience. Philip Berg, Karen Berg, Yehuda Berg are the modern teachers.

The library's category 08 treats the Tree of Life as the **most influential single diagram** in the Western esoteric tradition. Every other diagram (the geomancy chart, the astrological chart, the alchemical wheel) can be mapped onto it.

## The Practice

A modern Tree of Life practice requires:

1. **Learn the diagram** — the 10 sephirot, the 22 paths, the 3 pillars
2. **Learn the correspondences** — the colors, the divine names, the angelic choirs, the planetary attributions
3. **Learn the visualization** — the 10 colors, the 10 divine names, the 10 meditations
4. **Practice the meditation** — start with Malkuth, ascend through the Tree
5. **Find a teacher** — the operative Kabbalah requires a *Mequbbbal* (one who has received the tradition)
