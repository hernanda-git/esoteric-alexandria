# Gematria — The Number-Letter System

## The System

**Gematria** (גימטריא, from Greek *geōmetria*) is the Hebrew system of assigning numerical values to letters and computing on words. The system is **isomorphic** to the Arabic *abjad* (see [[02-Hurufiyya-Lettrism/02-Abjad-Numerals]]). Both descend from the Semitic *gematria*/*'ilm al-huruf* tradition.

The Hebrew alphabet has 22 letters (final forms bring it to 27 in the numerical computation). The values:

| Letter | Name | Value | Letter | Name | Value |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| א | Aleph | 1 | י | Yod | 10 |
| ב | Beth | 2 | כ | Kaph | 20 |
| ג | Gimel | 3 | ל | Lamed | 30 |
| ד | Daleth | 4 | מ | Mem | 40 |
| ה | He | 5 | נ | Nun | 50 |
| ו | Vav | 6 | ס | Samekh | 60 |
| ז | Zayin | 7 | ע | Ayin | 70 |
| ח | Cheth | 8 | פ | Pe | 80 |
| ט | Teth | 9 | צ | Tzadik | 90 |
| | | | ק | Quph | 100 |
| | | | ר | Resh | 200 |
| | | | ש | Shin | 300 |
| | | | ת | Tav | 400 |

Final forms (used when a letter is at the end of a word): ך (Kaph) = 500, ם (Mem) = 600, ן (Nun) = 700, ף (Pe) = 800, ץ (Tzadik) = 900. These give the values 500-900, just as in the Arabic *abjad* (see [[02-Hurufiyya-Lettrism/02-Abjad-Numerals]]).

## The Three Modes

The classical Kabbalistic tradition gives three modes of gematria:

### 1. *Mispar hechrechi* (מספר החרכי) — "ordinal value"

The simple ordinal: Aleph = 1, Beth = 2, ..., Tav = 22. The final forms are ignored. This mode is used for **sequence** and **progression** — what comes first, what comes second, what comes last.

### 2. *Mispar gadol* (מספר גדול) — "great number"

The standard integral gematria. Aleph = 1, Beth = 2, ..., Tav = 400, with the final forms for 500-900. This is the most commonly used mode.

### 3. *Mispar katan* (מספר קטן) — "small number"

The reduced gematria: each letter is its digital root (1-9), with 10 = 1, 20 = 2, etc. This mode is used for **hidden patterns** — what the word "really is" when reduced to its essence.

The three modes are **not interchangeable**. The Kabbalist chooses the mode based on the question.

## The Famous Gematrias

Some classical gematria results that are central to the tradition:

| Word | Value (Mispar Gadol) | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| **YHWH** (the 4-letter name) | 26 | The number of YHWH; also the gematria of "He shall be" (*yehi*) |
| **Elohim** (God, plural form) | 86 | The number of "the divine nature" |
| **Torah** (the Law) | 611 | The number of "instruction" |
| **Chokhmah** (wisdom) | 73 | The number of "the divine attribute" |
| **Chesed** (mercy) | 72 | The number of the Shem ha-Mephorash (see [[06-Angels-Jinn-Demons/02-Goetic-Spirits-72-Shemhamphorash]]) |
| **Malkuth** (kingdom) | 496 | The number of "the kingdom", also the **third perfect number** |
| **Amen** (so be it) | 99 | The number of the 99 names of God (in the Islamic parallel) |
| **Israel** | 541 | The number of "the one who wrestles with God" |
| **Metatron** (the archangel) | 314 | The number of **Pi** × 100 — the archangel of the circle |
| **Satan** | 364 | The number 364 = 7 × 52 — Satan has 52 weeks of power minus 1 (the Day of Atonement) |
| **Yeshua** (Jesus) | 329 | The number of "the savior" |
| **Sephirah** | 380 | The number of "the number" |

The most famous is **YHWH = 26**. The same calculation: י (10) + ה (5) + ו (6) + ה (5) = 26. The Tetragrammaton is the **26th** of the name of God.

## The Famous Gematria Pairs

The Kabbalists found that **many concepts that are theologically paired** have the **same gematria**. The famous pairs:

| Pair | Common gematria | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **YHWH** and **Elohim** | 26 + 5 + 30 + 5 = 26 + 40 = 66 each (with *vav ha-shem*, the "vav of the name" added to YHWH gives 26 + 6 = 32, but the classical is 66) | "Mercy and judgment" — the two faces of the divine |
| **Chesed** and **Geburah** | 72 and 216 (72 × 3) | Mercy and severity are **3x** of each other |
| **Ab** (father) and **Ben** (son) | 3 and 52 | The father-son relationship in divine emanation |
| **Chokhmah** and **Binah** | 73 and 67 (sum = 140 = "Kether") | Wisdom and Understanding together = Crown |
| **Metatron** and **Satan** | 314 and 364 (sum = 678 = "Torah" × ~) | The archangel and the accuser are in dynamic balance |

The pairs are **not coincidence** in the Kabbalistic reading. The gematria **reveals** the hidden connection. Two words with the same gematria are **the same word in the divine language**; the difference is only the human language.

## The Use in Practice

Gematria is used in the operative Kabbalah for:

1. **Generating the names of spirits** — the practitioner can compute the gematria of a desired operation and find the **divine name** that matches (e.g., to heal, find the name with gematria 415 = "refu'ah")
2. **Generating the seals of the spirits** — the seal of a spirit is often the visual representation of its name's gematria in the planetary square
3. **Predicting the time of an event** — the gematria of the event's name can be matched to a date
4. **Confirming the truth of a statement** — a true statement will have a gematria that "agrees" with the gematria of the context

The most famous use of gematria for confirmation: the **Baal Shem Tov** (founder of Hasidism, 18th c.) was famous for finding hidden meanings in the gematria of Torah verses. The Hasidic tradition has continued this practice.

## The Notarikon

**Notarikon** (נוטריקון) is the related system of using letters as **abbreviations** for words. The two forms:

- **Initial notarikon** — the first letters of words in a phrase spell a new word. E.g., **Aleinu** ("It is our duty") = "the first letters" of the Hebrew words spell "El", "God". The system is widely used in the Kabbalistic commentary.
- **Final notarikon** — the last letters of words in a phrase spell a new word. Less common but used in the *Zohar*.

## The Temurah

**Temurah** (תמורה, "exchange") is the system of letter substitution:

- **Albam** — the alphabet split in half, each letter substituted with the one 11 positions later (A↔L, B↔M, ...)
- **Atbash** — the alphabet reversed (A↔T, B↔Sh, ...)
- **Avgad** — each letter "advanced" by one (A↔B, B↔G, ...)

The *temurah* is used in the **cipher** tradition (the Kabbalists are the medieval cipher masters of the Jewish tradition) and in the **decoding** of hidden names.

## The Modern Note

Gematria is **alive** in three communities:

1. **The Orthodox Jewish tradition** — the *bat kol* (echo of the divine voice) tradition uses gematria actively
2. **The Western esoteric tradition** — the Golden Dawn used gematria extensively; the modern Thelemic tradition continues
3. **The popular Jewish spirituality movement** — the Kabbalah Centre teaches gematria to laypeople

The library's category 08 treats gematria as the **arithmetic** of the Tree of Life. The diagram, the names, the meditation, and the gematria are the four pillars of the Kabbalistic tradition.

## The Practice

A modern gematria practice requires:

1. **Learn the alphabet** — the 22 letters, the final forms, the values
2. **Learn the modes** — *mispar hechrechi*, *mispar gadol*, *mispar katan*
3. **Learn the famous gematrias** — the values of YHWH, Elohim, the sephirot, the archangels
4. **Practice the computation** — compute the gematria of biblical verses, of names, of phrases
5. **Find a teacher** — the operative gematria requires a *Mequbbbal*
