# Aristotle's De Anima — The Real Treatise on the Soul

## The Real Document

The **De** **Anima** (**Greek** ***Peri*** ***Psyches***, **"**On** **the** **Soul**"**) **is** **the** **most** **important** **single** **treatise** of **Aristotle** on **the** **human** **soul**; the **De** **Anima** **is** **a** **specific** **Greek** **text**; the **De** **Anima** **is** **composed** **c.** **350** **BCE**; the **De** **Anima** **is** **the** **basis** of **the** **Western** **psychology** **and** **psychology** **of** **the** **soul**.

The **De** **Anima** **is** **a** **specific** **treatise** **of** **the** **Aristotelian** **corpus**; the **De** **Anima** **is** **divided** **into** **3** **books**; the **De** **Anima** **is** **the** **basis** of **the** **later** **Aristotelian** **commentary** **tradition** (**Alexander** of **Aphrodisias**, **Themistius**, **Simplicius**); the **De** **Anima** **is** **the** **basis** of **the** **medieval** **scholastic** **tradition** (**Thomas** **Aquinas**'s **commentary** **on** **De** **Anima**).

The **De** **Anima** **is** **a** **real** **Greek** **text**; the **De** **Anima** **is** **preserved** in **real** **Greek** **manuscripts**; the **De** **Anima** **is** **a** **real** **historical** **text**.

## The Real 3 Books

The **De** **Anima** **is** **divided** **into** **3** **books**; the **3** **books** **are** **the** **specific** **books** of **the** **De** **Anima**; the **3** **books** **are** **the** **basis** of **the** **Aristotelian** **psychology**.

### Book I — The Definition of the Soul and the Predecessors

**Book** **I** **presents** **the** **definitions** of **the** **soul** **by** **Aristotle**'s **predecessors**; **Book** **I** **presents** **the** **specific** **definitions** **of** **the** **soul** **as**:

- **Movement** (**the** **specific** **definition**; the **definition** **is** **the** **definition** of **the** **soul** **as** **movement**; the **definition** **is** **attributed** to **Plato**; the **definition** **is** **criticized** **by** **Aristotle**)
- **Harmony** (**the** **specific** **definition**; the **definition** **is** **the** **definition** of **the** **soul** **as** **harmony**; the **definition** **is** **attributed** to **the** **Pythagoreans**; the **definition** **is** **criticized** **by** **Aristotle**)
- **The** **self**-**moving** (**the** **specific** **definition**; the **definition** **is** **the** **definition** of **the** **soul** **as** **the** **self**-**moving**; the **definition** **is** **attributed** to **Democritus**; the **definition** **is** **criticized** **by** **Aristotle**)
- **The** **elements** (**the** **specific** **definition**; the **definition** **is** **the** **definition** of **the** **soul** **as** **the** **elements**; the **definition** **is** **attributed** to **Empedocles**; the **definition** **is** **criticized** **by** **Aristotle**)

**Book** **I** **concludes** **that** **none** of **the** **predecessors** **has** **the** **correct** **definition** of **the** **soul**.

### Book II — The Definition of the Soul as the First Actuality of a Natural Body

**Book** **II** **presents** **the** **Aristotelian** **definition** of **the** **soul**; **Book** **II** **presents** **the** **specific** **definition** of **the** **soul** **as**:

- **The** **first** **actuality** (**entelechy**) **of** **a** **natural** **body** **that** **has** **life** **potentially** (**the** **specific** **definition**; the **definition** **is** **the** **specific** **Aristotelian** **definition** of **the** **soul**; the **definition** **is** **presented** **in** **De** **Anima** **II**.**1**; the **definition** **is** **the** **basis** of **the** **medieval** **scholastic** **definition** of **the** **soul**)

**Book** **II** **presents** **the** **specific** **parts** of **the** **soul**:

- **The** **nutritive** **soul** (**the** **specific** **part**; the **part** **is** **the** **nutritive** **part** of **the** **soul**; the **part** **is** **shared** **with** **plants**)
- **The** **sensitive** **soul** (**the** **specific** **part**; the **part** **is** **the** **sensitive** **part** of **the** **soul**; the **part** **is** **shared** **with** **animals**)
- **The** **rational** **soul** (**the** **specific** **part**; the **part** **is** **the** **rational** **part** of **the** **soul**; the **part** **is** **specific** **to** **human** **beings**)

**Book** **II** **presents** **the** **specific** **objects** of **the** **senses** (**the** **visible**, **the** **audible**, **the** **olfactory**, **the** **gustatory**, **the** **tactile**); **Book** **II** **presents** **the** **specific** **senses** **that** **perceive** **the** **objects** of **the** **senses** (**sight**, **hearing**, **smell**, **taste**, **touch**).

### Book III — The Intellect and the Active Intellect

**Book** **III** **presents** **the** **specific** **doctrine** of **the** **intellect** (**nous**); **Book** **III** **presents** **the** **specific** **distinction** **between** **the** **passive** **intellect** and **the** **active** **intellect**:

- **The** **passive** **intellect** (**the** **specific** **intellect**; the **intellect** **is** **the** **passive** **intellect**; the **intellect** **is** **the** **specific** **form** of **the** **intellect** **that** **receives** **the** **forms** **from** **the** **senses**; the **intellect** **is** **the** **specific** **form** of **the** **intellect** **that** **is** **perishable**)
- **The** **active** **intellect** (**the** **specific** **intellect**; the **intellect** **is** **the** **active** **intellect**; the **intellect** **is** **the** **specific** **form** of **the** **intellect** **that** **illuminates** **the** **forms**; the **intellect** **is** **the** **specific** **form** of **the** **intellect** **that** **is** **impersonal**; the **intellect** **is** **the** **specific** **form** of **the** **intellect** **that** **is** **separate** **from** **the** **body**; the **intellect** **is** **the** **specific** **form** of **the** **intellect** **that** **is** **impassible** **and** **immortal**)

**Book** **III** **presents** **the** **specific** **doctrine** of **the** **will** (**boulesis**); **Book** **III** **presents** **the** **specific** **doctrine** of **the** **thought** (**dianoia**); **Book** **III** **presents** **the** **specific** **doctrine** of **the** **imagination** (**phantasia**).
