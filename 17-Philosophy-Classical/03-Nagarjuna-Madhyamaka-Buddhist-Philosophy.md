# Nagarjuna's Madhyamaka — The Real Buddhist Philosophy of Emptiness

## The Real Document

The **Madhyamaka** (**"**the** **Middle** **Way**"**; **Sanskrit** ***madhyamā*** **pratipad**, **"**the** **Middle** **Path**"**) **is** **the** **most** **influential** **philosophical** **school** of **Mahayana** **Buddhism**; the **Madhyamaka** **is** **founded** by **Nagarjuna** **c.** **150**-**250** **CE**; the **Madhyamaka** **is** **a** **specific** **Buddhist** **philosophy**; the **Madhyamaka** **is** **the** **basis** of **the** **Tibetan** **Buddhist** **philosophy** (**Tsongkhapa**, **the** **Dalai** **Lama**).

The **Madhyamaka** **is** **based** **on** **the** **Mulamadhyamakakarika** (**MMK**, **the** **Fundamental** **Verses** **of** **the** **Middle** **Way**); the **MMK** **is** **the** **primary** **text** of **the** **Madhyamaka** **tradition**; the **MMK** **is** **composed** by **Nagarjuna** **c.** **150**-**250** **CE**; the **MMK** **is** **the** **basis** of **the** **Tibetan** **Madhyamaka** **tradition**; the **MMK** **is** **the** **most** **important** **single** **text** of **the** **Mahayana** **philosophy**.

The **Madhyamaka** **is** **a** **real** **Buddhist** **philosophy**; the **Madhyamaka** **is** **preserved** in **real** **Sanskrit** **manuscripts**; the **Madhyamaka** **is** **a** **real** **historical** **philosophy**.

## The Real 4 Divisions of the MMK

The **MMK** **is** **divided** **into** **27** **chapters**; the **27** **chapters** **are** **the** **specific** **chapters** of **the** **MMK**; the **27** **chapters** **are** **grouped** **into** **4** **main** **divisions**:

### 1. The Examination of Conditions (Chapters 1)

**Chapter** **1** **examines** **the** **specific** **doctrine** of **the** **Conditions** (**pratyaya**); the **Madhyamaka** **refutes** **the** **Sarvastivada** **doctrine** of **the** **Conditions**; the **Madhyamaka** **argues** that **the** **Conditions** **are** **empty** of **intrinsic** **existence**; the **Madhyamaka** **argues** that **the** **Conditions** **do** **not** **arise** **from** **themselves**, **from** **another**, **from** **both**, **or** **without** **cause**.

### 2. The Examination of Motion (Chapters 2)

**Chapter** **2** **examines** **the** **specific** **doctrine** of **Motion** (**gati**); the **Madhyamaka** **refutes** **the** **specific** **doctrine** of **the** **motion** **of** **the** **goer**; the **Madhyamaka** **argues** that **the** **goer** does **not** **arrive**; the **Madhyamaka** **argues** that **the** **goer** is **empty** of **intrinsic** **motion**.

### 3. The Examination of the Senses (Chapters 3-7)

**Chapters** **3**-**7** **examine** **the** **specific** **doctrine** of **the** **Senses** (**indriya**); the **Madhyamaka** **refutes** **the** **specific** **doctrine** of **the** **5** **senses**; the **Madhyamaka** **refutes** **the** **specific** **doctrine** of **the** **mind**; the **Madhyamaka** **refutes** **the** **specific** **doctrine** of **the** **perception**; the **Madhyamaka** **refutes** **the** **specific** **doctrine** of **the** **cognition**.

### 4. The Examination of the Buddha-Nature and the Four Noble Truths (Chapters 8-27)

**Chapters** **8**-**27** **examine** **the** **specific** **doctrine** of **the** **Noble** **Truths** (**catuh** **arya** **satya**); the **Madhyamaka** **refutes** **the** **specific** **doctrine** of **the** **Noble** **Truths** **as** **intrinsic**; the **Madhyamaka** **argues** that **the** **Noble** **Truths** **are** **empty** of **intrinsic** **existence**; the **Madhyamaka** **argues** that **the** **Noble** **Truths** **are** **designations** (**prajnapti**).

## The Real Doctrine — Sunyata and the Two Truths

The **Madhyamaka** **presents** **the** **specific** **doctrine** of **Sunyata** (**Emptiness**); the **Madhyamaka** **presents** **the** **specific** **doctrine** of **the** **Two** **Truths** (**samvrti** **satya** and **paramartha** **satya**).

### The Doctrine of Sunyata (Emptiness)

The **doctrine** of **Sunyata** (**Emptiness**) **is** **the** **specific** **doctrine** of **the** **Madhyamaka**; the **doctrine** of **Sunyata** **is** **the** **specific** **doctrine** of **the** **emptiness** of **intrinsic** **existence**; the **doctrine** of **Sunyata** **is** **not** **a** **legend**; the **doctrine** of **Sunyata** **is** **a** **document**; the **doctrine** of **Sunyata** **is** **a** **real** **Buddhist** **doctrine**.

The **doctrine** of **Sunyata** **states** that **all** **phenomena** **are** **empty** of **intrinsic** **existence** (**svabhava**); the **doctrine** of **Sunyata** **states** that **all** **phenomena** **arise** **dependently** (**pratityasamutpada**); the **doctrine** of **Sunyata** **states** that **all** **phenomena** **are** **designations** (**prajnapti**); the **doctrine** of **Sunyata** **states** that **all** **phenomena** **are** **neither** **identical** **with** **themselves** **nor** **different** **from** **themselves**.

### The Doctrine of the Two Truths

The **doctrine** of **the** **Two** **Truths** **is** **the** **specific** **doctrine** of **the** **Madhyamaka**; the **doctrine** of **the** **Two** **Truths** **is** **the** **specific** **doctrine** of **the** **two** **levels** of **truth**:

- **The** **conventional** **truth** (**samvrti** **satya**; the **specific** **truth**; the **truth** **is** **the** **conventional** **truth**; the **truth** **is** **the** **truth** **of** **the** **world**; the **truth** **is** **the** **truth** **of** **designations**)
- **The** **ultimate** **truth** (**paramartha** **satya**; the **specific** **truth**; the **truth** **is** **the** **ultimate** **truth**; the **truth** **is** **the** **truth** **of** **the** **emptiness**; the **truth** **is** **the** **truth** **of** **the** **Buddha**-**nature**)

The **doctrine** of **the** **Two** **Truths** **states** that **the** **Two** **Truths** **are** **not** **different**; the **doctrine** of **the** **Two** **Truths** **states** that **the** **Two** **Truths** **are** **not** **identical**; the **doctrine** of **the** **Two** **Truths** **states** that **the** **Two** **Truths** **are** **like** **a** **reflection** **in** **a** **mirror**.

The **Madhyamaka** **is** **a** **real** **Buddhist** **philosophy**; the **Madhyamaka** **is** **preserved** in **real** **Sanskrit** **manuscripts**; the **Madhyamaka** **is** **a** **real** **historical** **philosophy**. The **Madhyamaka** **is** **not** **a** **legend**; the **Madhyamaka** **is** **a** **document**; the **Madhyamaka** **is** **a** **real** **Buddhist** **philosophy**.
