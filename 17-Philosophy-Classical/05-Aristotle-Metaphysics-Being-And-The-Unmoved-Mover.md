# Aristotle's Metaphysics — Being and the Unmoved Mover

## The Real Work

The **Metaphysics** (**ta meta ta physika**, literally "the [books] after the physics") is **not** a single authored treatise; it is a **posthumous editorial compilation** of **14 books** (Alpha through Nu) assembled by the later Peripatetic school, traditionally attributed to **Andronicus of Rhodes** (c. 60 BCE) who **first arranged** the Aristotelian corpus. The title is a **library accident** — the works placed "after the *Physics*" — not Aristotle's own name for the project. The project is **first philosophy** (**prote philosophia**), the science of **being qua being** (**to on he on**).

The **Metaphysics** is a **real** historical text; the **Metaphysics** is preserved in **real** Greek manuscripts; the **Metaphysics** is **not** a legend. The **Metaphysics** is the **foundation** of the **Western** concept of **substance** and the **Western** concept of **theology** as a branch of philosophy.

## The Central Question — Being Qua Being

The **science** Aristotle seeks is the science of **being as being** — not this or that kind of being (**a particular science** studies a **part** of being, as the **physicist** studies **mobile** being), but **being itself** and the **attributes** that belong to it **as such**. The **highest** of these attributes is **unity** (**hen**): what is, is **one**; what is **one** in the relevant sense, is.

The **question** that drives the work is: **what is *ousia*?** — usually translated **substance**, but more precisely the **what-it-is** (**to ti en einai**, the "what it was to be"), the **primary** kind of being on which all other categories (quality, quantity, relation, place, time) **depend**. The **investigation** moves through **candidates** — the **substrate** (the *hyle*, matter), the **essence** (the *eidos*, form), the **universal** — and **settles** on the **composite** of **form and matter** in **sensible substances** (a **this-such**, *tode ti*), with **form** the **primary** sense of substance.

## The Four Causes

The **Metaphysics** (Book Alpha and the *Physics*) presents the doctrine of the **four aitiai** (the "causes," better: the **explanatory factors**) that make a thing **intelligible**:

1. **Material** (**hyle**) — that out of which (*ex hou*)
2. **Formal** (**eidos**) — the pattern, the what-it-is (*to ti en einai*)
3. **Efficient** (**kineton**) — the source of change (*hothen he arche tes kineseos*)
4. **Final** (**telos**) — the for-the-sake-of-which (*hou heneka*)

The **four causes** are the **framework** the esoteric tradition inherits: the **golem** needs **matter**, **form**, a **maker**, and a **purpose**; the **alchemical opus** is read as a **teleological** process. The **final cause** is the **most important** for Aristotle — nature does nothing in vain — and it is the **bridge** to the **unmoved mover**.

## The Unmoved Mover (Book Lambda)

Book **Lambda** (XII) presents the **theological** climax: the **unmoved mover** (**ho proton kinoun akineton**), the **first principle** of **motion**. The argument is **strict**:

- **Motion** is **eternal** (the *Physics* proves the **world** has **no beginning** of motion)
- An **eternal** motion requires an **eternal** mover that is **itself unmoved**
- This mover is **pure actuality** (**energeia**) with **no potentiality** (*oudemia dunamis*) — for potentiality is a **principle of change**, and the mover **does not change**
- It is **thought thinking itself** (**noesis noeseos**) — the **act of understanding** understanding **understanding**; its **life** is the **pleasure** of that thinking
- It **moves** the world **as the beloved moves the lover** — by **being desired**, not by **pushing**

The **unmoved mover** is **not** a **creator** (Aristotle denies **creation from nothing**); it is the **eternal** **telos** that **draws** the cosmos. This is the **structural ancestor** of the **Neoplatonic One** (see [[11-NeoPlatonism-Theurgy/02-Plotinus-Enneads-One-Mind-Soul]]) and, through emanation, of the **cosmological descent** (see [[17-Philosophy-Cosmology/01-Emanation-And-Descent]]) — but Aristotle's mover is **immanent** to the **everlasting** cosmos, **not** a **transcendent** source that **overflows**.

## The Real Note

The **Metaphysics** is the **hinge** between the **classical** cosmos and the **esoteric** one. Where **Plato** (see [[02-Plato-Timaeus-Real-Cosmology]]) places the **forms** **beyond** the world, Aristotle places **form** **inside** the **sensible** thing — and yet still posits a **single** **unchanging** source that **attracts** all motion. The **doctrine** is **not** the same as the **Eastern** claim of **emptiness** (see [[03-Nagarjuna-Madhyamaka-Buddhist-Philosophy]]): Nagarjuna **deconstructs** the **substance** Aristotle **establishes**. The **value** for the student is **methodological**: Aristotle gives the **vocabulary** of **substance, cause, act, and potency** that the **entire** later esoteric and **scholastic** tradition **speaks**.
