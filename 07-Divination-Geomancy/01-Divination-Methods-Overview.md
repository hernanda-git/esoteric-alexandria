# Divination — The Forty Methods

## The Mantic Catalogue

The world's cultures have independently developed **at least 40 distinct methods** of divination (sign-reading for the purpose of receiving guidance, prediction, or hidden knowledge). The number 40 is approximate — new methods are still being added (the "Motherpeace Tarot" deck was introduced in 1981; the "Osho Zen Tarot" in 1994).

The methods fall into **5 families** based on the medium:

| Family | Medium | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Cleromancy** | Random selection from a set of marked objects | Sortilege (lots), bibliomancy, rhapsody (Verse Divination), the Chinese *Shijing* |
| **Oneiromancy** | Dreams | Dream interpretation, the *Oneirocritica* of Artemidorus (2nd c. CE) |
| **Scapulimancy / Osteomancy** | Bones, shoulder-blades, ivory | Chinese *jiagu* divination, the I Ching's yarrow stalk, African bone-throwing |
| **Aeromancy** | Wind, smoke, clouds, birds | Greek ornithomancy (augury), Roman auspicium, the *tabaq* of the Bedouin |
| **Hydromancy** | Water | Crystal-gazing, lecanomancy (water bowl), the Mexican *amantecas* |

The **Geomancy** (sand divination) family is a special case that deserves its own doc — see [[02-Geomancy-Earth-Speaks]].

## The Two Theories of Divination

The world's cultures divide into two theories of how divination works:

### 1. The Omniscient Theory (monotheistic traditions)

- **Source**: God / the One / the Absolute
- **Mechanism**: God knows the future; the diviner is a **vessel** through which God's knowledge flows
- **Examples**: Hebrew prophecy, Islamic *irfan*, Christian prophecy, the I Ching in the Taoist frame
- **Method**: the diviner must be **morally and ritually clean** to receive the message
- **Limitation**: the message can be **wrong** if the diviner is unclean, if the time is wrong, or if God chooses to conceal

### 2. The Stochastic Theory (Eastern traditions)

- **Source**: the cosmos itself, the natural order
- **Mechanism**: the universe is a **web of causes and conditions**; at any moment, a small random event is **sympathetic** with the larger pattern. The diviner **reads** the random event as a sample of the larger pattern
- **Examples**: the I Ching, the modern Chaos Theory of divination, the *'ilm al-huruf* in its rationalist interpretation, modern pendulum dowsing
- **Method**: the diviner must be **attentive** to the random event, not morally or ritually clean (though the disciplined mind is a help)
- **Limitation**: the message is **probabilistic**, not certain

The two theories are not exclusive. The **I Ching** has been read in both ways (the Confucian / rationalist tradition and the Taoist / mystical tradition). The **Bible** has been read in both ways (the Hebrew prophets in the omniscient mode, the Greek *Sortes* in the stochastic mode).

## The Greek and Roman Traditions

The Greek and Roman traditions had a **fully developed** system of state divination:

- **Manteia** (μαντεία) — the technical term for divination, the "office" of the mantis
- **Mantis** (μάντις) — the diviner, the "one who is in the ecstatic state"
- **Oraculum** — the place where the oracles are given (Delphi, Didyma, Claros)
- **Augury** — divination by the flight of birds (the Roman state religion)
- **Haruspex** — divination by the entrails of sacrificed animals (Etruscan origin)
- **Sortilege** — divination by drawing lots (used in the Homeric tradition)
- **Kleros** — the "lot" or "ticket" (the root of our word "clergy" — the clergy are the "allotted" of God)
- **Orphic divination** — divination in the Orphic mysteries, the use of the dodecahedron

The Roman **augurs** were state officials; the **College of Augurs** was one of the most powerful religious bodies in the Republic. The *auspicium* (the taking of auspices before any important state action) was required for the action to be valid.

## The Arabic-Islamic Tradition

The Islamic tradition has a complex relationship with divination. The Quran explicitly condemns **some** practices (the *kuhhan* — the pre-Islamic diviners) but accepts **others** (the dream of the Prophet, the *'ilm al-huruf* in its esoteric reading). The medieval scholars distinguished:

- **Kahanah** (كهانة) — the pre-Islamic divination by the jinn (forbidden)
- **'Irfan** (عرفان) — the mystic insight into the unseen (permitted)
- **Firasah** (فراسة) — the physiognomic / astrological reading (permitted)
- **Jafr** (جفر) — the letter-arithmetic of sacred text (debated, see [[02-Hurufiyya-Lettrism/03-Jafr-Ilm-of-Letters]])
- **Ramal** (رمل) — geomancy, sand divination (debated, but widespread)
- **Istimara** — the "asking" of a child or a simple person, who is the innocent vessel of the divine message (permitted, with conditions)

The **'ilm al-huruf** tradition is the most **systematic** of the Islamic divination methods. See [[02-Hurufiyya-Lettrism/01-Ilm-al-Huruf-Overview]].

## The Chinese Tradition

The Chinese tradition is the **largest** and the **oldest** continuous divination tradition. The major methods:

- **Jiagu** (甲骨) — divination by cracks on heated turtle shells and ox shoulder-blades (Shang dynasty, ~1500-1000 BCE)
- **I Ching** (易經) — the **Book of Changes**, the 64 hexagrams, consulted by yarrow stalk or coin
- **Tui bei tu** (推背图) — the prophetic "Pushing the Back Chart" of Li Chunfeng and Yuan Tiangang (7th c. CE)
- **Zi wei dou shu** (紫微斗数) — Purple Star Astrology, the most complex of the Chinese systems
- **Qi men dun jia** (奇门遁甲) — the "Mysterious Gate, Concealing Jia" — a strategic and divination system
- **Da li ren** (大六壬) — the "Great Six Ren" — another complex system

The I Ching is the most influential outside East Asia; see [[03-I-Ching-64-Hexagrams]].

## The Practice

A modern practice of divination is **legitimate** if:

1. **The method is known and traditional** — do not invent a method
2. **The diviner is trained** — read the source texts, study with a teacher
3. **The purpose is correct** — divination is for **guidance**, not entertainment; the diviner is the **vessel**, not the source
4. **The reading is recorded** — the diviner should keep a log of every reading, the method, the result, the verification
5. **The verification is honest** — track which readings came true, which did not, why

## The Modern Note

Divination is **alive** in the modern world:

1. **The I Ching** is the most widely consulted divination system in the modern West. The Wilhelm/Baynes translation (1950) is the standard; the modern computer and app versions are popular.
2. **The Tarot** is the most popular Western system. The 78-card deck was codified in the 14th century (probably in northern Italy) and is the most-reproduced divination tool in the modern era. The Golden Dawn, the OTO, the modern chaos magicians have all developed sophisticated Tarot systems.
3. **Astrology** is the most popular system globally. The Western, Vedic, and Chinese systems are all alive.
4. **The Sorts and Bibliomancy** are simple but persistent — opening a book at random for guidance.
5. **The modern "intuitive" methods** — automatic writing, channeling, "tuning in" — are variations on the classical tradition of the diviner as the vessel.

The library's category 07 is a **catalogue**, not a manual. The reader is reminded: the most important thing in any divination is **the diviner's state of consciousness**, not the method. A simple bibliomancy done by a master is more accurate than an I Ching consultation done by a distracted mind.
