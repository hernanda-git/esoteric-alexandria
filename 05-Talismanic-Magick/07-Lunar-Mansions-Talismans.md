# The Mansions of the Moon — Talismans of the 28 Lunar Stations

## The Principle

Where planetary talismans bind the **seven** wandering stars, the **mansions of the Moon** bind the **fixed** backdrop against which the Moon moves. The lunar month is divided into **28 stations** (Arabic *manazil al-qamar*, "houses of the Moon"), each the arc the Moon traverses in roughly one day. Each mansion is a **place** on the ecliptic with its own **image**, **nature**, and **operation** — a finer, monthly grid layered over the weekly planetary grid.

The Moon, "the fastest of the planets," was the most-worked celestial body in the Arabic image-tradition precisely because it changes sign every ~2.3 days and transits a mansion every ~24 hours, giving the practitioner a near-daily election window.

## The 28 Stations — Structure

The *manazil* are counted from the Moon's node, beginning at **al-Sharatān** (β and γ Arietis, ~0° Aries). They do not align with the 12 zodiacal signs; they are an older, lunar-pastoral calendar overlaid on the solar one. The three great families:

- **The 7 benefic mansions** — used for growth, marriage, travel, building (e.g., **al-Dabaran** / Aldebaran, the 4th, for "vengeance and journeys")
- **The 7 malefic mansions** — used for binding, separation, destruction (e.g., **al-Haq''a** / the 6th, "the place of weeping")
- **The 14 mixed mansions** — context-dependent operations

Each mansion carries three working attributes in the source texts:

| Attribute | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Image** | The graven figure to inscribe (man, beast, star-pattern) |
| **Election** | The Moon must be in that mansion, often void of malefic aspect |
| **Operation** | What the talisman accomplishes (love, war, healing, revelation) |

## The Picatrix Mansion Images

The **Picatrix** (Book III) gives the most complete surviving list: 28 images, each with a prescribed material, timing, and a specific outcome. For example, the **3rd mansion (al-Thurayya**, the Pleiades) is worked for "the increase of harvests and the binding of enemies"; the **15th (al-Ghafr**) for "love and the reconciliation of estrangers." The operative logic is identical to the planetary talisman — **correct image + correct time + correct consecration = live talisman** — only the celestial clock is the Moon's daily transit rather than the planetary hour.

## The Consecration Difference

Because the window is ~24 hours, lunar mansion talismans are **time-critical but forgiving** compared to planetary elections (which may wait weeks for the right day/hour/aspect). The practitioner:

1. **Elects** — waits for the Moon to enter the target mansion
2. **Inscribes** — draws the mansion image on its prescribed material (waxing for increase, waning for decrease)
3. **Suffumigates** — passes it through the mansion's incense
4. **Seals** — wraps and deploys for the stated operation

The **direction of the Moon** (waxing vs. waning) is the decisive modifier: the same mansion image works oppositely for increase vs. decrease.

## The Western Reception

The *manazil* entered the Latin West through the **Picatrix** translation (c. 1256, for Alfonso X) and were simplified into the **Heptameron** and the grimoire tradition. The 28 stations survive in the modern **Golden Dawn** and **Thelemic** lunar workings as the "28 Mansions of the Moon," used for talismanic and pathworking work.

## The Modern Note

The mansions are a **precision timing system** — a 28-step calendar that any practitioner can verify against the Moon's position. They are the finest-grained election grid in the operative tradition, and they remain the standard bridge between **Arabic astral magic** and **Western talismanic practice**.
