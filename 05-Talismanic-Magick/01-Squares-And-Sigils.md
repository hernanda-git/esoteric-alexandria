# Squares, Sigils, and Pentacles

## The Three Talismanic Forms

The talismanic tradition uses three main geometric forms:

| Form | Tradition | Construction | Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Square** (*wafq*, *kamea*) | Islamic / Jewish | Numbers arranged in a square, every row/column/diagonal sums to the same constant | Capture a planetary force, encode a name |
| **Seal / Sigil** | Western / Islamic | A figure derived from letters of a name, often on a planetary square | Invoke a specific spirit |
| **Pentacle** (*pantacle*, *pantakel*) | Western Christian Kabbalah | A disc containing a square, sigil, divine name, and figure | Conjuration, protection, the "lesser" pentacle is drawn in the air with the sword |

The three are **not exclusive**. Most working talismans contain all three — a square (numbers), a sigil (the spirit), and a divine name (the authorization).

## The Square (Wafq / Kamea)

The *wafq* is the most mathematically rigorous of the three. See [[02-Hurufiyya-Lettrism/04-Wafq-Magic-Squares]] for the full treatment. The key fact: the wafq is the **same object** in the Islamic, Jewish, and Western traditions — the 3x3 Saturn square is the 3x3 Saturn wafq is the 3x3 Saturn kamea. The convergence is the cleanest case of cross-tradition concordance in the library.

## The Sigil

A sigil is a **figure derived from a name**. The most common method is the "magic square" method:

1. Write the spirit's name in a planetary square (e.g., the 7x7 Venus square for a Venusian spirit)
2. Trace a path through the letters in a specific pattern (e.g., a cross, a spiral, a planetary glyph)
3. The resulting path is the sigil
4. The sigil is **not** a depiction of the spirit; it is an **address** for the spirit in the planetary coordinate system

The sigil is a **compressed name**. In the ancient view, the name of a thing is the thing. A sigil is the name compressed into a figure that the eye cannot read but the spirit can.

## The Pentacle

The pentacle is the Western ceremonial form. The structure:

1. **Outer circle** — the containing boundary
2. **Inner disc** — the working surface
3. **Square** — the planetary square in the center
4. **Sigil** — the spirit's sigil
5. **Divine name** — the divine authorization (e.g., YHWH, Adonai, Elohim)
6. **Figure** — sometimes a depiction of the spirit or its planet

The pentacle is drawn in the air with the **ritual sword** during conjuration, and is also inscribed on a physical disc (typically wax, metal, or paper) for the consecration.

The Agrippan pentacles from *Three Books of Occult Philosophy* Book II, chapter 22, are the standard Western repertoire. There are 7 planetary pentacles (Saturn through Moon) plus numerous sub-pentacles for specific operations.

## The Construction Protocol

Every talisman follows a **construction protocol** that has been standardized across traditions. The 7 steps:

1. **Identify the goal** — what do you want the talisman to do?
2. **Select the planet** — which planet governs your goal?
3. **Choose the materials** — metal, gem, color, incense for that planet
4. **Compute the timing** — which day, which hour, which mansion
5. **Construct the figure** — the square, sigil, pentacle
6. **Inscribe and consecrate** — write the divine name, breathe upon it, suffumigate
7. **Activate and use** — carry, wear, or place the talisman; the force is now available

The protocol is **the same in al-Buni, the Picatrix, Agrippa, and the Solomonic grimoires**. The protocol is the **operating system** of talismanic magic.

## The Austin Osman Spare Reformulation

The English artist **Austin Osman Spare** (1886-1956) reformulated the sigil for the modern era. His method:

1. Write a **statement of intent** in clear language
2. **Remove the repeated letters** to get a unique set
3. **Combine the letters** into a single glyph (a sigil)
4. **Charge the sigil** by staring at it in a state of gnosis (sexual arousal, exhaustion, drugs, or meditative absorption)
5. **Forget the sigil** — the working is done once the sigil is charged and the conscious mind has released it
6. The desire, no longer held in mind, **manifests** when conditions are right

Spare's method removes most of the ceremonial apparatus (no planetary timing, no metals, no suffumigation) and focuses on the **psychological mechanism** — the **sigil as compressed desire** that the unconscious continues to work on after the conscious has let go.

Spare's method is the most **modern** sigil technique and the easiest to test. The chaos magic tradition (Peter Carroll, *Liber Null*, 1978) extends Spare's method and adds a **paradigm-shifting** element — the magician can choose the belief system to work within for any given operation.

## The Modern Note

The talismanic tradition is alive in several forms:

1. **The grimoire revival** — modern editions of the classical grimoires (Picatrix, Goetia, Sworn Book) are widely available, and serious practitioners still use them.
2. **The chaos magic tradition** — Spare, Carroll, the IOT (Institute of Operative Theurgy) — most active online, less ceremonial.
3. **The magical orders** — the Golden Dawn offshoots (A∴A∴, BOTA, Whare Ra), the OTO, the Temple of the Silver Star — most are **esoteric churches** as much as magical orders.
4. **The psychological tradition** — Jung's work on the talisman as a symbol of the Self; the talisman read as a personal mandala.

The library's category 05 treats all four as legitimate continuations of the tradition.

## The Practice

A modern talismanic practice can be light or heavy:

- **Light** — Spare's sigil method. No ceremonial, no material, no planetary timing. Pure psychological technique.
- **Medium** — Chaos magic. Some ceremonial, the magician chooses the paradigm.
- **Heavy** — Classical grimoire. Full ceremonial, planetary timing, material apparatus, a circle, a sword, a robe.

The library takes no position on which is "correct". Each has a tradition, a lineage, and a community of practitioners.

The reader is reminded: the most important thing in any talismanic work is **the practitioner's state of consciousness**. The talisman is the **focus**, not the cause. A perfectly inscribed Saturn pentacle inscribed by a distracted practitioner is **less effective** than a crooked sigil drawn by a master of gnosis.
