# Planetary Talismans — The Seven Operations

## The Principle

A **planetary talisman** is a physical object inscribed with a planetary sigil, square, divine name, and figure, and consecrated at the correct astrological time. The talisman is the **operating mechanism** for capturing a planetary force and making it available to the practitioner.

The seven classical planets have **seven** corresponding talismans, one for each planet. Each planet has a specific purpose, specific materials, specific timing, and specific operations.

## The Timing Protocol

The **planetary hour** is the time unit. The day is divided into 24 planetary hours, 12 of day and 12 of night. The first hour after sunrise is ruled by the planet of the day (Sunday = Sun, Monday = Moon, etc.). The 24 hours then cycle through the seven planets in the Chaldean order.

For a planetary talisman to be properly consecrated, the **deposition** (the inscription) must be done:

1. In the **day** of the planet
2. In the **first hour** of the day (or any hour ruled by the planet)
3. With the **Moon** in a favorable sign for the operation
4. With the planet **strong** in the chart (e.g., the Sun's talisman should be made when the Sun is in Aries, its exaltation)
5. Not during a **combustion** (the planet is too close to the Sun)
6. Not during a **retrograde** of the planet (in the modern reckoning)

The timing protocol is the **operating protocol** of the talisman. A talisman made on the wrong day, with the wrong materials, at the wrong hour is **operationally broken**. The tradition is explicit that the timing matters as much as the inscription.

## The Consecration Protocol

After the talisman is inscribed, it is **consecrated**:

1. **The suffumigation** — pass the talisman through the smoke of the planetary incense
2. **The breathing** — the practitioner breathes on the talisman three times while reciting the divine name
3. **The prayer** — the practitioner reads a specific prayer or invocation (varies by tradition)
4. **The sealing** — the talisman is wrapped in silk of the planetary color
5. **The activation** — the talisman is now **live**

The consecration is the moment when the planetary force is "captured" by the talisman. Before the consecration, the object is a piece of metal with a sigil on it. After the consecration, it is a **talisman**.

## The Modern Note

The classical planetary talismans are still being made by modern practitioners. The **Hermetic Order of the Golden Dawn** (1888-1903) preserved and extended the tradition; the modern **Whare Ra** offshoot and the **BOTA** (Builders of the Adytum) are direct descendants. The Picatrix, the Shams al-Ma'arif, and the Agrippan tradition are still in print in modern critical editions.

The chaos magic tradition has reformulated the planetary talisman as a **focal device** for a chosen paradigm. The principle is the same — a physical object that captures and concentrates a specific force — but the **mechanism** is described in psychological rather than metaphysical terms.

## The Practice

A modern practitioner of the planetary talismanic tradition should:

1. **Study the source texts** — the Picatrix, the Shams al-Ma'arif, Agrippa Book II chapter 22
2. **Learn the planetary correspondences** — the days, hours, metals, gems, colors
3. **Learn the timing** — basic astrology, the planetary hours, the elections
4. **Learn the construction** — sigil construction, square construction, pentacle construction
5. **Practice the consecration** — the suffumigation, the breathing, the prayer
6. **Find a teacher** — the tradition is oral, and the technical details are best learned in person
