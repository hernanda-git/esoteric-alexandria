# Gnosticism — The Knowing Ones

## The Name

**Gnosticism** (from Greek *gnosis*, "knowledge") is the umbrella term for a diverse set of religious movements in the 1st-4th century CE that emphasized **special knowledge** (gnosis) as the path to salvation. The modern term was coined by **Henry More** (1660) and systematized by **Gilles Quispel** (1940s-1980s).

The Gnostics called themselves **"the knowing ones"** (*hoi gnostikoi*) or by specific group names (Valentinians, Basilideans, Sethians, Mandaeans, Manichaeans). The term "Gnosticism" is the **polemical** name used by their opponents — especially the Christian heresiologists Irenaeus, Hippolytus, and Epiphanius.

## The Source — The Nag Hammadi Codices

The **modern knowledge of Gnosticism** was revolutionized in 1945 when an Egyptian farmer named **Muhammad Ali al-Samman** discovered a sealed jar at **Nag Hammadi**, Egypt, containing **12 papyrus codices** (the *Nag Hammadi Codices*). The codices contained **52 treatises**, most of which were previously unknown, in Coptic translation of Greek originals.

The discovery was **monumental** for the history of religion. It gave the modern scholar the **primary sources** of the Gnostic tradition directly, without the filter of the heresiologists' polemics. The codices were published in English in 1977 (Robinson, ed., *The Nag Hammadi Library*).

The most famous treatises:

- **The Gospel of Thomas** (NHC II,2) — 114 sayings of Jesus, no narrative, parallel to the synoptic gospels but earlier
- **The Gospel of Philip** (NHC II,3) — a Valentinian treatise on sacraments and initiation
- **The Gospel of Truth** (NHC I,3) — a Valentinian meditation on the meaning of the name "Christ"
- **The Apocryphon of John** (NHC II,1; III,1; IV,1; BG 8502) — the most important Gnostic creation myth
- **The Hypostasis of the Archons** (NHC II,4) — the "Reality of the Rulers" — on the Archons who rule the world
- **On the Origin of the World** (NHC II,5; XIII,2) — a cosmological treatise
- **The Tripartite Tractate** (NHC I,5) — a Valentinian cosmology
- **The Thunder, Perfect Mind** (NHC VI,2) — a poem of the divine feminine
- **The Discourse on the Eighth and the Ninth** (NHC VI,6) — a mystical initiation discourse
- **The Gospel of the Egyptians** (NHC III,2; IV,2) — a Sethian treatise
- **Pistis Sophia** (Askew Codex, separate from Nag Hammadi) — the "Faith-Wisdom", a major Gnostic text

## The Core Teaching

The Gnostic traditions differ in many details, but share a **common structure**:

### 1. The Pleroma (Fullness)

The **highest** level of reality is the **Pleroma** ("Fullness") — the **30 eons** (in the Valentinian system) or the **hierarchical emanation** (in other systems) of the divine. The Pleroma is the **Source**, the **pre-cosmic** reality, the **absolute**.

The Pleroma is generated by the **Father** (also called *Bythos*, "the Depth") together with his female aspect **Ennoia** (Thought) or **Sigē** (Silence). The first emanation is the **Mind** (Nous), the second is the **Word** (Logos), and the rest are the **eons** in pairs (male-female syzygies).

The Pleroma is the **non-created**, the **non-becoming**, the **eternal**.

### 2. The Fall of Sophia

The **last** of the 30 eons is **Sophia** (Wisdom). Sophia attempts to **know the Father** without her partner (Syzygy, the *Christos*), and **falls**. Her fall produces **Achamoth** (the "lower Sophia"), a deficient being who is **outside the Pleroma**.

Achamoth, in her longing for the Pleroma, produces the **material world** — but her production is **deficient**. The creator of the material world is the **Demiurge** (the "craftsman", identified by many Gnostics with the God of the Old Testament, the "YHWH Sabaoth" of the Jews).

### 3. The Demuirge and the Archons

The **Demiurge** is the **blind craftsman** — ignorant of the true God, the Pleroma, and Sophia's origins. He creates the material world in ignorance, and rules it through his **Archons** (rulers, the 7 planetary rulers, the 12 zodiacal rulers).

The **Archons** are the **principalities and powers** of the New Testament (Ephesians 6:12, "For our struggle is not against flesh and blood, but against the rulers, against the authorities, against the powers of this dark world, against the spiritual forces of evil in the heavenly realms"). The Gnostics interpreted the Archons as **malevolent** — they are the **prison wardens** of the human spirit.

### 4. The Spark

The human is the **child of Sophia** who has fallen into the material world. The human has a **spark** (Greek *sparnasma*, Latin *semen ignotum*) of the divine — the **remnant** of the Pleroma that is **trapped** in the human soul. The body is the **prison** of the spark; the material world is the **prison** of the body.

### 5. The Gnosis

The **salvation** of the human is by **gnosis** — the **knowledge** of the spark, of the Pleroma, of the way out. The gnostic is the one who has **remembered** who they are (the *anamnesis*, the "un-forgetting") and can now **ascend** through the spheres, **bypass** the Archons, and **return** to the Pleroma.

The gnosis is **not** intellectual knowledge. The gnostic is the one who has **experienced** the Pleroma, has **seen** the spark in their own soul, and can now **act** from that knowledge. The gnostic is **already saved**; the rest of the human race is **asleep** in the prison of the material world.

### 6. The Two Cosmologies

The Gnostic cosmology is **strictly dualist** — the Pleroma is **light**, the material world is **darkness**; the spark is **good**, the body is **evil**; the Pleroma is **eternal**, the material world is **temporary**. The Gnostic rejects the **created world** as the work of a **blind demiurge**, and longs for the **escape** from the world.

This is the **fundamental difference** from mainstream Christianity. The mainstream Christian affirms the **goodness of the material world** as the creation of the true God. The Gnostic affirms the **evil of the material world** as the creation of an **ignorant demiurge**.

## The Major Teachers

| Teacher | System | Date | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Simon Magus** | The first Gnostic, identified with the Simon of Acts 8 | 1st c. CE | The "great power of God" |
| **Menander** | Simon's student, the "first resurrection" through baptism | 1st c. CE | Antioch-based |
| **Satornilus** (Saturninus) | The first Christian Gnostic, Syrian | 1st c. CE | Docetist Christology |
| **Basilides** | Alexandrian, the "365 heavens" | 2nd c. CE | Highly developed emanation system |
| **Valentinus** | The most influential, the 30-aeon Pleroma | 2nd c. CE (d. ~180) | Almost became Pope |
| **Carpocrates** | The "Jesus was a regular man" tradition | 2nd c. CE | The basis of some modern theories |
| **Ptolemy** | Valentinian, the "Letter to Flora" | 2nd c. CE | Survives in Epiphanius |
| **Marcus** | Valentinian, the "sacramental system" | 2nd c. CE | The basis of some modern theurgic ideas |
| **Mani** | The founder of Manichaeism, the synthesis of Zoroastrianism, Christianity, and Buddhism | 3rd c. CE (d. 277) | A world religion for 1000 years |
| **Sethians** | The pre-Christian tradition, the Gnostic lineage of Seth | 1st-4th c. CE | The "saved by knowledge" tradition |
| **Mandaeans** | The still-existing tradition of the Sabaeans of Iraq | 1st c. CE to present | A living Gnostic religion, ~70,000 members |
| **Cathars** | The medieval European Gnostics | 11th-13th c. CE | The "pure ones", persecuted by the Catholic Church |

## The Mandaean Survival

The **Mandaeans** (مندائيون) are a still-living Gnostic religion. They are ethnically and religiously distinct, living in southern Iraq and Iran, in diaspora communities in Australia, Europe, and the US. Their population is small (~70,000) and they are under threat in the modern Middle East.

The Mandaean religion is **essentially Gnostic** in structure:
- The Pleroma is the **World of Light**
- The material world is the **World of Darkness**
- **Yahya-Yuhana** (John the Baptist) is the central prophet
- Baptism (in flowing water, *masbuta*) is the central sacrament
- The soul ascends through the planets after death
- The savior is a Mandaean figure (sometimes called Hibel-Ziwa, "splendor of the father")

The Mandaeans are the **only** surviving Gnostic tradition that has been continuous since antiquity. They are a **living link** to the ancient Gnostic world.

## The Modern Note

Gnosticism is **alive** in three communities:

1. **The Mandaean community** (continuous since antiquity) — the only unbroken Gnostic tradition
2. **The Christian esoteric orders** — the **Ecclesia Gnostica** (the Gnostic Church, founded 1953 by Jean Bricaud, continued by Stephan Hoeller), the **Lectorium Rosicrucianum**, the **Order of the Surrogate Temple** — all draw on the Gnostic tradition
3. **The popular spirituality movement** — the **Gnostic Gospels** by Elaine Pagels (1979) is a bestseller; the **Da Vinci Code**-adjacent literature; the modern "hidden gospels" literature

The library's category 10 treats Gnosticism as the **first major heresy** of the Christian tradition, and as the **first systematic philosophy** of the radical transcendence of the divine. The Gnostic tradition is alive, diverse, and influential.

## The Practice

A modern Gnostic practice (in the Christian Gnostic churches) requires:

1. **The scripture** — the Nag Hammadi texts, the Pistis Sophia, the Gospel of Thomas
2. **The tradition** — the Mandaean tradition, the Valentinian tradition, the Sethian tradition
3. **The community** — a Gnostic church or study group
4. **The discipline** — meditation, study, the sacraments (in the traditions that have them)
5. **The teacher** — a Gnostic bishop (in the hierarchical churches) or a *didaskalos* (teacher) in the looser groups

The library's category 10 is the **textual foundation**. The practice requires the community.
