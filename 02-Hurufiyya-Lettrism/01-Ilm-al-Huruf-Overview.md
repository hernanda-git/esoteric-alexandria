# 'Ilm al-Huruf — The Science of Letters

## The Core Claim

> **Every letter is a being. Every name is a power. Every name embeds a structure that, if you decode it, reveals the order of the cosmos.**

This is the central thesis of *'ilm al-huruf* (literally: the science of letters). It is not a metaphor. Practitioners treat the 28 letters of the Arabic alphabet, the 99 names of God, and the basmala formula as **operating system primitives** — instruction codes for a metaphysical machine that runs reality.

The most influential treatise is **Shams al-Ma'arif al-Kubra** ("The Great Sun of Gnoses") by Ahmad ibn Ali al-Buni (d. 622 AH / 1225 CE), a Maghrebi Sufi and traditionist from Tahert. The work is 604 pages in standard printings, divided into four books covering:

1. Letters, names, and Divine Names
2. Magic squares (*wafq*) and talismans
3. *Simiya* (magical properties) and *himiya* (protective formulae)
4. *Jafr* (the science of the unseen) and the science of the letters of the cosmos

The book has been **banned, burned, and revered** in equal measure since the 13th century. Most classical scholars of the ulema considered it either dangerous or *bid'a* (innovation). Sufis, on the other hand, have kept the manuscript tradition alive for 800 years.

## The Three Sciences of Letters

The tradition distinguishes three related but distinct sciences:

### 1. *'Ilm al-Huruf* (Science of Letters)

The base science. Every Arabic letter has:

- A **numerical value** (*abjad* or *jummal*) — see [[02-Abjad-Numerals]]
- A **planetary ruler** — each letter is dominated by one of the seven planets
- A **zodiacal correspondence** — letters map to the 12 signs
- An **element** — fire, water, air, earth
- A **fixed fire** (*nar*) and **spiritual nature** — the inner quality of the letter

This is a **multi-dimensional key**: the same letter is a number, a planet, a sign, an element, and a quality. The combination is the "address" of the letter in the metaphysical coordinate system.

### 2. *Simiya* (Magical Sympathy / Properties)

The applied science. Once you know the letter's coordinates, you can use it to **attract or repel** the corresponding planetary / zodiacal / elemental force. This is the source of:

- *Du'a* formulae — prayers structured to specific letter-combinations
- Talismans — squares, seals, sigils
- Naming children at astrologically favorable moments

### 3. *Jafr* (Divination by Letters / The Science of the Unseen)

The most advanced (and most controversial). *Jafr* is the practice of taking an Arabic text, mapping it to letter-numbers, and computing the hidden date or hidden meaning. Two main branches:

- *Jafr asghar* (lesser jafr) — letter arithmetic on Quranic verses to extract dates and predictions
- *Jafr akbar* (greater jafr) — the doctrine of the *jafr jami'* (the comprehensive cipher), supposedly a complete predictive system given to the Imams

The Ismailis and some Shia communities have extensive *jafr* traditions. The Druze have a *jafr* practice they trace to Hamza ibn Ali (11th c.).

## The System in Practice: A Worked Example

Take the basmala: **بسم الله الرحمن الرحيم** (*bismillah al-rahman al-rahim*).

Total: 786.

Step 2: Reduce. 786 → 7+8+6 = 21 → 2+1 = 3. The basmala's hidden number is **3** (the divine trinity of wahdat, or the letter *ta'* in abjad).

Step 3: The number 3 corresponds to the letter *jim*, dominated by the planet **Mercury** in the *huruf al-falak* tradition. So a practitioner who needs Mercurial influence (learning, eloquence, business) can write the basmala on a Wednesday at sunrise, focus on the 3, and use the corresponding *wafq* (magic square of 3x3 = 9 cells).

This is the **operating logic** of the whole tradition. It is a closed-form computation once you know the keys.

## Why the Tradition Got Banned

The ulema banned *'ilm al-huruf* in multiple fatwas (13th-19th c.) for two reasons:

1. **Theological** — it seemed to give the letters a power independent of God, which smells of *shirk* (association, polytheism)
2. **Social** — practitioners could predict political events, identify criminals, locate hidden treasure, and influence outcomes. The state did not want uncontrolled prediction.

The Sufi defense (best articulated by Ibn Arabi and al-Buni himself): letters are not the *cause* — God is the cause, and letters are the **mechanism through which God has chosen to act in this world**. Knowing the mechanism does not bypass God; it submits more completely to the divine pattern.

This is a strong claim, philosophically. It is also the *exact same claim* a structural engineer makes about physics: gravity is not the engineer, the engineer is the one who learned to *use* gravity.

## How This Library Treats It

We do not endorse or reject the metaphysical claim. We treat the system as:

1. **A knowledge graph** — the correspondences are real data
2. **A computation** — the abjad arithmetic is reproducible
3. **A historical document** — 800 years of practice is meaningful data
4. **A bridge** — to the Hermetic tradition (which has the same alphabet-code structure), to the Kabbalah tradition (which has the same letter-number structure), to modern information theory (which is the modern name for the same general field)
