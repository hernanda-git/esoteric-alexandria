# Simiya — The Magical Properties of Letters

## The Core Claim

> **Letters and names are not only signs but operative powers: by knowing the spiritual hierarchy bound to each letter, the practitioner can move the elements of the sublunar world.**

*Simiya* (Arabic: السيمياء, also romanized *simiyya*) is the branch of *'ilm al-huruf* concerned with the **magical and natural properties of letters and Divine Names** — the applied discipline by which the letter-sciences act upon the physical world. It is conventionally paired with *himiya* (الهيمياء, from the same root as *kimiya*/alchemy): where *himiya* works on matter through material means, *simiya* works on matter through the **spiritual agency of letters**.

The distinction is old and stable. Al-Dimashqi (c. 1300 CE), in his *Nukhbat al-Dahr*, lists *simiya* among the legitimate "natural and mathematical" sciences, distinct from sorcery (*sihr*), which he treats as forbidden. The Ikhwan al-Safa likewise classify *simiya* as a sub-branch of the natural sciences (*'ilm al-tabia*), a technology of causation rather than a violation of it.

## Etymology and Scope

The etymology is contested. The dominant view derives *simiya* from the Arabic root **s-w-m** ("to be high, sublime, heavenly"), aligning it with the celestial origin of the letter-spirits. A minority view derives it from Greek **sēmeion** ("sign, mark"), reinforcing the semiotic reading: a letter is a sign that *does* what it signifies. The house position is agnostic; both readings converge on the same practice.

The scope of *simiya* covers:

- **Elemental command** — evoking the fire/water/air/earth nature assigned to a letter (see [[01-Ilm-al-Huruf-Overview]]) to heat, cool, still, or move a substance
- **Name-operations** — deploying the 99 Names of God or specific *ism* formulae to bind or release a natural force
- **Talismanic activation** — *simiya* is what *wafq* (magic squares, see [[05-Wafq-Magic-Squares]]) are *for*: the square is the diagram, *simiya* is the engine

## The Mechanism: Letter → Spirit → Element

In the *huruf al-falakiyya* scheme, each of the 28 Arabic letters is governed by a planet and an element, and behind the planet stands an intelligence (*ruh*) and a sovereign (*malik*). *Simiya* is the art of addressing that intelligence directly. The practitioner:

1. Identifies the target effect (e.g., to still water, to ignite, to protect from fire)
2. Selects the letter(s) whose elemental nature matches the effect
3. Recites/inscribes the letter under its planetary hour (see [[05-Planetary-Hours]]) with the correct *niyya* (intention)
4. Relies on the letter's attached spirit to carry the command into the elemental sphere

This is a **closed-form causal chain**, structurally identical to the abjad arithmetic of *'ilm al-huruf*: once the keys (letter, planet, element, hour) are known, the operation is reproducible.

## The Boundary With *Himiya* and *Sihr*

The tradition draws three lines with care:

| Discipline | Means | Status |
|---|---|---|
| *Simiya* | Spiritual agency of letters/names | Permitted (within bounds) |
| *Himiya* / *Kimiya* | Material transmutation (alchemy) | Permitted (within bounds) |
| *Sihr* | Coercion of spirits / illusion | Forbidden (*haram*) |

Al-Buni is explicit that *simiya* succeeds only because God has appointed the letter as the vehicle of the effect; the operator is a *user* of the pattern, not its author. This is the same theodical defense the Sufis mount for all letter-magic (see [[01-Ilm-al-Huruf-Overview]]).

## The Modern Note

*Simiya* is the pre-modern name for what we would now locate at the intersection of **information theory and control theory**: a sign (letter) that, by virtue of its position in a structured code, exerts causal control over a physical system. The letter is an instruction; the intelligence behind it is the controller; the element is the plant. The "magic" is the claim that the controller is non-material — a metaphysical layer we neither assert nor deny, but which the structure itself does not require to be operational. The reproducibility of the *procedure* is separable from the truth of the *ontology*.
