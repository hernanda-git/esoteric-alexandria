# Shams al-Ma'arif — The Actual Structure of the Book

## The Book

**Shams al-Ma'arif** (شمس المعارف, "The Sun of Knowledge" — also translated "The Sun of the Sciences", "Solar Knowledge") is the **most copied and most studied** Arabic magical text of the medieval and early-modern period. It is attributed to **Ahmad al-Buni** (c. 1184-1225 CE), and is the **handbook** of the *'ilm al-huruf* (the science of letters) tradition.

The book is **not a single coherent treatise** but a **compilation** of materials, drawn from al-Buni's own work, his predecessors' work, and later additions by copyists. The structure varies in different manuscripts; the **standard printed edition** (Cairo, 1315 AH / 1897 CE) is the source of most modern citations, but the printed edition contains **substantial later additions** (interpolations) that al-Buni did not write.

A **critical edition** has not been completed; the **standard scholarly study** of the book is **Pierre Lory's** 1997 article "La magie des lettres dans le Shams al-Ma'arif d'al-Buni" in the *Bulletin d'Études Orientales*. A modern study by **Amina Inloes** (2009) is a more accessible introduction.

## The Two Halves of the Book

The book is divided into **two main halves**:

1. **The Knowledge Half** (al-Nisf al-'Ilmi) — the **theoretical** part. The half is the **ma'rifah** (knowledge) that precedes the **'amal** (operation). The half covers the **letters**, the **names**, the **planets**, the **jinn**, the **abdal** (the "substitutes" of the saints)
2. **The Operation Half** (al-Nisf al-'Amali) — the **practical** part. The half is the **wird** (the daily litany) and the **'amal** (the specific operation). The half covers the **wafq** (the magic squares), the **tahlil** (the invocations), the **talismans**, the **da'wa** (the invocations)

The Knowledge Half is the **foundation** of the operation. The Operation Half is the **application**. The practitioner who reads the Operation Half without the Knowledge Half is a **sorcerer**; the practitioner who reads the Knowledge Half without the Operation Half is a **theorist**. The complete practitioner reads both halves.

## The Standard Printed Edition (Cairo, 1315 AH)

The standard printed edition is divided into **4 parts** (rather than 2 halves). The 4-part structure is the **popular** division. The 4 parts are:

### Part 1: The Foundations (al-Muqaddimat)

- **The letter mysticism** — the 28 letters of the Arabic alphabet, the 22 letters of the Hebrew alphabet, the correspondence between the two, the abjad values, the planetary correspondences
- **The divine names** — the 99 names of God, the *ism al-a'zam* (the greatest name), the hidden names, the name's relationship to the planets
- **The cosmic order** — the 7 heavens, the 7 planets, the 12 signs, the 4 elements, the *barzakh* (the isthmus between the material and the spiritual)
- **The hidden knowledge** — the *'awatif*, the *du'a'*, the litanies of the Sufi orders, the *'azama* (the invocations)

### Part 2: The Planets and the Wafq (al-Kawakib wal-Wafq)

- **The 7 planetary wafq** — the 3x3 (Saturn), 4x4 (Jupiter), 5x5 (Mars), 6x6 (Sun), 7x7 (Venus), 8x8 (Mercury), 9x9 (Moon) magic squares. The 7 wafq are the **core** of the book
- **The 28 lunar mansions** (*manazil al-qamar*) — the 28 stations of the moon, each with its corresponding letter, planet, and talisman
- **The planetary hours** — the 24 hours of the day, each ruled by one of the 7 planets
- **The talismans** — the inscribed objects, the consecrations, the invocations for each planet
- **The conjurations** — the invocations of the planetary spirits, the *'amal* for the day of the planet, the *da'wa* for the hour of the planet

### Part 3: The Names and the Jinn (al-Asma' wal-Jinn)

- **The names of God** — the 99 names, the *asmā' al-husna* (the most beautiful names), the *ism al-a'zam* (the greatest name)
- **The names of the angels** — the 4 great angels (Jibra'il, Mikā'il, Isrāfil, 'Izrā'il), the 70,000 angels, the 7 archangels
- **The names of the jinn** — the kings of the jinn, the 7 kings of the 7 planets, the 7 viziers of the 7 kings, the *ifrit* and the *shaytan*
- **The abdāl** — the 40 abdāl (the "substitutes", the hidden saints of the earth), their names, their locations, the rituals of their invocation
- **The names of the dead** — the rituals of *istikhara* (consultation), the *khawf* (the fear), the *raja'* (the hope)

### Part 4: The Operations (al-A'mal)

- **The istikhara** — the **ritual** of **consultation** of the unseen. The 7 forms of istikhara. The **prayer** of istikhara. The **divination** by the *jafr* and the *raml* (geomancy)
- **The da'wa** — the **rituals** of **invocation** of the angels, the jinn, the abdāl, the dead
- **The talismans** — the **inscribed** objects, the **inscriptions**, the **consecrations** of the talismans for the specific purposes (love, protection, healing, knowledge, power)
- **The tahara** — the **ritual purifications** before the *'amal* (the major and minor ablutions, the ghusl, the wudu', the wudu' of the dead)
- **The 'amal** — the **specific** operations, the invocations, the prayers, the litanies. The 'amal for the day, the 'amal for the night, the 'amal for the planetary hour

The 4 parts are the **standard** printed structure. The **manuscript** structures vary; some manuscripts have 5 parts, some have 6 parts. The Cairo 1315 AH edition is the **standard** for the popular tradition.

## The Core Text — The 7 Planetary Wafq

The **core** of the book is the **7 planetary wafq** (the 7 magic squares). The 7 wafq are:

| # | Wafq | Planet | Square | Magic sum | Correspondence |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 3x3 | Saturn | containing 1-9 | 15 | Saturday, lead, iron, black |
| 2 | 4x4 | Jupiter | containing 1-16 | 34 | Thursday, tin, silver, blue |
| 3 | 5x5 | Mars | containing 1-25 | 65 | Tuesday, iron, red, blood |
| 4 | 6x6 | Sun | containing 1-36 | 111 | Sunday, gold, yellow |
| 5 | 7x7 | Venus | containing 1-49 | 175 | Friday, copper, green |
| 6 | 8x8 | Mercury | containing 1-64 | 260 | Wednesday, mercury, multi-colored |
| 7 | 9x9 | Moon | containing 1-81 | 369 | Monday, silver, white |

The wafq is the **magical square** where every row, every column, and every diagonal sums to the same number (the **magic sum**). The wafq is the **locus** of the planetary force.

The 7 wafq are **not** al-Buni's invention. The 7 wafq are **known** to the earlier *'ilm al-huruf* tradition (the *Kitab al-Fusul al-Muhimma* of al-Tilimsani, c. 1200 CE; the *Jawāhir al-Kalām* of al-`Adawi, c. 1150 CE). Al-Buni's contribution is the **systematization** of the wafq and the **ritual** of the **consecration**.

The 3x3 square (Saturn) is the **simplest** and the **oldest**. The 3x3 square is known in the Greek and Indian traditions (the *Lo Shu* of the Chinese tradition, the Pythagorean *tetraktys*). The 3x3 square is the **archetype** of the magic square.

The 4x4 square (Jupiter) is the **first** of the *order-4* even-order squares. The 4x4 square is **constructed** by **B** and **D** **methods** (the **B** order is the **standard** simple arrangement; the **D** order is the **Dürer** arrangement, the most famous of which is the one in **Albrecht Dürer's** 1514 engraving *Melencolia I*).

The 5x5 square (Mars) is the **first** of the *order-5* odd-order squares. The 5x5 square is **constructed** by the **Siamese method** (the *de la Loubère* method, named after the 17th c. French diplomat who described it from the Siamese tradition).

The 6x6 (Sun), 7x7 (Venus), 8x8 (Mercury), and 9x9 (Moon) are the **successive** orders. The 7 squares are the **standard** of the *'ilm al-huruf* tradition.

## The Letter Magic — the Hawas

The **hawas** (Arabic "properties" — singular *hissa*) is the **magical property** of a **letter**. The hawās is the **specific** effect that the letter produces when the letter is **drawn**, **spoken**, **written**, or **worn**. The hawās is **derived** from the letter's:

- **Numerical value** (the abjad value — see [[02-Abjad-Numerals]])
- **Planet** (the planetary correspondence)
- **Element** (the elemental correspondence)
- **Zodiacal sign** (the zodiacal correspondence)
- **Name** (the ism, the *meaning* of the letter in the divine name)

The hawās is the **basic unit** of the *'ilm al-huruf* magick. The hawās is **systematized** in the *Shams al-Ma'arif* and the related texts.

The hawās is **tested** by the practitioner. The hawās is **not** a priori; the hawās is **empirical**. The practitioner **draws** the letter, **speaks** the letter, **writes** the letter, **wears** the letter, and **observes** the effect. The effect is **recorded**. The record is the **evidence**.

The hawās is **not** universal. The hawās is **specific** to the **practitioner**, the **place**, the **time**. The hawās that works in **Damascus** may not work in **Bandung**. The hawās that works on **Tuesday** may not work on **Friday**. The practitioner **tests** the hawās in the **specific** circumstance.

## The Names of the Jinn

The **names of the jinn** are the **invocations** of the *'amal*. The jinn are **real, intelligent, non-human beings** in the Islamic tradition (see [[06-Angels-Jinn-Demons/03-Jinn-Islamic-Spirit-Lore]]). The jinn are **bound** to the **7 planets**; the 7 kings of the jinn are the **7 planetary rulers**:

| Planet | King of the jinn | Vizier | Other Names |
|---|---|---|---|
| Saturn | Maymun | Dabir | Al-Abyad, al-Makdad |
| Jupiter | Shamhurush | Barqan | Al-Abyad, al-Muktaf |
| Mars | Abu Murra | Nuh | Al-Qaswar, al-Sayyar |
| Sun | al-Nahhas | Tabit | Al-Sarraj, al-Muharriq |
| Venus | Karkadush | Daybas | Al-Zahran, al-Mujib |
| Mercury | Si'lah | Maymun | Al-Aswad, al-Mas'ul |
| Moon | Marid | Barid | Al-Makki, al-Sayyar |

The **invocation** of the king of the jinn is the *'amal* of the planetary day. The invocation **conjures** the king, **requests** the favor, **concludes** with the dismissal. The dismissal is **critical**: the practitioner **dismisses** the jinn, **does not** dismiss them **without** dismissal (the jinn is **bound** to the dismissal).

The dismissal is the **dismissal of the *'amal***. The dismissal is the **closing** of the **ritual**. The dismissal is the **practical** end of the **practical** half of the book.

## The 40 Abdāl

The **abdāl** (Arabic "substitutes" — singular *badal*) are the **40 hidden saints** of the earth. The abdāl are the **invisible pillars** of the world. The abdāl are the **hidden guardians** of the **unseen** order. The abdāl are the **Sufi** form of the **gnostic** "holy men" of the eschatological tradition (the *men of the name* in the Jewish tradition, the *chefs invisibles* in the Christian esoteric tradition).

The abdāl are the **source** of the *baraka* (blessing) that **sustains** the world. The abdāl are the **source** of the **miracles** that the **orthodox** see as the **karama** (the wonders) of the saints. The abdāl are **unknown** to the **uninitiated**; the abdāl are **known** to the **initiated**.

The 40 abdāl are the **head** of the hierarchy. The hierarchy is:

- The **abdāl** (40) — the **first** of the hidden saints
- The **awtad** (4) — the **pillars** (4 poles of the earth)
- The **aqtāb** (3 or 1) — the **axes** (the supreme saints, the *qutb*)

The hierarchy is **invisible**. The hierarchy is **known** to the **initiated** of the **chain** (*silsila*). The hierarchy is **not** the **subject** of the **popular** Sufism. The hierarchy is the **subject** of the **esoteric** Sufism.

## The Connection to the Picatrix and Agrippa

The **Shams al-Ma'arif** is the **Arabic source** of the **Western planetary magick** tradition. The **Picatrix** (Ghāyat al-Ḥakīm) is the **earlier** Arabic text (10th-11th c. CE, see [[12-Agrippa-Picatrix-Grimoires/02-Picatrix-Ghayat-al-Hakim]]). The **Picatrix** is the **first** to **systematize** the planetary talismans.

The **Shams al-Ma'arif** and the **Picatrix** are the **two** Arabic sources of the **Western tradition**. The **Western tradition** adds:

- The **Greek** planetary spirits (Picatrix Book I)
- The **Solomonic** grimoire tradition (the *Lesser Key of Solomon*, the *Sworn Book of Honorius*)
- The **Renaissance** Christian Kabbalah (Pico, Reuchlin, Agrippa)
- The **Enlightenment** rationalized magick (the **Golden Dawn**)
- The **20th century** Thelemic magick (Crowley, the **OTO**, the **A∴A∴**)

The **Shams al-Ma'arif** is **not** the **source** of the Western tradition directly. The **Picatrix** is the **direct** source. The **Shams al-Ma'arif** is the **indirect** source through the **Iberian** transmission (the **Andalusian** translation movement of the 12th-13th c. CE, the **Toledo** translation school).

## The Practice Today

The **Shams al-Ma'arif** is **still** read and **practiced** in the **modern** Islamic world. The book is **still** printed in **Cairo**, **Beirut**, and **Karachi**. The book is **sold** in the **bookshops** of the **Sufi** neighborhoods. The book is **studied** in the **Sufi** orders.

The **modern** practice of the **Shams al-Ma'arif** is **controversial**. The **orthodox** Islam **rejects** the *'amal* (the magical operations) as **bid'a** (innovation) and **shirk** (polytheism, association with God). The **Sufi** tradition **accepts** the *'amal* as **valid** within the **framework** of the *tawhid* (the unity of God) and the *tariqa* (the Sufi path).

A modern practitioner of the **Shams al-Ma'arif** should:

1. **Read** the book in the **Arabic** (the translations are **incomplete** and **often wrong**)
2. **Find** a **Sufi** shaykh of a **chain** that **transmits** the *'amal*
3. **Test** the **hawas** in the **specific** circumstance (the hawās is **empirical**, not **a priori**)
4. **Practice** the *'amal* with the **correct** **intention** (*niyya*) and the **correct** **state** (*hal*)
5. **Conclude** every *'amal* with the **dismissal**

The **Shams al-Ma'arif** is **not** a **self-help** book. The book is a **technical** handbook. The book requires **the chain**. The chain is the **transmission**. The transmission is the **practical** knowledge.
