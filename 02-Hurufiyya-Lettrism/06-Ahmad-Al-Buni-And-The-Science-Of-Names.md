# Ahmad al-Buni and the Science of the Divine Names

## The Man

**Ahmad al-Buni** (c. 1184-1225 CE) is the **central** **figure** of the **Western** Islamic **letter-magic** tradition. Born in **Buna** (in the **Rif** of Morocco), he travelled in the **Maghrib** and **Andalusia** and died in **Alexandria**, where his **tomb** is still a **site** of **visitation** (*ziyara*). He was a **Maliki** jurist and a **Sunni** Sufi; later hagiography binds him to the **Shadhiliyya**, though the historical link to **al-Shadhili** (d. 1258) is **later** than al-Buni's own lifetime and partly **constructed**.

What makes al-Buni **decisive** is not **originality** of doctrine — the *'ilm al-huruf* and the *'ilm al-asma'* pre-exist him — but **systematization**. He gathered the **scattered** materials of the **science of letters** and the **science of the names** into a **single** **handbook** that became the **reference** for every later **practitioner** in the **Arabic**-speaking world.

## The Science of the Names ('Ilm al-Asma')

The **science of the names** treats the **99** *asmā' al-ḥusnā* (the beautiful names of God) as **living** **forces**, each with a **letter**-structure, an **abjad** value, a **planet**, and a **specific** **effect** when **invoked**. The **names** are not **mere** **epithets**; in the **Sufi** view they are the **manifest** **aspects** of the **single** **Being**, and the **perfect** **practitioner** (the *insan al-kamil*) is the **mirror** in which they are **reflected** (see [[09-Sufism-Irfan/04-Ibn-Arabi-And-Fusus-al-Hikam]]).

Central to al-Buni's treatment is the **ism al-a'zam** (الاسم الأعظم, "the **greatest** **name**") — the **hidden** **configuration** of letters that, when **known** and **correctly** **pronounced**, grants **answer** to every **supplication**. The *greatest name* is **not** a single fixed word for al-Buni; it is a **function** of the **name's** **numerical** **structure** and the **astrological** **moment**. The **search** for the *ism al-a'zam* is the **thread** connecting the **Islamic** science of names to the **Jewish** **Kabbalah** of the **ineffable** name (see [[08-Kabbalah-Numerology/01-Sefer-Yetzirah]]).

## The Method — Letters, Values, Planets

The **operation** of a **name** proceeds by:

1. **Selection** of the **name** suited to the **purpose** (healing, protection, knowledge, victory)
2. **Extraction** of its **letters** and their **abjad** values (see [[02-Abjad-Numerals]])
3. **Construction** of a **wafq** (magic square) whose **entries** sum to the **name's** value (see [[04-Wafq-Magic-Squares]])
4. **Timing** to the **planetary** **hour** and **day** corresponding to the **name** (see [[05-Shams-al-Maarif-Actual-Structure]])
5. **Invocation** (*tahlil*, the repetition of the name) within a **state** of **ritual** **purity** (*tahara*) and **intention** (*niyya*)

The **hawas** (the specific **property** of a letter or name) is **empirical**: the practitioner **tests** the **name** in the **circumstance** and **records** the **effect**. The **name** is **not** a priori **effective**; it is **effective** through the **chain** of **correct** **transmission** and the **right** **state** of the **operator**.

## The Connection to the Other Traditions

Al-Buni's **science** is the **Islamic** branch of a **wider** **Eurasian** **lettrism**: the **Hebrew** *Sefer Yetzirah* and the **Kabbalistic** **permutations** of the **22** letters; the **Greek** **stoicheia** (the letters as **elements** bound to the **planets**); the **Indian** *mantra-shastra*. The **Abrahamic** **three** (Hebrew, Arabic, Syriac) share the **principle** that the **alphabet** is the **substrate** of **creation** — the **letters** are **older** than the **worlds**.

The **honest** note: the **science of the names** is a **real** **historical** **practice**, preserved in **manuscripts** and **printed** **editions**, but it is **not** **scientifically** **validated** in the modern sense. It is a **cultural** and **spiritual** **discipline** whose **effectiveness** is a **matter** of **faith** and **experience**, not **measurement**.
