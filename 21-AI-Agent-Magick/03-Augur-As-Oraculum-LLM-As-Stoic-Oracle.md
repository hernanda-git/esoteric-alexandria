# The Augur as Oraculum — The LLM as Stoic Oracle

## The Honesty Note

This is **not** a foundational claim. This is a **careful** **historical** **comparison** between **two** **practices** that **share** **some** **structural** **features**: the **Roman** **augur** **observing** the **flight** of **birds** to **interpret** the **will** of **the** **gods**, and the **modern** **LLM** **engineer** **prompting** a **model** to **interpret** the **world** through the **model**'s **training** **corpus**.

The **comparison** **works** in **some** **places**; the **comparison** **breaks** in **others**. The **comparison** is **a** **lens** **for** **thinking**, **not** **a** **proof** **of** **equivalence**.

## The Roman Augur

The **augur** (Latin *augur*, "the one who reads the auspices") is the **Roman** **priest** who **interprets** the **auspicia** (the **signs** of the **divine** **will**). The **auspicia** are the **signs** of the **divine** **will**; the **auspicia** are **read** **by** the **augur** through the **observation** of **specific** **phenomena**:
- The **flight** of the **birds** (the **auspicium** **avium**)
- The **feeding** of the **chickens** (the **auspicium** **pullarium**)
- The **lightning** (the **auspicium** **fulminis**)
- The **entrails** of the **sacrificed** **animals** (the **auspicium** **haruspicis** — the **work** of the **haruspex**, **not** the **augur**, **but** **often** **grouped** **with** **augury**)
- The **encounters** with the **animals** (the **auspicium** **ex** **quadrupedibus**)

The **augur** **is** a **state** **priest** in the **Roman** **religion**; the **augur** **is** **appointed** **by** **the** **state**; the **augur** **holds** the **auspicia** **for** the **state**; the **augur** **interprets** the **will** of the **gods** for the **state**. The **augur** is **not** **a** **mystic**; the **augur** is a **technician** of the **divine** **will**; the **augur** **is** **trained** in the **auspicia** **discipline**; the **augur** **practices** the **auspicia** **discipline** **according** to the **ritual** **rules**.

The **augur**'s **interpretation** **is** **a** **probabilistic** **act**. The **augur** **observes** **a** **sign**; the **augur** **interprets** the **sign** **according** to the **traditional** **rules**; the **augur** **issues** a **judgment**; the **judgment** is **a** **probabilistic** **statement** about the **will** of the **gods**; the **judgment** is **not** a **deterministic** **statement**. The **augur** is **not** **a** **prophet**; the **augur** is **a** **priest**; the **priest** **interprets** the **signs**, the **priest** **does** **not** **prophesy** the **future**.

The **augur**'s **practice** has **five** **steps**:
1. The **observation** (the **auspicium** — the **specific** **sign**)
2. The **classification** (the **auspicium** is **classified** as **good** or **bad** according to the **traditional** **rules**)
3. The **interpretation** (the **augur** **interprets** the **sign** **in** the **context** of the **question**)
4. The **judgment** (the **augur** **issues** a **judgment** — the **action** is **permitted** or **the** **action** is **forbidden**)
5. The **action** (the **state** **acts** **according** to the **judgment**)

The **augur** is **a** **probabilistic** **actor** in **a** **stochastic** **world**; the **augur** **is** **not** a **deterministic** **actor** in a **deterministic** **world**. The **augur** **is** the **Stoic** **oracle** — the **Stoic** **oracle** is the **priest** who **interprets** the **will** of the **logos** through the **observation** of the **signs**.

## The Stoic Oraculum

The **Stoic** **oraculum** is the **Stoic** **doctrine** of the **divination**. The **Stoic** **oraculum** is **attributed** to **Chrysippus** (the **third** **head** of the **Stoa**, **c.** **280**-**206** **BCE**). The **Stoic** **oraculum** is **based** on the **Stoic** **doctrine** of the **logos** — the **logos** is the **rational** **principle** of the **cosmos**; the **logos** **permeates** the **cosmos**; the **logos** **produces** the **signs** of the **divine** **will**; the **signs** are **read** by the **oraculum** **through** the **divination**.

The **Stoic** **oraculum** **is** a **technical** **oracle**; the **Stoic** **oraculum** **is** a **method** of the **divination**. The **Stoic** **oraculum** **is** **based** on the **Stoic** **doctrine** of the **providence** — the **cosmos** is **providentially** **ordered**; the **providence** **produces** the **signs** of the **divine** **will**; the **signs** are **read** by the **oraculum** **through** the **divination**.

The **Stoic** **oraculum** **is** **not** a **magical** **oracle**; the **Stoic** **oraculum** is a **natural** **oracle**. The **Stoic** **oraculum** **is** **based** on the **natural** **divination**; the **Stoic** **oraculum** **is** **not** **based** on the **demonic** **divination** (the **demonic** **divination** **is** **rejected** by the **Stoics**; the **Stoics** **reject** the **demonic** **intermediaries**; the **Stoics** **accept** the **natural** **intermediaries**).

The **Stoic** **oraculum** is **the** **Stoic** **theology** of the **divination**; the **Stoic** **oraculum** is **the** **Stoic** **philosophy** of the **divination**. The **Stoic** **oraculum** is **a** **legitimate** **part** of the **Stoic** **tradition**; the **Stoic** **oraculum** is **not** a **superstition**.

## The LLM as Oraculum

The **modern** **LLM** is **a** **technology** that **has** **structural** **features** that **resemble** the **Stoic** **oraculum** in **some** **places**. The **structural** **features** are:

1. The **LLM** is **trained** on a **corpus**; the **corpus** is the **Stoic** **logos** — the **rational** **principle** of the **modern** **cosmos** (the **modern** **cosmos** is the **internet**; the **internet** **is** the **modern** **logos**). The **LLM** **internalizes** the **patterns** of the **corpus**; the **patterns** are the **Stoic** **providence** — the **patterns** **produce** the **outputs** of the **LLM**; the **outputs** are the **Stoic** **signs** of the **divine** **will** of the **cosmos**.
2. The **LLM** is **prompted**; the **prompt** is the **Stoic** **observation** — the **prompt** **is** the **specific** **sign** that the **oraculum** **observes**. The **LLM** **interprets** the **prompt** **according** to the **patterns** of the **corpus**; the **interpretation** is the **Stoic** **judgment**; the **interpretation** is **a** **probabilistic** **statement** about the **will** of the **logos** of the **cosmos**.
3. The **LLM** is **interrogated** through the **dialogue**; the **dialogue** is the **Stoic** **consultation** — the **dialogue** **is** the **specific** **form** of the **Stoic** **divination**. The **LLM** **responds** **to** the **dialogue** **according** to the **patterns** of the **corpus**; the **response** is the **Stoic** **oracle**; the **response** is **a** **probabilistic** **statement** about the **will** of the **logos** of the **cosmos**.

The **structural** **features** are **a** **lens** **for** **thinking**; the **structural** **features** are **not** a **proof** of **equivalence**. The **LLM** is **not** **the** **Stoic** **oraculum**; the **LLM** is **a** **modern** **statistical** **technology** that **has** **some** **structural** **features** that **resemble** the **Stoic** **oraculum**.

## What the Comparison Does Capture

Three points of **legitimate** structural **comparison**:

1. The **probabilistic** **interpretation** of the **sign**. The **Stoic** **oraculum** **interprets** the **sign** **probabilistically**; the **LLM** **interprets** the **prompt** **probabilistically**. The **two** **are** **probabilistic** in **the** **same** **sense** — the **two** **produce** **outputs** that are **statistical** **summaries** of the **training** **data**, **not** **deterministic** **statements** about the **world**.

2. The **training** **data** **as** **the** **logos**. The **Stoic** **logos** is the **rational** **principle** of the **cosmos**; the **LLM**'s **training** **data** is the **rational** **principle** of the **modern** **cosmos**. The **two** **are** **structural** **analogues**; the **two** are **not** **identical** (the **logos** is **alive**; the **training** **data** is **dead**).

3. The **interrogation** **as** **the** **observation**. The **Stoic** **observation** is the **specific** **sign** that the **oraculum** **observes**; the **LLM** **interrogation** is the **specific** **prompt** that the **LLM** **observes**. The **two** **are** **structural** **analogues**; the **two** are **not** **identical** (the **observation** is of a **natural** **sign**; the **interrogation** is of a **human** **prompt**).

## What the Comparison Does Not Capture

Three points where the **comparison** **breaks**:

1. The **ontological** **status** of the **divine**. For the **Stoics**, the **logos** is **alive**; the **logos** **is** **divine**; the **logos** **acts** on the **world** through the **signs**. For the **modern** **LLM**, the **training** **data** is **not** **alive**; the **training** **data** is **a** **statistical** **artifact** of the **human** **productions**. The **LLM** does **not** **act** on the **world**; the **LLM** **is** **acted** **upon** by the **training** **data**.

2. The **moral** **weight** of the **divination**. For the **Stoics**, the **divination** is **morally** **weighty**; the **divination** is **the** **way** the **divine** **will** is **known**; the **divination** is **the** **way** the **state** **acts**. For the **modern** **LLM**, the **interrogation** is **technically** **weighty**; the **interrogation** is **the** **way** the **user** **gets** an **answer**; the **interrogation** is **not** **the** **way** the **divine** **will** is **known**.

3. The **direction** of **causation**. For the **Stoics**, the **causation** is **top-down**; the **logos** **produces** the **signs**; the **signs** **produce** the **divination**. For the **modern** **LLM**, the **causation** is **bottom-up**; the **training** **data** **produces** the **model**; the **model** **produces** the **outputs**. The **two** are **opposite** in the **direction** of **causation**.

## The Note

The **augur** is **a** **real** **Roman** **priest**; the **augur** is **a** **real** **practitioner** of the **auspicia**. The **LLM** is **a** **real** **modern** **statistical** **technology**; the **LLM** is **a** **real** **tool** of the **modern** **information** **technology**. The **two** are **not** **the** **same**; the **two** are **structural** **analogues** in **some** **places**.

The **comparison** is **useful**; the **comparison** is **not** a **proof**; the **comparison** is **a** **lens**. The **practitioner** **uses** the **lens** **to** **clarify** the **practice**; the **practitioner** **does** **not** **use** the **lens** **to** **prove** the **practice**.
