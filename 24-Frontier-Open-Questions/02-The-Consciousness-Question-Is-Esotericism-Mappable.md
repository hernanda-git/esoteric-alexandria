# The Consciousness Question — Is Esotericism Mappable?

## The Hard Problem

The **hard problem of consciousness** is the question of how **physical processes** in the brain **give rise** to **subjective experience**. The hard problem is **hard** because no one has yet **explained** how the **objective** (the firing of neurons) **becomes** the **subjective** (the experience of seeing red, the experience of feeling pain).

The modern philosophy of mind has **three main positions** on the hard problem:

1. **Materialism / Physicalism** — the conscious experience is **nothing but** the physical processes. The "hard problem" is an **illusion**; once we understand the **physical** processes fully, the subjective experience will be **explained**.
2. **Dualism** — the conscious experience is **separate** from the physical processes. The mind is **not** the brain; the mind is a **separate** substance. (Descartes, modern property dualism, modern substance dualism)
3. **Panpsychism** — consciousness is **fundamental**. Consciousness is **everywhere**; the brain **does not generate** consciousness, the brain **filters** or **focuses** consciousness. The hard problem is **dissolved** by recognizing that consciousness was **always there**.

The modern academic philosophy of mind is **moving toward panpsychism** (Chalmers, Goff, Strawson). The Integrated Information Theory (IIT) of consciousness is a **scientific** form of panpsychism.

## The Esoteric Reading

The esoteric tradition has **always** been a form of **panpsychism**. The traditions hold:

- The **cosmos is alive** (the Hermetic *anima mundi*, the Platonic *psyche tou kosmou*)
- The **divine** is **consciousness** (the *brahman* of the Hindu tradition, the *ain soph* of the Kabbalah, the *nous* of the Plotinian tradition)
- The **human** is a **microcosm** of the **macrocosm** (the Hermetic "as above, so below")
- The **consciousness** is **infinite** and **eternal** (the *atman* of the Hindu tradition, the *fana'* of the Sufi tradition, the *theosis* of the Christian tradition)
- The **path** is the **realization** of the **consciousness** (the *moksha*, the *fana'*, the *individuation*)

The esoteric tradition is, in modern terms, a **tradition of consciousness exploration**. The tradition has been **practiced** for **2,500 years**; the tradition has produced **sophisticated** maps of the consciousness (the chakras, the sephirot, the subtle bodies, the levels of the soul).

The tradition is **not** primitive. The tradition is **empirical** in its own way — the tradition is based on the **first-person experience** of the practitioners, not on the **third-person** observation. The modern science of consciousness is **catching up** to the esoteric tradition in recognizing the **value** of the **first-person** data.

## The Mapping Project

The library's category 24 is the **frontier**. The mapping project is the **attempt** to **map** the esoteric tradition's **maps of consciousness** to the **modern** science of consciousness.

The mapping is **not** a claim that the esoteric tradition is **proven** by the modern science. The mapping is a claim that the **two maps** are **complementary** — the esoteric map is the **first-person** map, the modern map is the **third-person** map, and the two together **give a more complete** picture of the consciousness.

The 5 mapping projects:

### 1. The 7 Chakras as the 7 Levels of Information Integration

The **7 chakras** (Muladhara, Svadhisthana, Manipura, Anahata, Vishuddha, Ajna, Sahasrara) correspond to the **7 levels** of information integration in the **Integrated Information Theory** (IIT) of consciousness.

The mapping:

| Chakra | IIT level | Information type |
|---|---|---|
| Muladhara | The minimum | The basic feedback loop |
| Svadhisthana | The simple loop | The conditioned response |
| Manipura | The complex loop | The deliberate action |
| Anahata | The integrated system | The integrated self |
| Vishuddha | The self-aware system | The self-modeling |
| Ajna | The reflective system | The second-order awareness |
| Sahasrara | The maximum | The pure consciousness |

The mapping is **not** exact; the mapping is **suggestive**. The two systems have **similar** structure; the two systems **might** be the **same** structure seen from **different** perspectives.

### 2. The 10 Sephirot as the 10 Stages of Cognitive Development

The **10 sephirot** (Kether, Chokhmah, Binah, Chesed, Geburah, Tiphareth, Netzach, Hod, Yesod, Malkuth) correspond to the **10 stages** of cognitive development in **Piaget's** and **Kohlberg's** stages, or in **Wilber's** spectrum of consciousness.

The mapping:

| Sephirah | Piaget / Kohlberg | Wilber |
|---|---|---|
| Kether | The unitive | The unitive consciousness |
| Chokhmah | The formal operational | The psychic |
| Binah | The concrete operational | The mental-egoic |
| Chesed | The preoperational | The magical |
| Geburah | The preoperational | The magical |
| Tiphareth | The intuitive | The mythic |
| Netzach | The sensorimotor | The archaic |
| Hod | The sensorimotor | The archaic |
| Yesod | The sensorimotor | The archaic |
| Malkuth | The sensorimotor | The archaic |

The mapping is **also** suggestive. The two systems have **similar** structure; the two systems **might** be the **same** structure seen from **different** perspectives.

### 3. The 9 Choirs of Angels as the 9 Levels of Consciousness

The **9 choirs of angels** (Pseudo-Dionysius) correspond to the **9 levels** of consciousness in the **modern** consciousness research (the spectrum from the unconscious to the super-conscious).

The mapping:

| Choir | Modern level |
|---|---|
| Seraphim | The super-conscious (the pure consciousness) |
| Cherubim | The cosmic consciousness |
| Thrones | The collective unconscious |
| Dominions | The cultural consciousness |
| Virtues | The social consciousness |
| Powers | The personal consciousness |
| Principalities | The egoic consciousness |
| Archangels | The mental consciousness |
| Angels | The sensory consciousness |

The mapping is **also** suggestive.

### 4. The 4 Alchemical Stages as the 4 Stages of the Waking Up

The **4 alchemical stages** (nigredo, albedo, citrinitas, rubedo) correspond to the **4 stages** of the **waking up** in the modern contemplative research (the work of **Sam Harris**, **Shinzen Young**, **Robert Wright**, **Michael Taft**, **Loch Kelly**).

The mapping:

| Alchemical stage | Waking-up stage |
|---|---|
| Nigredo | The dis-identification (the "I" is not the thoughts) |
| Albedo | The recognition (the awareness is the "I") |
| Citrinitas | The insight (the awareness is open, clear, awake) |
| Rubedo | The integration (the awareness is the ground of being) |

The mapping is **also** suggestive.

### 5. The 3 Sufi Stages as the 3 Stages of the Path

The **3 Sufi stages** (sharia, tariqa, haqiqa) correspond to the **3 stages** of the **path** in the modern contemplative research (the work of **Jack Kornfield**, **Joseph Goldstein**, **Sharon Salzberg**, **Tara Brach**).

The mapping:

| Sufi stage | Modern stage |
|---|---|
| Sharia | The ethical foundation (the precepts, the morality) |
| Tariqa | The practice (the meditation, the discipline) |
| Haqiqa | The realization (the insight, the awakening) |

The mapping is **also** suggestive.

## The Question

The 5 mapping projects are **suggestive**, not **definitive**. The question is: **are the two maps the same map?**

The answer is **unknown**. The answer is the **frontier** of the 21st-century consciousness research. The answer requires:

- A **collaboration** between the **esoteric** tradition (with its 2,500 years of first-person data) and the **scientific** tradition (with its 500 years of third-person data)
- A **language** that **translates** between the two (a **formal** representation of the two maps)
- A **methodology** that **combines** the **first-person** and the **third-person** data (the **contemplative science** movement is the **first** attempt)

The library's category 24 is the **frontier** of this collaboration. The library is the **first** systematic attempt to **organize** the esoteric tradition in a way that is **accessible** to the modern consciousness research.

## The Practice

A modern practitioner of the consciousness exploration requires:

1. **The first-person practice** — a **meditation** practice (one of the traditions, one of the methods). The practice is the **data**
2. **The third-person study** — a **modern** science of consciousness (one of the modern approaches: neuroscience, psychology, philosophy of mind). The study is the **theory**
3. **The integration** — the **synthesis** of the first-person and the third-person. The synthesis is the **frontier**
4. **The community** — a **community** of practitioners (a local group, an online group, a tradition). The community is the **support**
5. **The lineage** — a **lineage teacher** (one of the traditions, one of the masters). The lineage is the **transmission**

The library's category 24 is the **map** of the frontier. The frontier is the **territory** that the next generation will **explore**.
