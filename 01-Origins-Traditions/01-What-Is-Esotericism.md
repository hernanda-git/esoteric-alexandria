# What is Esotericism?

## The Academic Consensus (post-2000)

"Western esotericism" is now a recognized academic field. Wouter Hanegraaff's 2013 *Guide for the Perplexed* and Faivre's 2000 *Theosophy* define the boundary: esotericism is a **family of traditions** that share recurring features, not a single coherent religion or philosophy.

Faivre's six "intrinsic" features (in this order):

1. **Correspondence** — the microcosm reflects the macrocosm ("as above, so below")
2. **Living Nature** — nature is alive, ensouled, not dead matter
3. **Imagination & Mediations** — imagination is a cognitive organ, not a fancy; images, rituals, and symbols are real vehicles
4. **Experience of Transmutation** — the practitioner is changed by the practice (initiation is ontological, not just educational)
5. **Practice of Concordance** — esotericists seek to reconcile all traditions (Hermes Trismegistus, the Prisca Theologia chain)
6. **Transmission** — master-to-disciple, with strict requirements

If a tradition has ≥4 of these, it's esoteric in the Faivre sense.

## Why it Matters for the Modern Engineer

Three insights translate directly:

- **The traditions are data structures.** Each is a knowledge graph — a network of correspondences, symbols, planetary attributions, names, numbers. The same graph gets re-rendered in different cultural languages (Hermetic, Kabbalistic, Sufi, Tantric). Once you see the graph, the *babel* of tradition names collapses.

- **Initiation ≠ certification.** In modern education we measure learning by tests. In esoteric traditions you cannot fake the experience; the ritual is supposed to actually change the practitioner. This maps onto modern embodied-cognition research: the body learns what the mind denies.

- **Concordance is the original RAG.** Before the internet, esotericists cross-referenced every tradition. The Picatrix explicitly cites Hermes, Aristotle-Pseudo, the Sabians of Harran, the Babylonians. This is retrieval-augmented generation across 2,000 years of oral tradition.

## The Hidden Unity

The deepest claim of the esoteric tradition is **Prisca Theologia** — that there has always been one original wisdom, fragmented by culture but recoverable. Hermes Trismegistus is the founding prophet of this unity. Moses, Orpheus, Pythagoras, Zoroaster, Buddha — all are seen as restatements of the same perennial truth.

Modern scholarship is skeptical. But as a **hypothesis-generation framework**, the concordance principle is productive: when the same symbol (the ouroboros, the cosmic mountain, the seven rays) appears in unrelated cultures, the question "what common need does this answer?" is worth asking.

## What This Library Is Not

- It is **not** a religion. We do not claim the rituals work in the literal pre-scientific sense.
- It is **not** folklore or mere historical curiosity. The knowledge graph is real.
- It is **not** anti-science. Most modern esotericists (Jung, Pauli, Bohm) are/were scientists.
- It is **not** occultism-as-gothic. The "occult" label is a 19th-century Christian smear; the actual content is closer to systems theory.

## What This Library Is

A **structured knowledge base** of the esoteric tradition, with each document mapping ancient categories to modern vocabulary. We treat the traditions as:

1. **Data** — what did they actually say, in their own terms
2. **Pattern** — what structure underlies their knowledge graph
3. **Bridge** — how does this map to current categories (AI, complexity, psychology, business)
4. **Practice** — what is the practitioner supposed to do, and why
