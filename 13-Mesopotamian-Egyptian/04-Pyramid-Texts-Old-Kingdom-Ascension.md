# The Pyramid Texts — Old Kingdom Funerary Ascension

## The Core Claim

> **The king does not die like a man; inscribed with the correct words, his soul flies up the eastern sky to join the imperishable stars and the sun-god Ra.**

The **Pyramid Texts** are the oldest corpus of religious literature in the world — older than the *Enuma Elish*, older than the *Rigveda*. They are ~760 **utterances** (*spells*, *Sprüche*) carved in vertical columns on the limestone walls of royal pyramids at Saqqara, beginning with the Pyramid of **Unas** (last king of the 5th Dynasty, c. 2375–2345 BCE) and continuing through the 6th Dynasty pyramids of Teti, Pepi I, Merenre, Pepi II, and several queens.

## The Structure

The texts are not a single book but a **reusable liturgical kit**. Each pyramid reproduces a selection of the utterances; no two pyramids carry the identical set. The spells cluster into functional groups:

| Group | Purpose | Sample Utterance |
|---|---|---|
| **Offering / purification** | Equip the king with food and ritual purity in the beyond | "O Unas, take the eye of Horus, that you may be satisfied therewith." |
| **Ascension / star-journey** | Ferry the king to the sky | "O King, you are this great star, the companion of Ra, who traverses the sky." |
| **Osirian union** | Identify the king with the resurrected Osiris | "You have not departed dead; you have departed alive." |
| **Apotropaic / ward** | Repel the serpent and the enemies of the dead | "The cord is severed; the god is freed." |

The recurring **"Cannibal Hymn"** (Utterances 273–274, in the pyramids of Unas and Teti) describes the king consuming the gods to absorb their power — a striking instance of ritual theophagy at the dawn of recorded religion.

## The Logic of Ascension

The central drama is the king's transformation (*akh*, "effective spirit"). The texts present two intertwined routes to immortality:

1. **Solar** — the king joins **Ra** in his day-bark, sailing the sky by day and the underworld by night. This is the path of light and kingship.
2. **Stellar** — the king becomes one of the **imperishable stars** (*akhui*, the circumpolar stars that never set), dwelling in the "Field of Offerings" (*Sekhet Hetep*). This is the path of permanence.

Both routes require the **words**. The utterance is not a prayer *to* the gods so much as a **technology of identity**: to speak "I am Ra" correctly is to *become* Ra. The spoken formula *is* the mechanism of transformation — a view of language as ontologically effective that the Egyptian tradition never abandoned (see the later [[02-Egypt-Book-Of-The-Dead]] and the Coffin Texts).

## Historical Position

The Pyramid Texts are the **ancestor** of all later Egyptian funerary literature. In the Middle Kingdom they democratized: copied onto coffins for non-royal officials, they became the **Coffin Texts** (c. 2000 BCE), which in turn expanded into the **Book of the Dead** (c. 1550 BCE onward). The trajectory is one of **democratization of the afterlife** — from exclusive royal inscription to a purchasable papyrus for any competent household.

The texts also preserve the earliest explicit **Osiris myth** in writing, centuries before the great temples of Abydos. They are, in effect, the seed-bed of Egyptian eschatology.

## The Modern Note

The Pyramid Texts are the earliest evidence we possess for the claim that **language constructs reality** — that the correctly uttered word *does* what it says. This is the same operating assumption underwriting *'ilm al-huruf* (see [[02-Hurufiyya-Lettrism/01-Ilm-al-Huruf-Overview]]) and the Hermetic *verbum* tradition: speech is not description but causation. The Egyptian king's ascent is the prototype of every later "as above, so below" ascent doctrine in the library.
