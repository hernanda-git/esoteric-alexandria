# Source Index — The Major Texts of the Tradition

## How to Use This Index

The library's 60+ documents cite **primary** and **secondary** sources, often in a **traditional** form (the *Fusus*, the *Futuhat*, the *Corpus Hermeticum*). This index collects the **major primary texts** by **tradition**, with the **standard edition or translation** the library relies on, so the practitioner can **locate** the **real** source behind each claim. It is the **bibliographic** companion to the [[04-Biographical-Index-Major-Figures]] and the [[02-Cross-Tradition-Concordance-Table]].

## Islamic & Hurufi Sources

- **al-Futuhat al-Makkiyya** (Ibn Arabi) — the Meccan Illuminations; Osman Yahya critical edition (1968–1992); partial English in Chittick
- **Fusus al-Hikam** (Ibn Arabi) — R.W.J. Austin trans. (1980, the standard English)
- **Shams al-Ma'arif** (al-Buni) — the major Arabic lettrist grimoire; see [[02-Hurufiyya-Lettrism/05-Shams-al-Maarif-Actual-Structure]]
- **Ghayat al-Hakim** (Picatrix) — the Arabic source of the Latin *Picatrix*; see [[12-Agrippa-Picatrix-Grimoires/02-Picatrix-Ghayat-al-Hakim]]

## The Note

The **editions** listed are the **ones the corpus cites**; they are **not** the only ones. Where a document gives a **traditional** title without a **specific** edition, the **title** is the **canonical** reference and the **linked doc** carries the **scholarly** detail. The practitioner should **prefer** the **edition** named in the **linked** document over any **generic** reprint.
