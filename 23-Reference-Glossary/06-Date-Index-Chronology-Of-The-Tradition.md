# Date Index — Chronology of the Tradition

## How to Use This Index

The **Date Index** is the **chronology** companion to the reading order (see [[03-Reading-Order-Index-By-Tradition]]). Where the reading order is *by tradition*, the Date Index is **by time** — both the **historical timeline** of the tradition's texts and the **annual liturgical cycle** practitioners actually keep. The two grids let the reader answer "what was happening when?" and "what do I observe when?"

## Part B — The Annual Observance Cycle

The practitioner keeps time by **cycles**, not only by history. The major living observances:

- **Weekly** — the seven planetary days; the Kabbalistic Sephirah of each day; the planetary hours (cf. [[03-Planetary-Astrology/05-Planetary-Hours]], [[19-Business-Applications/01-Planetary-Hours-Business]]).
- **Lunar** — the new-moon (*kiddush levanah*) and full-moon talismanic elections (cf. [[05-Talismanic-Magick/02-Planetary-Talismans]]).
- **Solar / Seasonal** — the eight-fold wheel (solstices, equinoxes, cross-quarters) echoed in the *four worlds* and the alchemical *stage operations* (cf. [[04-Alchemy-Transmutation/02-Stage-Operations]]).
- **Festival** — the Jewish *mo'edim* as *tikkun* nodes (cf. the Kabbalistic curriculum, [[22-Case-Studies-Curricula/06-Kabbalistic-Tradition-12-Month-Curriculum]]); the Islamic *mawlid* and the *'ilm al-huruf* anniversaries (cf. [[09-Sufism-Irfan/01-Sufism-Overview]]).

## The Meta-Note

Time in the tradition is **cyclical and linear at once**: the *historical timeline* (Part A) is a once-only sequence; the *observance cycle* (Part B) returns. The esoteric calendar holds both — the eternal return folded into the arrow of history. As Eliade framed it, the sacred *recurs*; the profane *passes*.
