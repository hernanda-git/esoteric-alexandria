# Cross-Tradition Concordance — Same Concept, Different Names

## The Master Concordance

The esoteric traditions have **independently developed** similar concepts in different vocabularies. The **concordance** is the **master index** of these parallel concepts. The concordance is the **most important reference** in the library — it is the **bridge** between the traditions.

The concordance is **not** a claim that the traditions are **identical**. The traditions are **distinct**, with **distinct** practices, **distinct** lineages, **distinct** commitments. The concordance is the claim that the traditions are **isomorphic** — they map to the **same** underlying structure, with **different** names for the same **point**.

The concordance is the **most powerful argument** for the **perennial philosophy** (see [[01-Origins-Traditions/05-Perennial-Philosophy]]). The concordance is the **evidence** for the **independent cross-tradition convergence**.

## The Use of the Concordance

The concordance has **three uses**:

1. **The bridge** — the concordance is the **bridge** between the traditions. The practitioner can move from the **familiar** tradition to the **unfamiliar** tradition through the **shared** concept
2. **The deep study** — the concordance is the **foundation** of the deep study. The practitioner can study **one** concept (e.g., the master number, the path of return) and see the **same** concept in **all** the traditions
3. **The perennial philosophy** — the concordance is the **evidence** for the **perennial philosophy** (see [[01-Origins-Traditions/05-Perennial-Philosophy]]). The concordance shows that the **same** underlying structure appears in **all** the traditions

The concordance is **not** a claim that the traditions are **identical**. The concordance is a claim that the traditions are **isomorphic** — they map to the **same** underlying structure, with **different** names for the **same** point.
