# Esoteric Glossary — The Master Index

## A

- **Abjad** — the Semitic number-letter system used in Arabic, Hebrew, and Aramaic. See [[02-Hurufiyya-Lettrism/02-Abjad-Numerals]]
- **Adept** — a master of the esoteric tradition. In the Golden Dawn, the highest rank
- **Agathodaimon** — the "good spirit" of the Egyptian-Greek tradition, identified with Hermes and the Sabians
- **Albedo** — the second stage of the alchemical work, the "whitening". See [[04-Alchemy-Transmutation/02-Stage-Operations]]
- **Anamnesis** — the "un-forgetting", the Platonic doctrine that learning is the recovery of what the soul already knows
- **Anima** — the feminine archetype in the male psyche. See [[18-Psychology-Jungian/03-Archetypes-And-Collective-Unconscious]]
- **Animus** — the masculine archetype in the female psyche
- **Archetype** — the universal, inherited pattern of the human psyche. See [[18-Psychology-Jungian/03-Archetypes-And-Collective-Unconscious]]
- **Asana** — the yoga posture, the physical position of the meditation
- **Astral** — the subtle body, the vehicle of consciousness between the gross and the divine. Modern usage is from the Theosophical Society

## B

- **Bagua** — the 8 Chinese trigrams, the building blocks of the I Ching
- **Bann, Al-** (البنّ) — the fourth class of jinn in the esoteric Islamic fourfold classification. The **hidden jinn** of the subterranean realm, associated with fragrance (*bān* = the fragrant shrub), treasure guardianship, and esoteric knowledge. The most material of the jinn classes, they are the most amenable to binding operations (*'azima*). See [[06-Angels-Jinn-Demons/08-The-Four-Jinn-Classes-Hinn-Bann-Jann-Jinn]]
- **Basmala** — the Islamic invocation "In the name of God, the Merciful, the Compassionate" (Bismillah al-Rahman al-Rahim)
- **Bats** — Arabic for the "inner" (in Sufism, the esoteric as opposed to the *zahir*)

## C

- **Chakra** — the energy center in the subtle body. See [[14-Indian-Tantric-Chinese/01-Seven-Chakras]]
- **Chi** — Chinese for the vital breath, the life force. See [[14-Indian-Tantric-Chinese/02-Chinese-Alchemy-Neidan-Waidan]]
- **Citrinitas** — the third stage of the alchemical work, the "yellowing"
- **Coniunctio Oppositorum** — the conjunction of opposites, the central symbol of the individuation process. See [[18-Psychology-Jungian/04-Individuation-Process]]
- **Cymatics** — the study of visible sound and vibration. See [[16-Science-Resonance/01-Cymatics-And-Vibration]]

## D

- **Dantian** — the "cinnabar field" in the Chinese subtle body, the lower elixir field in the abdomen
- **Dharma** — the cosmic order, the law, the truth. In Hinduism and Buddhism
- **Dhikr** — the Sufi practice of remembrance, the repetition of the divine names
- **Dodecahedron** — the 12-faced Platonic solid, the symbol of the cosmos in the Greek tradition
- **Druze** — a Gnostic religion centered in Lebanon, Syria, and Israel; a surviving tradition of the Ismaili and Isma'ili Shia communities

## E

- **Egregore** — the thought-form, the entity created by a group mind. The concept is central to the ceremonial magic tradition
- **Ein Sof** — the Infinite, the source of the 10 sephirot in the Kabbalistic tradition. See [[08-Kabbalah-Numerology/01-Tree-Of-Life]]
- **Emerald Tablet** (Tabula Smaragdina) — the foundational alchemical text, attributed to Hermes Trismegistus
- **Enochian** — the magical language and the system of the Elizabethan magus John Dee (1527-1608/9)
- **Exoteric** — the outer, public, exoteric teaching (as opposed to esoteric). See [[01-Origins-Traditions/02-Esoteric-Vs-Exoteric]]

## F

- **Fana'** — the Sufi doctrine of the annihilation of the ego. See [[09-Sufism-Irfan/01-Sufism-Overview]]
- **Faust** — the figure of the Western magus, the seeker of forbidden knowledge, popularized by Goethe
- **Feng Shui** — the Chinese art of placement, the system of harmonizing human beings with their environment
- **Four Worlds** — the 4 levels of reality in the Kabbalistic tradition (Atziluth, Beriah, Yetzirah, Assiah). See [[08-Kabbalah-Numerology/01-Tree-Of-Life]]

## G

- **Gematria** — the Hebrew number-letter system. See [[08-Kabbalah-Numerology/02-Gematria-Mapping]]
- **Goetia** — the section of the Lesser Key of Solomon dealing with the summoning of spirits. See [[05-Talismanic-Magick/03-Goetia-Solomonic-Grimoire]]
- **Gnosis** — the direct knowledge of the divine, the central concept of the Gnostic tradition. See [[10-Hermeticism-Gnosticism/02-Gnosticism-Overview]]
- **Golden Dawn** — the Hermetic Order of the Golden Dawn, the influential 19th-century Western esoteric order (1888-1903)
- **Great Work** (Opus Magnum) — the central alchemical project, the transmutation of lead to gold. See [[04-Alchemy-Transmutation/01-Alchemy-Overview]]

## H

- **Heka** — the Egyptian magical power, the personified deity of magic
- **Hinn, Al-** (الحينّ) — the third class of jinn in the esoteric Islamic fourfold classification. Created from the interaction of fire and earth, the Hinn are the **mutable jinn** associated with time, transformation, and decay. They inhabit the liminal threshold zones between worlds and are the most commonly encountered jinn in folk tradition. See [[06-Angels-Jinn-Demons/08-The-Four-Jinn-Classes-Hinn-Bann-Jann-Jinn]]
- **Hermes Trismegistus** — "Hermes the Thrice-Greatest", the legendary author of the Hermetic texts. See [[10-Hermeticism-Gnosticism/01-Corpus-Hermeticum]]
- **Hexagram** — the 6-line figure in the I Ching, one of 64. See [[07-Divination-Geomancy/03-I-Ching-64-Hexagrams]]
- **Hieros Gamos** — the sacred marriage, the coniunctio oppositorum
- **Hod** — the 8th sephirah, Splendor. See [[08-Kabbalah-Numerology/01-Tree-Of-Life]]
- **Hyle** — matter, the lowest level of the Plotinian emanation

## I

- **I Ching** (Yi Jing) — the Book of Changes, the Chinese divination classic. See [[07-Divination-Geomancy/03-I-Ching-64-Hexagrams]]
- **'Ilm al-Huruf** — the Islamic science of letters. See [[02-Hurufiyya-Lettrism/01-Ilm-al-Huruf-Overview]]
- **Iamblichus** — the 3rd-4th c. Neoplatonist, the founder of theurgy. See [[11-NeoPlatonism-Theurgy/01-Iamblichus-De-Mysteriis]]
- **Idris** — in the Islamic tradition, the prophet identified with Hermes Trismegistus and Enoch
- **Ifa** — the West African divination system, structurally identical to geomancy. See [[07-Divination-Geomancy/02-Geomancy-Earth-Speaks]]
- **Individuation** — the Jungian process of becoming a self. See [[18-Psychology-Jungian/04-Individuation-Process]]
- **Isnad** — the chain of transmission in the Islamic hadith tradition. See [[01-Origins-Traditions/03-Oral-Transmission-Chains]]

## J

- **Jafr** — the Islamic science of the unseen, the divination by letter arithmetic. See [[02-Hurufiyya-Lettrism/03-Jafr-Ilm-of-Letters]]
- **Jann, Al-** (الجانّ) — the second class of jinn in the esoteric Islamic fourfold classification. The **primordial progenitor** of the jinn, created from scorching fire (*nār al-samūm*) before the creation of Adam (Quran 15:27). The "father of the jinn" from whom all jinn descend. Associated with Iblis in esoteric commentary. See [[06-Angels-Jinn-Demons/08-The-Four-Jinn-Classes-Hinn-Bann-Jann-Jinn]]
- **Jinn** — the non-human, non-angel intelligent beings of the Islamic tradition. See [[06-Angels-Jinn-Demons/03-Jinn-Islamic-Spirit-Lore]] and [[06-Angels-Jinn-Demons/08-The-Four-Jinn-Classes-Hinn-Bann-Jann-Jinn]]
- **Junius** — the Avestan term for the spirit world, the ancestor of the jinn concept
- **Jung** — Carl Gustav Jung (1875-1961), the founder of Analytical Psychology. See [[18-Psychology-Jungian/01-Jung-And-Alchemy]]

## K

- **Ka** — the Egyptian concept of the life-force, the astral body
- **Kabbalah** — the Jewish esoteric tradition. See [[08-Kabbalah-Numerology/]]
- **Kamea** — the Jewish equivalent of the *wafq*, the magic square. See [[02-Hurufiyya-Lettrism/04-Wafq-Magic-Squares]]
- **Karma** — the Buddhist and Hindu doctrine of action and consequence
- **Kether** — the 1st sephirah, Crown. See [[08-Kabbalah-Numerology/01-Tree-Of-Life]]
- **Kundalini** — the dormant power at the base of the spine. See [[14-Indian-Tantric-Chinese/01-Seven-Chakras]]

## L

- **Liberation** — the goal of all the esoteric traditions, the *moksha* of Hinduism, the *nibbana* of Buddhism, the *fana'* of Sufism, the *apotheosis* of theurgy
- **Lilith** — the female demon of the Jewish tradition, the first wife of Adam in some traditions

## M

- **Ma'at** — the Egyptian goddess of truth, justice, order. The "feather of Ma'at" in the judgment of the dead. See [[13-Mesopotamian-Egyptian/02-Egypt-Book-Of-The-Dead]]
- **Magus** — the master of magic, the Western esoteric title. The modern title in the Golden Dawn
- **Malkuth** — the 10th sephirah, Kingdom. The lowest sephirah, the material world
- **Mandala** — the circular diagram representing the cosmos. See [[18-Psychology-Jungian/03-Archetypes-And-Collective-Unconscious]]
- **Mantra** — the sacred syllable or phrase. See [[14-Indian-Tantric-Chinese/03-Tantra-Left-Hand-Right-Hand]]
- **Maqam** — the Sufi station, the level of the spiritual path. See [[09-Sufism-Irfan/01-Sufism-Overview]]
- **Maqlû** — the Mesopotamian fire-rite, the ritual that burns the witch
- **Melekh** — the King, the archangelic designation in the Kabbalistic tradition
- **Merkabah** — the chariot, the throne-chariot of God in the prophetic vision of Ezekiel; the basis of the Merkabah mysticism
- **Moksha** — the Hindu doctrine of liberation
- **Monad** — the One, the source, the principle of unity. See [[17-Philosophy-Cosmology/01-Emanation-And-Descent]]

## N

- **Nadis** — the channels of prana in the subtle body. See [[14-Indian-Tantric-Chinese/01-Seven-Chakras]]
- **Neidan** — the inner alchemy of the Chinese Taoist tradition. See [[14-Indian-Tantric-Chinese/02-Chinese-Alchemy-Neidan-Waidan]]
- **Neoplatonism** — the philosophical tradition of Plotinus, Iamblichus, Proclus. See [[11-NeoPlatonism-Theurgy/]]
- **Nigredo** — the first stage of the alchemical work, the "blackening". See [[04-Alchemy-Transmutation/02-Stage-Operations]]
- **Nirvana** — the Buddhist doctrine of liberation, the "blowing out" of the flames
- **Numinous** — the sacred, the experienced presence of the divine. See [[18-Psychology-Jungian/03-Archetypes-And-Collective-Unconscious]]

## O

- **One** (to Hen) — the Plotinian first principle, the source of all
- **Opus Magnum** — the Great Work of alchemy. See [[04-Alchemy-Transmutation/01-Alchemy-Overview]]
- **Oracle** — the place or the practice of divine communication. See [[07-Divination-Geomancy/01-Divination-Methods-Overview]]
- **Ouija** — the modern board, the descendant of the ancient planchette

## P

- **Pentacle** — the Western disc containing the square, sigil, and divine name. See [[05-Talismanic-Magick/01-Squares-And-Sigils]]
- **Persona** — the mask, the social face. See [[18-Psychology-Jungian/03-Archetypes-And-Collective-Unconscious]]
- **Philosopher's Stone** — the goal of the alchemical work, the *lapis philosophorum*. See [[04-Alchemy-Transmutation/01-Alchemy-Overview]]
- **Picatrix** — the most influential Arabic talismanic text. See [[12-Agrippa-Picatrix-Grimoires/02-Picatrix-Ghayat-al-Hakim]]
- **Plotinus** — the founder of Neoplatonism (3rd c. CE)
- **Prima Materia** — the primary matter, the base material of the alchemical work
- **Prisca Theologia** — the "original theology" doctrine, the claim of a single esoteric source for all traditions. See [[01-Origins-Traditions/05-Perennial-Philosophy]]
- **Puja** — the Hindu and Tantric ritual worship. See [[14-Indian-Tantric-Chinese/03-Tantra-Left-Hand-Right-Hand]]

## Q

- **Qabalah** (alternate spelling) — see Kabbalah
- **Qi** (or Chi) — the Chinese vital breath, the life force. See [[14-Indian-Tantric-Chinese/02-Chinese-Alchemy-Neidan-Waidan]]
- **Qutb** — the "axis", the supreme saint of a Sufi order

## R

- **Rebis** — the "two-thing", the alchemical hermaphrodite. See [[04-Alchemy-Transmutation/01-Alchemy-Overview]]
- **Rubedo** — the fourth stage of the alchemical work, the "reddening". See [[04-Alchemy-Transmutation/02-Stage-Operations]]

## S

- **Sabian** — the pre-Islamic Babylonian star-worshippers of Harran. See [[13-Mesopotamian-Egyptian/03-Sabians-Of-Harran-Last-Babylonians]]
- **Sama** — the Sufi practice of listening, the music and the dance
- **Sampradaya** — the Hindu lineage of teachers
- **Sanhedrin** — the ancient Jewish council; also the text of the Jewish magical tradition
- **Sefer Yetzirah** — the Jewish "Book of Creation", one of the foundational texts of the Kabbalah
- **Sephirah** (singular of sephirot) — the divine attribute in the Kabbalistic tradition
- **Sephirot** — the 10 divine attributes of the Kabbalistic Tree of Life. See [[08-Kabbalah-Numerology/01-Tree-Of-Life]]
- **Shakti** — the feminine power, the active energy in the Hindu Tantric tradition
- **Shaytan** — the Arabic for Satan, the rebellious jinn
- **Shem ha-Mephorash** — the 72-fold name of God in the Jewish tradition. See [[06-Angels-Jinn-Demons/02-Goetic-Spirits-72-Shemhamphorash]]
- **Shiva** — the Hindu deity, the "destroyer" (also "creator" and "regenerator")
- **Sigil** — the inscribed figure, the compressed name. See [[05-Talismanic-Magick/01-Squares-And-Sigils]]
- **Silsila** — the Sufi chain of transmission. See [[09-Sufism-Irfan/01-Sufism-Overview]]
- **Soma** — the sacred plant of the Vedic tradition; also the inner plant, the nectar of the moon
- **Sorcery** — the popular term for magic, usually with negative connotations
- **Sufism** — the Islamic mystical tradition. See [[09-Sufism-Irfan/]]
- **Sushumna** — the central nadi, the channel of the Kundalini
- **Svadhisthana** — the 2nd chakra. See [[14-Indian-Tantric-Chinese/01-Seven-Chakras]]

## T

- **T'ai Chi** (Taiji) — the Supreme Ultimate, the Taoist concept of the unity of yin and yang
- **Tantra** — the Hindu and Buddhist tradition of the esoteric teachings. See [[14-Indian-Tantric-Chinese/03-Tantra-Left-Hand-Right-Hand]]
- **Tao** (Dao) — the Way, the source. See [[17-Philosophy-Cosmology/03-Tao-Te-Ching-Laozi]]
- **Tao Te Ching** — the foundational text of Taoism. See [[17-Philosophy-Cosmology/03-Tao-Te-Ching-Laozi]]
- **Tetragrammaton** — the 4-letter name of God (YHWH)
- **Tetraktys** — the Pythagorean symbol. See [[17-Philosophy-Cosmology/04-Tetraktys-And-Pythagorean-Number]]
- **Theurgy** — the ritual work of the gods, the Neoplatonic and Hermetic practice. See [[11-NeoPlatonism-Theurgy/01-Iamblichus-De-Mysteriis]]
- **Tiamat** — the Babylonian chaos-dragon, the primordial mother
- **Tikkun** — the Lurianic doctrine of repair, restoration
- **Tiphareth** — the 6th sephirah, Beauty. See [[08-Kabbalah-Numerology/01-Tree-Of-Life]]
- **Tohu** — the primordial chaos in the Kabbalistic tradition
- **Tophet** — the place of child sacrifice in the Canaanite tradition; the symbol of the suppressed
- **Trigrams** — the 8 trigrams of the I Ching. See [[07-Divination-Geomancy/03-I-Ching-64-Hexagrams]]

## U

- **Ulema** — the Islamic legal scholars, the interpreters of the Sharia
- **Upanishad** — the Hindu philosophical texts, the "sitting near" the teacher
- **Ur** — the ancient Sumerian city, the origin of much of the Mesopotamian tradition
- **Usul al-Fiqh** — the principles of Islamic jurisprudence

## V

- **Vamamarga** — the "left-hand" Tantric path. See [[14-Indian-Tantric-Chinese/03-Tantra-Left-Hand-Right-Hand]]
- **Vayu** — the wind god, the breath of the Hindu tradition
- **Veda** — the Hindu foundational knowledge; the four Vedas
- **Vishuddha** — the 5th chakra, the throat. See [[14-Indian-Tantric-Chinese/01-Seven-Chakras]]
- **Viveka** — the discriminating wisdom, the discernment

## W

- **Wafq** — the Islamic magic square. See [[02-Hurufiyya-Lettrism/04-Wafq-Magic-Squares]]
- **Waidan** — the outer alchemy of the Chinese Taoist tradition. See [[14-Indian-Tantric-Chinese/02-Chinese-Alchemy-Neidan-Waidan]]
- **Wu Wei** — the Taoist practice of "non-action" or "effortless action". See [[17-Philosophy-Cosmology/03-Tao-Te-Ching-Laozi]]

## X

- **Xenomancy** — divination by strangers, by foreigners, by their speech or appearance

## Y

- **Yantra** — the geometric diagram of the deity. See [[14-Indian-Tantric-Chinese/03-Tantra-Left-Hand-Right-Hand]]
- **YHWH** (Yahweh) — the Tetragrammaton, the 4-letter name of God. See [[08-Kabbalah-Numerology/02-Gematria-Mapping]]
- **Yin-Yang** — the Chinese complementary opposites. See [[17-Philosophy-Cosmology/03-Tao-Te-Ching-Laozi]]
- **Yoga** — the Hindu practice of union, the discipline of the body-mind
- **Yogi** — the practitioner of yoga

## Z

- **Zazen** — the Japanese Zen practice of sitting meditation
- **Zhenren** — the "true person", the Taoist immortal. See [[14-Indian-Tantric-Chinese/02-Chinese-Alchemy-Neidan-Waidan]]
- **Zodiac** — the 12 signs of the astrological tradition
- **Zohar** — the "Book of Splendor", the central text of the Kabbalah
- **Zuhd** — the Sufi practice of voluntary simplicity
