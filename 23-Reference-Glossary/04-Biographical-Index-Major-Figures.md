# Biographical Index — Major Figures of the Esoteric Tradition

## The Index

The esoteric tradition is carried by **people** in **lines of transmission**. This index lists the major figures, their dates, their tradition, and their single most important contribution. It is the **who's who** of the library — pair it with the concordance ([[02-Cross-Tradition-Concordance-Table]]) and the reading order ([[03-Reading-Order-Index-By-Tradition]]).

The index is **structured by era**, not by tradition, to show the **overlap and transmission** across cultures.

## The Cross-Tradition Note

The index shows the **transmission chains**: Alexandria → Plotinus → Proclus → Pseudo-Dionysius → the Renaissance; and Mecca/Baghdad → Al-Buni → Ibn Arabi → the Hurufi current ([[02-Hurufiyya-Lettrism]]). The same **perennial structure** (see [[01-Origins-Traditions/05-Perennial-Philosophy]]) recurs under different names — exactly the claim the concordance table documents.
