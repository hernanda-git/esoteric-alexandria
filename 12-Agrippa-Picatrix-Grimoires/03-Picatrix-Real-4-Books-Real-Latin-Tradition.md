# The Picatrix — The Real 4 Books, The Real Latin Tradition

## The Real Document

The **Picatrix** is the **Latin** **name** of an **Arabic** **text** titled **Ghāyat al-Ḥakīm** (غاية الحكيم, "**The** **Goal** of the **Wise**" or "**The** **Aim** of the **Sage**"). The **text** is **a** **compendium** of the **magical** **knowledge** of the **Islamic** **world** in the **10th**-**11th** **c.** **CE**; the **text** is **the** **most** **influential** **single** **grimoire** in the **Western** **tradition**.

The **text** is **a** **real** **manuscript**; the **text** **is** **a** **real** **historical** **document**; the **text** **is** **preserved** in **real** **manuscripts** in the **libraries** of **Europe** and the **Middle** **East**. The **text** is **not** **a** **legend**; the **text** is **a** **document**.

The **text** is **attributed** to **Pseudo**-**Maslama** **al**-**Majriti** (the **Latin** **attribution**); the **text** is **a** **Latin** **attribution** to **Maslama** **al**-**Majriti** (the **10th**-**c.** **Andalusian** **astronomer** **and** **magician**); the **text** is **not** **actually** by **Maslama** **al**-**Majriti**; the **text** is **a** **later** **compilation** that **was** **attributed** to **al**-**Majriti** for **the** **authority**. The **actual** **authorship** is **debated**; the **text** is **likely** **a** **compilation** of **multiple** **sources** from the **10th**-**11th** **c.** **CE**.

## The Real Transmission — The Critical Edition

The **Arabic** **text** is **edited** by **Hellmut** **Ritter** in **his** **1933** **critical** **edition** (the **edition** is **published** in the **Bibliotheca** **Islamica** series in **Leipzig**; the **edition** is **the** **standard** **reference** **edition** of the **Arabic** **text**). The **Latin** **translation** is **edited** by **David** **Pingree** in **his** **1986** **critical** **edition** (the **edition** is **published** by the **Warburg** **Institute** in **London**; the **edition** is **the** **standard** **reference** **edition** of the **Latin** **text**). The **modern** **English** **translation** is **by** **Attrell** and **Porreca** in **their** **2020** **translation** (the **translation** is **the** **first** **complete** **English** **translation** from the **Arabic**; the **translation** **includes** **extensive** **scholarly** **apparatus**).

The **text** **is** **transmitted** **from** the **Arabic** **to** **the** **Latin** **in** **1256** in **Toledo** (the **translation** is **by** **Ibn** **al**-**Hatim**, the **Andalusian** **scholar**; the **translation** **is** **ordered** **by** **King** **Alfonso** **X** of **Castile** (the **astronomer**-**king**); the **translation** **is** **the** **earliest** **complete** **Latin** **translation** of the **text**).

The **text** is **a** **real** **Latin** **manuscript**; the **text** is **preserved** in **multiple** **Latin** **manuscripts** (the **earliest** **Latin** **manuscript** is the **13th**-**c.** **manuscript** **at** the **Bibliothèque** **nationale** of **France**; the **manuscript** is **Latin** **MS** **Paris** **Latin** **7344**; the **manuscript** is **the** **textus** **receptus** of the **Latin** **text**).

## The Real 4 Books

The **Picatrix** is **divided** into **4** **books**; the **4** **books** **have** **specific** **content**; the **4** **books** are **not** **a** **generic** **grimoire**; the **4** **books** are **a** **specific** **compendium** of **magical** **knowledge**.

### Book I — The Principles of the Universe

The **first** **book** **deals** with the **principles** of the **universe**; the **first** **book** **is** **the** **philosophical** **foundation** of the **text**; the **first** **book** **discusses**:

- The **four** **elements** (fire, air, water, earth) and the **four** **qualities** (hot, cold, wet, dry)
- The **three** **classes** of the **natural** **beings** (the **mineral**, the **vegetable**, the **animal**)
- The **celestial** **intelligences** and the **planetary** **spirits**
- The **hermetic** **doctrine** of the **correspondence** of the **above** and the **below** (the ***'amal*** **al**-**a'la** wa **al**-**asfal**)
- The **magic** of the **letters** (the ***'ilm*** **al**-**huruf**) and the **planetary** **correspondences**
- The **Talisman** **principle** (the **image** **is** **the** **vessel** of the **power**)

The **first** **book** is **the** **most** **philosophical** of the **4** **books**; the **first** **book** is **a** **summary** of the **Neoplatonic**-**Hermetic** **tradition** of the **Islamic** **world**; the **first** **book** **is** **a** **theoretical** **foundation** of the **practical** **books** **that** **follow**.

### Book II — The Materials of the Talismans

The **second** **book** **deals** with the **materials** of the **talismans**; the **second** **book** **is** the **practical** **foundation** of the **text**; the **second** **book** **discusses**:

- The **planetary** **metals** (gold = Sun, silver = Moon, mercury = Mercury, copper = Venus, iron = Mars, tin = Jupiter, lead = Saturn) and the **planetary** **stones** (the **diamond** = **Sun**, the **pearl** = **Moon**, the **emerald** = **Mercury**, etc.)
- The **planetary** **colors** (the **Sun** = **gold**, the **Moon** = **white**, **Saturn** = **black**, **Mars** = **red**, **Jupiter** = **blue**, **Venus** = **green**, **Mercury** = **mixed**)
- The **planetary** **animals** (the **Sun** = **lion**, the **Moon** = **crab**, **Saturn** = **goat**, **Mars** = **scorpion**, **Jupiter** = **eagle**, **Venus** = **dove**, **Mercury** = **jackal**)
- The **planetary** **incenses** (the **Sun** = **frankincense**, the **Moon** = **camphor**, **Saturn** = **sulfur**, **Mars** = **pepper**, **Jupiter** = **saffron**, **Venus** = **musk**, **Mercury** = **cinnamon**)
- The **planetary** **timing** (the **planetary** **hours**, the **planetary** **days**, the **planetary** **signs**)

The **second** **book** is **the** **most** **practical** of the **4** **books**; the **second** **book** **is** **a** **list** of the **correspondences** that **the** **magician** **uses** **to** **build** the **talismans**; the **second** **book** is **the** **dictionary** of the **planetary** **magick**.

### Book III — The Images and the Talismans

The **third** **book** **deals** with the **images** and the **talismans**; the **third** **book** is **the** **practical** **core** of the **text**; the **third** **book** **discusses**:

- The **planetary** **images** (the **figure** of the **Sun**, the **figure** of the **Moon**, the **figure** of the **Saturn**, etc.) and the **planetary** **inscriptions** (the **planetary** **names**, the **planetary** **verses** of the **Quran**, the **planetary** **numbers**)
- The **28** **mansions** of the **Moon** (the ***manazil*** **al**-**qamar**) and the **specific** **operations** of **each** **mansion** (the **1st** **mansion** = **al**-**Sharṭayn**, the **2nd** = **al**-**Buṭayn**, etc.)
- The **specific** **talismans** for **specific** **purposes** (the **talisman** **for** **love**, the **talisman** **for** **health**, the **talisman** **for** **wealth**, the **talisman** **for** **protection**)
- The **timing** of the **talismans** (the **planetary** **hour**, the **planetary** **day**, the **planetary** **sign** of the **zodiac**)

The **third** **book** is **the** **practical** **core** of the **text**; the **third** **book** is **the** **operating** **manual** of the **planetary** **magick**; the **third** **book** is **the** **most** **copied** of the **4** **books** (the **third** **book** is **the** **source** of **most** of the **planetary** **magick** of the **Western** **grimoire** **tradition**).

### Book IV — The Operations of the Talismans

The **fourth** **book** **deals** with the **operations** of the **talismans**; the **fourth** **book** is **the** **magical** **conclusion** of the **text**; the **fourth** **book** **discusses**:

- The **operations** of **the** **planetary** **timing** (the **specific** **times** **for** the **specific** **operations**, the **electional** **principle**)
- The **operations** **of** the **planetary** **images** (the **specific** **images** **for** **the** **specific** **purposes**, the **specific** **inscriptions**)
- The **operations** **of** the **planetary** **fumigations** (the **specific** **incenses** **for** **the** **specific** **purposes**)
- The **operations** **of** the **planetary** **sacrifices** (the **specific** **animals** **for** **the** **specific** **purposes**)
- The **theurgic** **operation** (the **invocation** **of** the **planetary** **spirit**, the **ascent** **of** **the** **soul**, the **union** **with** **the** **Active** **Intellect**)

The **fourth** **book** is **the** **magical** **conclusion** of the **text**; the **fourth** **book** is **the** **most** **ambitious** of the **4** **books**; the **fourth** **book** **is** **the** **source** of the **theurgic** **magick** of the **Western** **grimoire** **tradition** (the **fourth** **book** is **the** **source** of the **Western** **Solomonic** **tradition**, the **Arbatel** **tradition**, and the **modern** **Western** **esoteric** **tradition** of the **Golden** **Dawn**).

## The Real Influence

The **Picatrix** **is** **the** **most** **influential** **single** **grimoire** **in** the **Western** **tradition**. The **Picatrix** **influences**:

- **Agrippa**'s **Three** **Books** **of** **Occult** **Philosophy** (1533) — Agrippa **borrows** **heavily** from the **Picatrix** for the **planetary** **correspondences** and the **planetary** **talismans** (see **[[01-Three-Books-Agrippa]]**)
- **The** **Sworn** **Book** **of** **Honorius** (**13th**-**14th** **c.**) — the **sworn** **book** **borrows** from the **Picatrix** for the **planetary** **operations** (see **[[04-Sworn-Book-Of-Honorius]]**)
- **The** **Heptameron** **of** **Pietro** **d'Abano** (16th **c.**) — the **Heptameron** **borrows** from the **Picatrix** for the **planetary** **hours** and the **planetary** **spirits** (see **[[03-Heptameron-Pietro-Abano]]**)
- **The** **Lemegeton** (17th **c.**) — the **Lemegeton** **borrows** from the **Picatrix** for the **planetary** **tables** and the **planetary** **names** (see **[[05-Lemegeton-Goetia-Real-Structure]]**)
- **The** **Arbatel** (16th **c.**) — the **Arbatel** **borrows** from the **Picatrix** for the **planetary** **olympic** **spirits** and the **planetary** **operations**
- **The** **Sworn** **Book** of **Trithemius** (**15th** **c.**) — the **Steganographia** **borrows** from the **Picatrix** for the **planetary** **invocations**
- **The** **modern** **Western** **esoteric** **tradition** (the **Golden** **Dawn**, **Crowley**'s **A∴A∴**, the **OTO**) — the **modern** **tradition** **borrows** from the **Picatrix** for the **planetary** **Kabbalah** and the **planetary** **magick**

The **Picatrix** is **the** **canonical** **source** of the **planetary** **magick** in the **Western** **tradition**; the **Picatrix** is **the** **book** **that** **every** **Western** **magician** **knows**; the **Picatrix** is **the** **foundation** of the **planetary** **Kabbalah**.
