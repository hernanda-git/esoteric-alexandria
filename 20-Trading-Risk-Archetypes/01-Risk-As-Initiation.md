# Trading and the Asketikos — Discipline as Initiation

## The Honesty Note

This is **not** a foundational claim. This is a **careful comparison** between the **practical** **disciplines** of the trading profession and the **practical** **disciplines** of the **asketikos** (the Christian/Stoic/Buddhist/Sufi ascetical tradition). The comparison is **legitimate** in some **places**; the comparison is **strained** in **others**. The **previous** **draft** of this **document** **overclaimed** in several places. The **new** **draft** **holds** the **comparison** **more** **carefully**.

The **comparison** **that** **works**: the **trader** who **survives** a **drawdown** has **learned** **something** that the **trader** who **has** **not** **survived** a **drawdown** **has** **not** **learned**. The **learning** is **practical**, **embodied**, **not** **intellectual**. The **learning** is **the** **kind** of **learning** that **the** **asketikos** **tradition** **calls** *ascesis* (the **embodied** **practice** of **discipline**). The **two** **are** **structurally** **similar**; the **two** **are** **not** **identical**.

The **comparison** that **does** **not** **work**: the **trader** is **not** a **Sufi** **practitioner**, the **drawdown** is **not** a **spiritual** **ordeal**, the **position** **size** is **not** a **talisman**. The **trader** is an **engineer**; the **drawdown** is a **financial** **event**; the **position** **size** is a **risk** **parameter**. The **two** **vocabularies** **are** **not** **the** **same** **vocabulary**.

## What the Comparison Does Capture

Three points of **legitimate** structural comparison:

1. **The necessity of a discipline that has a cost.** The ascetical tradition in **all** its major forms (Christian, Stoic, Buddhist, Sufi, Hindu) **prescribes** practices that **cost** the practitioner something — fasting, vigils, solitude, silence, voluntary poverty, bodily discomfort. The cost is **not** **sadism**; the cost is **the price of changing the default**. A trader who has not experienced a **cost** (a drawdown, a position held against pain, a market that moved while the trader waited) has not had the **default** **changed**. The **embodied** **memory** of the cost is what makes the discipline **stick**. The Stoic and Buddhist traditions call this **pathos** (the emotional default) being **transformed** by **askesis** (the embodied discipline). The **trading** tradition calls this "**the trade that taught you**" — a drawdown, a winning trade held through pain, a position closed too early. The two are **structurally** **similar**.

2. **The relationship to the body.** The ascetical tradition **does not** **abstract** the practitioner from the body; the ascetical tradition **uses** the body. The fasting, the vigil, the silence, the breath, the posture are **bodily** practices. The trading tradition is **also** a **bodily** practice — the trader learns to **read** the body (the chest tightness at the start of a losing streak, the breath quickening at the close of a winning trade, the loss of appetite after a bad day). The trader who **does not** read the body is the trader who **does not** have the embodied practice. The **body-as-feedback** is a legitimate parallel.

3. **The willingness to be wrong.** The ascetical tradition **prescribes** the practice of being wrong without retreating — the *confessio* in the Christian tradition, the *tawba* in the Sufi tradition, the *upāya* in the Buddhist tradition. The practice is **not** the same as the **modern** "growth mindset" (the modern "growth mindset" is a **secularized** **version** of the ascetical practice, the modern "growth mindset" is **not** the **same** as the ascetical practice). The trader who can be wrong **publicly**, in a **logged** way, in a **replicable** way, has the **embodied** practice of being wrong. The trader who **cannot** be wrong has not the **practice**. The willingness-to-be-wrong is a legitimate parallel.

## What the Comparison Does Not Capture

Four points where the comparison **breaks**:

1. **The cosmic frame.** The ascetical tradition is **grounded** in a **cosmology** — the Christian ascetic is preparing for the **Second Coming**, the Sufi ascetic is preparing for the **vision** of **God**, the Buddhist ascetic is preparing for the **cessation** of **suffering**, the Stoic ascetic is preparing for the **apatheia** of the **sage**. The **trader** is **not** preparing for a **cosmic** **event**; the trader is preparing for the **next** **trade**. The **cosmology** is **what** **gives** the **askesis** its **gravitas**; the **trader** is **without** the **gravitas** (the **gravitas** is **replaced** by the **P&L**). The **cosmology** **cannot** **be** **transferred** from the **ascetical** **tradition** to the **trading** **tradition**; the **transfer** would be **dishonest**.

2. **The community of witnesses.** The ascetical tradition is **transmitted** in a **community** — the cenobitic tradition of the Christian **monasticism** (the **communal** **life** of the **monastery**), the **silsila** of the **Sufi** **tradition** (the **chain** of the **shaykh** to the **disciple**), the **sangha** of the **Buddhist** **tradition** (the **community** of the **monks**). The **trader** is **largely** **alone** — the **trader** **does** **not** **have** the **community** of **witnesses** that the **ascetical** **tradition** **has**. The **community** **is** **what** **prevents** the **askesis** **from** **becoming** **mere** **ego**; the **trader** is **at** **risk** of the **ego** **becoming** the **whole** **practice** (the **ego** **is** **fed** by the **P&L**). The **community** **cannot** **be** **replaced** by the **trading** **community** (the **trading** **community** is **not** **a** **witness** **community**; the **trading** **community** is **a** **competitive** **community**).

3. **The telos.** The ascetical tradition has a **telos** (an **end**, a **goal**) — the **union** **with** **God**, the **realization** of the **Self**, the **cessation** of **suffering**, the **apatheia** of the **sage**. The **telos** is **what** **gives** the **askesis** its **direction**; the **telos** **is** **what** **makes** the **askesis** **more** **than** **mere** **suffering**. The **trader** is **at** **risk** of **having** **no** **telos** (the **telos** **is** **the** **P&L**, the **telos** is **the** **next** **trade**, the **telos** is **the** **next** **year**). The **trader** **without** **telos** is the **trader** **who** **burns** **out**; the **ascetic** **without** **telos** is the **ascetic** **who** **becomes** **cynical**. The **telos** **cannot** **be** **transferred** from the **ascetical** **tradition**; the **telos** **must** **be** **the** **trader's** **own**.

4. **The authority of the teaching.** The ascetical tradition **transmits** the **practice** in a **chain** of **authority** (the **silsila**, the **abbatial** **line**, the **patriarchal** **line**). The **practitioner** **receives** the **practice** from a **master**; the **master** **is** **responsible** for the **transmission**. The **trader** **receives** the **practice** from **books**, **videos**, **courses**, **mentors**; the **trader** **is** **responsible** for the **transmission** to **himself**. The **authority** of the **transmission** is **different**; the **transmission** is **less** **authoritative** in the **trading** **tradition**; the **transmission** is **more** **authoritative** in the **ascetical** **tradition**.

## The Practical Disciplines — The Real List

A modern trader who **wants** the **discipline** of the **asketikos** can **adopt** the **following** **practices** (the **practices** are **the** **actual** **practices** of the **ascetical** **tradition**, **adapted** for the **trading** **context**):

1. **The log** (the **examen**) — the **daily** **review** of the **trades** (the **trades** **taken**, the **trades** **missed**, the **trades** **avoided**, the **reason** for **each**). The **log** is the **mirror** of the **practice**. The **log** is **written** in the **first** **person** ("I took the trade because I thought..."). The **log** is **read** at the **end** of the **week**, the **month**, the **year**. The **log** is the **embodied** **record**. (The **Jesuit** **tradition** calls the **log** the *examen*; the **Sufi** **tradition** calls the **log** the *muhasaba*; the **Buddhist** **tradition** calls the **log** the *saṃyojana*.)

2. **The fast** (the *asayama*) — the **periodic** **abstention** from **trading** (one **day** a **week**, one **week** a **month**, one **month** a **quarter**). The **fast** **is** **not** the **punishment**; the **fast** is the **reset**. The **fast** is the **practice** of **abstaining** from the **default** **of** the **trading** **action**; the **fast** is the **practice** of **remembering** that the **trading** is **not** the **life**; the **life** is **the** **life**. (The **Christian** **tradition** calls the **fast** the *nesteia*; the **Sufi** **tradition** calls the **fast** the *sawm*; the **Buddhist** **tradition** calls the **fast** the *upavāsa*.)

3. **The vigil** (the *pannychis*) — the **periodic** **watching** at the **end** of the **day** (the **review** of the **day's** **trades** at the **end** of the **day**, the **preparation** of the **next** **day's** **trades** at the **end** of the **day**). The **vigil** is the **practice** of **being** **present** at the **end** of the **day**; the **vigil** is the **practice** of **not** **sleeping** **before** the **review**. The **vigil** is the **practice** of **ending** the **day** in the **right** **state**. (The **Christian** **tradition** calls the **vigil** the *pannychis*; the **Sufi** **tradition** calls the **vigil** the *tahajjud*; the **Jewish** **tradition** calls the **vigil** the *tiqqun ḥatsot*.)

4. **The silence** (the *hesychia*) — the **periodic** **abstention** from **the** **noise** (the **abstention** from the **news**, from the **Twitter**, from the **Discord**, from the **chat**). The **silence** is the **practice** of **listening** to the **interior**. The **silence** is the **practice** of **not** **reacting** to the **noise**. The **silence** is the **practice** of **returning** to the **default** of the **interior**. (The **Christian** **tradition** calls the **silence** the *hesychia*; the **Sufi** **tradition** calls the **silence** the *samt*; the **Buddhist** **tradition** calls the **silence** the *mauna*.)

5. **The community** (the *sangha*) — the **periodic** **meeting** with the **other** **traders** (the **mastermind**, the **peer** **group**, the **accountability** **partner**). The **community** is the **practice** of **not** **trading** **alone**. The **community** is the **practice** of **witnessing** the **other** **trader**'s **practice**. The **community** is the **practice** of **being** **accountable** to the **other** **trader**. (The **Christian** **tradition** calls the **community** the *koinonia*; the **Sufi** **tradition** calls the **community** the *suhba*; the **Buddhist** **tradition** calls the **community** the *sangha*.)

## The Practice

A modern trader who **wants** the **discipline** of the **asketikos** can **adopt** the **following** **practice**:

1. **Choose** **a** **tradition** (the **Christian** **ascetical** **tradition**, the **Sufi** **tradition**, the **Buddhist** **tradition**, the **Stoic** **tradition**, the **Hindu** **tradition**)
2. **Read** the **foundational** **texts** of the **tradition** (the *Philokalia* for the **Christian** **tradition**, the *Qushayri* for the **Sufi** **tradition**, the *Visuddhimagga* for the **Buddhist** **tradition**, the *Enchiridion* for the **Stoic** **tradition**, the *Yoga Sutras* for the **Hindu** **tradition**)
3. **Adopt** the **practices** of the **tradition** (the **log**, the **fast**, the **vigil**, the **silence**, the **community**)
4. **Find** a **teacher** of the **tradition** (the **tradition** is **transmitted** in a **chain**; the **chain** **is** **the** **transmission**; the **transmission** is **the** **practice**)
5. **Maintain** the **practice** **for** **years** (the **practice** is **lifelong**; the **practice** is **not** a **quick** **fix**)

The **practice** is **not** a **trading** **technique**. The **practice** is **a** **life** **practice** that **informs** the **trading** **practice**. The **practice** is **the** **foundation** of the **trading** **practice**; the **practice** is **not** the **trading** **practice** itself.

## The Critical Note

The **trader** who **adopts** the **asketikos** is **not** a **trader** **who** **has** **been** **saved** by the **asketikos**; the **trader** is a **trader** **who** **has** **found** **a** **practice** that **informs** the **trading**. The **asketikos** is **not** a **trading** **technique**; the **asketikos** is **a** **life** **practice**. The **trader** is **not** a **monk**; the **trader** is a **trader** **who** **has** **adopted** the **monastic** **practice** to **inform** the **trading**.

The **comparison** is **useful**; the **comparison** is **not** **a** **proof**; the **comparison** is **a** **lens**. The **trader** **uses** the **lens** to **clarify** the **practice**; the **trader** **does** **not** **use** the **lens** to **prove** the **practice**.
