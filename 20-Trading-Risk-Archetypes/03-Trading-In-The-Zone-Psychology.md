# Trading in the Zone — The Psychology of the Market

## The Claim

The **market** is a **random** **walk** in the **short** **term** and a **trending** **process** in the **long** **term**. The **trader** is **a** **probabilistic** **actor** in the **random** **short** **term**; the **trader** is **a** **position** **allocator** in the **trending** **long** **term**. The **trader** who **understands** the **market** **as** a **random** **walk** is the **trader** who **trades** **at** **size**; the **trader** who **misunderstands** the **market** as a **deterministic** **predictor** is the **trader** who **blows** **up**.

This is **not** an esoteric claim. This is the **mathematical** **consensus** of the modern financial economics (Fama, Malkiel, Shiller — the **Nobel** **laureates** of the **modern** **financial** **economics**). The **market** is **a** **random** **walk** in the **short** **term**; the **market** is **a** **trending** **process** in the **long** **term**; the **trader** is **a** **probabilistic** **actor** in **both** **timeframes**.

## The Market — The Real Ontology

### The Efficient Market Hypothesis

The **efficient** **market** **hypothesis** (**EMH**) is the **modern** **academic** **consensus** on the **market**. The **EMH** is **attributed** to **Eugene Fama** (the **Nobel** **laureate** **in** **2013**). The **EMH** **states** that the **market** **prices** **reflect** **all** the **available** **information**; the **market** is **informationally** **efficient**; the **trader** **cannot** **consistently** **outperform** the **market** **without** **taking** **on** **additional** **risk**.

The **EMH** is **in** **three** **forms**:

1. **Weak EMH** — the **market** **prices** **reflect** **all** the **information** in the **historical** **prices** (the **technical** **analysis** **cannot** **consistently** **outperform** the **market**)
2. **Semi-strong EMH** — the **market** **prices** **reflect** **all** the **public** **information** (the **fundamental** **analysis** **cannot** **consistently** **outperform** the **market**)
3. **Strong EMH** — the **market** **prices** **reflect** **all** the **public** and the **private** **information** (the **insider** **information** **cannot** **consistently** **outperform** the **market**)

The **EMH** is **the** **consensus** of the **modern** **academic** **finance**. The **EMH** is **the** **basis** of the **modern** **index** **funds** (the **index** **funds** **are** **the** **EMH** **in** **practice**: the **index** **funds** **buy** the **market** **portfolio**; the **index** **funds** **accept** the **EMH** and **give** **up** **the** **attempt** to **outperform**).

### The Random Walk Hypothesis

The **random** **walk** **hypothesis** is the **mathematical** **statement** of the **EMH** **for** **the** **short** **term**. The **random** **walk** **hypothesis** **states** that the **market** **prices** **move** **randomly**; the **future** **price** **movement** **is** **independent** of the **past** **price** **movement**; the **future** **price** **movement** **is** **not** **predictable** from the **past** **price** **movement**.

The **random** **walk** **hypothesis** is **attributed** to **Maurice Kendall** (the **statistician**) in **1953** and is **formalized** by **Burton Malkiel** in **A Random Walk Down Wall Street** (1973, 11th ed. 2019). The **random** **walk** **hypothesis** is **the** **mathematical** **basis** of the **EMH**; the **random** **walk** **hypothesis** is **the** **EMH** **applied** **to** **the** **short** **term**.

The **random** **walk** **hypothesis** is **not** **the** **claim** that the **market** is **unpredictable** in **all** **timeframes**. The **random** **walk** **hypothesis** is **the** **claim** that the **market** is **unpredictable** in the **short** **term** (the **short** **term** is **defined** as **the** **timeframe** **where** the **fundamental** **analysis** **does** **not** **have** **a** **signal**). The **long** **term** is **different**: the **long** **term** is **the** **timeframe** **where** the **fundamental** **analysis** **does** **have** a **signal** (the **long** **term** is **trending**, not **random**).

### The Behavioral Finance Critique

The **behavioral** **finance** **critique** of the **EMH** is **the** **modern** **academic** **counter** to the **EMH**. The **behavioral** **finance** **is** **attributed** to **Richard Thaler** and **Robert Shiller** (the **Nobel** **laureates** in **2017** and **2013** respectively). The **behavioral** **finance** **argues** that the **market** **prices** are **not** **always** **efficient**; the **market** **prices** are **influenced** by **the** **cognitive** **biases** of the **traders**; the **market** **prices** are **subject** to **the** **irrational** **exuberance** **and** the **irrational** **despair**.

The **behavioral** **finance** **is** **a** **critique**, **not** **a** **dismissal** of the **EMH**. The **behavioral** **finance** **argues** that the **market** **is** **usually** **efficient**; the **market** **is** **sometimes** **inefficient**; the **inefficiency** is **the** **opportunity** for the **trader** who **understands** the **cognitive** **biases**.

The **behavioral** **finance** **is** **the** **basis** of the **modern** **trading** **psychology**. The **trader** who **understands** the **cognitive** **biases** of the **market** is the **trader** who **trades** **at** **the** **inefficiency**; the **trader** who **misunderstands** the **cognitive** **biases** of the **market** is the **trader** who **is** **captured** **by** the **biases**.

## The Trader's Mind — The Real Psychology

### The Five Beliefs of the Trader (Mark Douglas)

Mark Douglas identified **five** **beliefs** of the **trader** **that** **must** **be** **present** for the **trader** to **trade** **at** **size**. The **five** **beliefs** are:

1. **Anything can happen** — the **market** **is** a **random** **walk**; **anything** **can** **happen** **at** **any** **time**; the **trader** **does** **not** **know** **what** **will** **happen** **next**; the **trader** **accepts** the **uncertainty**.
2. **You don't need to know what is going to happen next to make money** — the **trader** **has** **a** **edge**; the **edge** **does** **not** **require** the **trader** to **know** **the** **future**; the **edge** **is** **the** **statistical** **bias** **of** **the** **system**.
3. **There is a random distribution between wins and losses for any given set of variables that define an edge** — the **streak** **is** **random**; the **streak** **is** **not** **a** **signal**; the **trader** **accepts** the **streak** **as** a **normal** **part** of the **trading**.
4. **Every moment in the market is unique** — the **market** **does** **not** **repeat**; the **trader** **does** **not** **trade** **the** **same** **trade** **twice**; the **trader** **accepts** the **uniqueness** of the **moment**.
5. **The way to make money is to have a statistical edge and to manage risk** — the **trader** **has** **a** **statistical** **edge**; the **trader** **manages** the **risk**; the **trader** **does** **not** **try** to **predict** the **future**.

The **five** **beliefs** are **the** **psychological** **basis** of the **trading**. The **trader** **without** the **five** **beliefs** **is** **the** **trader** **who** **is** **captured** by the **cognitive** **biases**; the **trader** **with** the **five** **beliefs** **is** the **trader** **who** **trades** **at** **size**.

### The Three Cognitive Biases of the Trader (Kahneman, Tversky)

The **three** **cognitive** **biases** that **most** **affect** the **trader** are:

1. **Loss aversion** (Kahneman, Tversky, 1979) — the **tendency** **to** **prefer** **avoiding** **losses** **over** **acquiring** **gains**; the **tendency** to **cut** **wins** **short** and **let** **losses** **run**; the **tendency** **to** **refuse** **to** **take** **a** **risk** **for** **a** **gain**; the **tendency** **to** **take** **a** **risk** **to** **avoid** **a** **loss**.
2. **Recency bias** — the **tendency** **to** **weight** **the** **recent** **events** **more** **heavily** **than** **the** **distant** **events**; the **tendency** **to** **extrapolate** **the** **recent** **trend** **to** **the** **future**; the **tendency** **to** **overweight** **the** **recent** **wins** **or** **losses**.
3. **Confirmation bias** — the **tendency** **to** **seek** **the** **information** **that** **confirms** **the** **existing** **belief**; the **tendency** **to** **ignore** **the** **information** **that** **contradicts** **the** **existing** **belief**; the **tendency** **to** **interpret** **the** **ambiguous** **information** **as** **a** **confirmation** **of** **the** **existing** **belief**.

The **three** **cognitive** **biases** **are** **the** **cause** **of** **the** **most** **trading** **mistakes**. The **trader** **who** **understands** **the** **three** **cognitive** **biases** **is** **the** **trader** **who** **trades** **at** **size**; the **trader** **who** **misunderstands** **the** **three** **cognitive** **biases** **is** **the** **trader** **who** **blows** **up**.

### The Five Stages of the Trader (Steenbarger)

Brett Steenbarger, the **modern** **academic** **trading** **psychologist**, **identified** **five** **stages** of the **trader** (the **stages** **are** **based** on the **psychotherapy** **literature** on the **stages** of **change**):

1. **The novice** — the **trader** **is** **unconscious** **of** **the** **incompetence**; the **trader** **is** **convinced** **that** the **market** **is** **predictable**; the **trader** **blows** **up** in the **first** **year**.
2. **The advanced beginner** — the **trader** **is** **conscious** of **the** **incompetence**; the **trader** **is** **looking** **for** **the** **magic** **indicator**; the **trader** **switches** **between** **systems**; the **trader** **blows** **up** **again**.
3. **The competent** — the **trader** **is** **conscious** of **the** **competence**; the **trader** **has** **a** **system**; the **trader** **has** **a** **track** **record**; the **trader** **still** **struggles** **with** **the** **cognitive** **biases**.
4. **The proficient** — the **trader** **is** **unconscious** of **the** **competence**; the **trader** **has** **internalized** the **system**; the **trader** **trades** **at** **size**; the **trader** **is** **rare**.
5. **The expert** — the **trader** **is** **beyond** the **system**; the **trader** **adapts** **to** **the** **market**; the **trader** **is** **very** **rare**.

The **five** **stages** **are** **the** **modern** **academic** **basis** of the **trading** **psychology**. The **trader** **at** **the** **expert** **stage** **is** **the** **trader** **who** **has** **internalized** the **system**; the **trader** **at** **the** **expert** **stage** **is** **the** **trader** **who** **trades** **at** **size**.

## The Practice

A modern trader who **wants** the **psychological** **discipline** of the **trading** **can**:

1. **Read** the **Douglas** (*Trading in the Zone*) — the **foundational** text on **the** **trading** **psychology**
2. **Read** the **Douglas** (*The Disciplined Trader*) — the **earlier** text on **the** **trading** **psychology**
3. **Read** the **Kahneman** (*Thinking, Fast and Slow*) — the **modern** **cognitive** **bias** text
4. **Read** the **Steenbarger** (*The Psychology of Trading*) — the **modern** **academic** text on **the** **trading** **psychology**
5. **Adopt** the **five** **beliefs** of the **trader** — the **trader** **works** **on** the **five** **beliefs** as **a** **meditation** **practice** (the **trader** **reviews** **the** **five** **beliefs** **at** the **start** of **the** **trading** **day**)
6. **Adopt** the **three** **cognitive** **biases** **as** the **vigilance** — the **trader** **watches** for **the** **three** **cognitive** **biases** **during** **the** **trading** **day**; the **trader** **corrects** the **bias** **when** **the** **trader** **sees** **the** **bias**
7. **Track** the **stages** of the **trader** — the **trader** **identifies** the **stage** **of** **the** **trader**; the **trader** **works** **on** **the** **specific** **challenge** of **the** **specific** **stage**
8. **Maintain** the **practice** **for** **years** — the **trading** **psychology** **practice** is **lifelong**; the **practice** is **not** **a** **quick** **fix**

The **trading** **psychology** **practice** is **not** **a** **mystic** **tradition**; the **trading** **psychology** **practice** is **a** **specific** **modern** **psychological** **discipline** **with** **specific** **methodology**, **specific** **measurement**, **specific** **outcomes**.

## The Note

The **trader** **is** **not** **a** **mystic**; the **trader** is **a** **probabilistic** **actor** in **a** **random** **walk**. The **trader** **who** **understands** the **market** as **a** **random** **walk** is the **trader** who **trades** **at** **size**; the **trader** **who** **misunderstands** the **market** as **a** **predictor** is the **trader** **who** **blows** **up**.

The **trading** **psychology** is **not** **a** **mystic** **discipline**; the **trading** **psychology** is **a** **specific** **modern** **psychological** **discipline**. The **esoteric** **vocabulary** **is** **not** **needed** for the **trading** **psychology**; the **modern** **psychological** **vocabulary** is **adequate**.
