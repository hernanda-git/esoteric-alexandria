# The Seven Chakras — Mapping the Subtle Body

## The Subtle Body

The **Tantric tradition** of Hinduism (and the parallel **Tantric Buddhism**) developed a sophisticated model of the **subtle body** (Sanskrit *sukshma sharira*). The subtle body is the **intermediate** between the gross physical body and the **causal body** (the deepest layer, the soul).

The subtle body has **three main components**:

1. **Nadis** (नाडी) — the **72,000 channels** through which *prana* (life-force) flows. The three principal nadis are **Ida** (the left, lunar, cooling), **Pingala** (the right, solar, heating), and **Sushumna** (the central, neutral).

2. **Prana** (प्राण) — the **life-force** itself, the vital breath, the moving energy. Prana is divided into 5 sub-pranas: *prana* (the breath, the heart), *apana* (the downward breath, the elimination), *samana* (the equalizing breath, the digestion), *udana* (the upward breath, the speech), and *vyana* (the diffusing breath, the circulation).

3. **Chakras** (चक्र) — the **7 (or more) energy centers** where the nadis converge and the prana is concentrated. The chakras are **vortices** or **wheels** of energy.

The 7-chakra system is the most common in the modern West. Other systems (the **21** in some Tantric systems, the **9** in the Vajrayana system, the **12** in some Shaiva systems) are also present in the tradition.

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Muladhara** | Base of spine | Red | Earth | **Lam** (लं) | Survival, grounding, foundation | 4 | Adrenal cortex |
| 2 | **Svadhisthana** | Lower abdomen | Orange | Water | **Vam** (वं) | Sexuality, pleasure, creativity | 6 | Gonads / ovaries |
| 3 | **Manipura** | Solar plexus | Yellow | Fire | **Ram** (रं) | Will, power, digestion | 10 | Adrenal medulla / pancreas |
| 4 | **Anahata** | Heart | Green | Air | **Yam** (यं) | Love, compassion, connection | 12 | Thymus |
| 5 | **Vishuddha** | Throat | Blue | Ether / Sound | **Ham** (हं) | Speech, truth, expression | 16 | Thyroid / parathyroid |
| 6 | **Ajna** | Between the eyebrows | Indigo | Light (the 5th element, the "no-element") | **Om** (ॐ) | Intuition, insight, will | 2 | Pituitary |

The 7 chakras correspond to the **7 layers** of the subtle body, the **7 stages** of the kundalini's ascent, the **7 colors** of the rainbow (in the modern Western mapping), the **7 notes** of the musical scale, the **7 planets** (in the Western alchemical reformulation).

## The Kundalini

The **Kundalini** (कुण्डलिनी) is the **dormant power** at the base of the spine, coiled 3.5 times around the **Sushumna** nadi at the level of **Muladhara** (the base chakra). The Kundalini is the **shakti** (the feminine power) of the body.

In the **ascent**, the practitioner awakens the Kundalini through specific practices (yoga, mantra, breath, visualization) and **raises** it through the 7 chakras. The ascent transforms the practitioner:

- Muladhara → survival consciousness → **grounded**
- Svadhisthana → emotional/sexual consciousness → **pleasured**
- Manipura → will/ego consciousness → **powerful**
- Anahata → love consciousness → **loving**
- Vishuddha → expression consciousness → **artistic**
- Ajna → intuitive consciousness → **wise**
- Sahasrara → unity consciousness → **unbound**

The ascent is the **classic Tantric path**. The mature path is the **integration** of all 7 centers, not the abandonment of the lower for the higher.

## The Modern Note

The chakra system is the **most influential** of the Tantric teachings in the modern West. The 7-chakra system has been adopted by:

- **The modern yoga community** — almost every yoga school in the West teaches the 7-chakra system
- **The New Age movement** — the chakra system is a staple of New Age teaching
- **The Western occult tradition** — the Golden Dawn mapped the 7 chakras to the 7 sephirot of the lower Tree of Life (Muladhara = Malkuth, ..., Sahasrara = Kether)
- **The modern psychology community** — the chakra system has been used by the **somatic psychology** tradition (Peter Levine, Bessel van der Kolk) and the **transpersonal psychology** tradition (Stanislav Grof, Ken Wilber)
- **The modern medical community** — the **Ayurvedic** and **Yogic** understanding of the subtle body has been used in integrative medicine

The library's category 14 treats the chakra system as the **central document** of the Indian esoteric tradition. The system is **alive**, **practiced**, and **integrated** into the modern global spirituality.

## The Practice

A modern practice of the chakra system requires:

1. **Learn the 7 chakras** — names, locations, colors, elements, bija mantras
2. **Learn the meditation** — sit with each chakra, activate its bija
3. **Learn the asana** — the yoga postures for each chakra
4. **Learn the prana** — the breath exercises for each chakra
5. **Find a teacher** — the operative kundalini practice is **dangerous** without a teacher; the **kundalini syndrome** is a real medical condition

The library's category 14 is the **textual foundation**. The practice requires a teacher and a tradition.
