# Hatha Yoga Pradipika — The Real Source of Hatha Yoga

## The Real Document

The **Hatha** **Yoga** **Pradipika** (**"**the** **Light** **on** **Hatha** **Yoga**"**, **HYP**) **is** **the** **most** **important** **single** **text** of **the** **Hatha** **Yoga** **tradition**; the **HYP** **is** **a** **specific** **Hindu** **text**; the **HYP** **is** **composed** **c.** **1450** **CE** **by** **Yogi** **Swatmarama**; the **HYP** **is** **the** **basis** **of** **the** **modern** **Hatha** **Yoga** **tradition**.

The **HYP** **is** **the** **most** **widely** **studied** **Hindu** **text** on **Hatha** **Yoga**; the **HYP** **is** **the** **text** that **codified** **the** **4** **limbs** of **the** **Hatha** **Yoga** **practice**; the **HYP** **is** **the** **text** that **preserved** **the** **asanas**, **pranayamas**, **mudras**, **bandhas**, and **samadhi** of **the** **Hatha** **Yoga** **tradition**; the **HYP** **is** **the** **text** that **is** **the** **basis** of **the** **modern** **Hatha** **Yoga** **tradition** **in** **the** **West**.

The **HYP** **is** **a** **real** **Hindu** **text**; the **HYP** **is** **preserved** in **real** **Sanskrit** **manuscripts**; the **HYP** **is** **a** **real** **historical** **text**.

## The Real 4 Chapters

The **HYP** **is** **divided** **into** **4** **chapters**; the **4** **chapters** **are** **the** **specific** **chapters** of **the** **HYP**; the **4** **chapters** **are** **the** **basis** of **the** **Hatha** **Yoga** **tradition**.

### Chapter I — The Asanas (Verses 1-39)

**Chapter** **I** **presents** **the** **asanas** (**physical** **postures**; the **asanas** **are** **the** **specific** **forms** of **the** **Hatha** **Yoga** **practice**); the **asanas** **in** **the** **HYP** **are**:

- **Svastikasana** (the **auspicious** **pose**)
- **Gomukhasana** (the **cow**-**face** **pose**)
- **Padmasana** (the **lotus** **pose**; the **lotus** **pose** **is** **the** **most** **important** **asana** in **the** **Hatha** **Yoga** **tradition**)
- **Virasana** (the **hero**'s **pose**)
- **Simhasana** (the **lion**'s **pose**)
- **Bhadrasana** (the **gentle** **pose**)
- **Muktasana** (the **liberated** **pose**)
- **Mayurasana** (the **peacock** **pose**)
- **Paschimottanasana** (the **back**-**stretching** **pose**)
- **Mayurasana** (the **peacock** **pose**)
- **Shavasana** (the **corpse** **pose**; the **corpse** **pose** **is** **the** **most** **important** **asana** for **the** **modern** **yoga** **tradition**)
- **Siddhasana** (the **adept**'s **pose**; the **adept**'s **pose** **is** **the** **most** **important** **asana** for **the** **traditional** **Hatha** **Yoga** **tradition**; the **adept**'s **pose** **is** **one** **of** **the** **two** **main** **meditation** **asanas** **with** **Padmasana**)

The **HYP** **chapter** **I** **describes** **each** **asana** **with** **specific** **instructions**; the **HYP** **chapter** **I** **is** **the** **basis** of **the** **modern** **asana** **tradition**.

### Chapter II — The Pranayama (Verses 1-78)

**Chapter** **II** **presents** **the** **pranayama** (**breath** **control**; the **pranayama** **is** **the** **specific** **form** of **the** **breath** **control** **practice**); the **pranayamas** **in** **the** **HYP** **are**:

- **Nadi** **Shodhana** (the **channel** **purification**; the **channel** **purification** **is** **the** **most** **important** **pranayama** in **the** **Hatha** **Yoga** **tradition**; the **channel** **purification** **purifies** **the** **3** **main** **channels** **of** **the** **body** (**Ida**, **Pingala**, **Sushumna**))
- **Kapalabhati** (the **skull**-**shining** **breath**; the **skull**-**shining** **breath** **is** **a** **specific** **purification** **pranayama**)
- **Bhastrika** (the **bellows** **breath**; the **bellows** **breath** **is** **a** **specific** **heating** **pranayama**)
- **Ujjayi** (the **victorious** **breath**; the **victorious** **breath** **is** **a** **specific** **breath** **that** **is** **performed** **with** **a** **slight** **constriction** of **the** **glottis**)
- **Kumbhaka** (the **breath** **retention**; the **breath** **retention** **is** **the** **most** **advanced** **pranayama**; the **breath** **retention** **is** **performed** **after** **the** **inhalation** (**antara** **kumbhaka**) **or** **after** **the** **exhalation** (**bahya** **kumbhaka**))

The **HYP** **chapter** **II** **describes** **each** **pranayama** **with** **specific** **instructions**; the **HYP** **chapter** **II** **is** **the** **basis** of **the** **modern** **pranayama** **tradition**.

### Chapter III — The Mudras and Bandhas (Verses 1-130)

**Chapter** **III** **presents** **the** **mudras** (**gestures**; the **mudras** **are** **specific** **gestures** **of** **the** **body** **that** **direct** **the** **prana**) **and** **the** **bandhas** (**locks**; the **bandhas** **are** **specific** **muscular** **contractions**); the **mudras** **and** **bandhas** **in** **the** **HYP** **are**:

- **Mahamudra** (the **great** **gesture**; the **great** **gesture** **is** **the** **most** **important** **mudra** in **the** **Hatha** **Yoga** **tradition**)
- **Mahabandha** (the **great** **lock**; the **great** **lock** **is** **the** **most** **important** **bandha** in **the** **Hatha** **Yoga** **tradition**)
- **Mula** **Bandha** (the **root** **lock**; the **root** **lock** **is** **the** **contraction** of **the** **perineum**)
- **Uddiyana** **Bandha** (the** **abdominal** **lock**; the **abdominal** **lock** **is** **the** **contraction** of **the** **abdomen**)
- **Jalandhara** **Bandha** (the** **throat** **lock**; the **throat** **lock** **is** **the** **contraction** of **the** **throat**)
- **Khechari** **Mudra** (the **tongue** **gesture**; the **tongue** **gesture** **is** **the** **folding** of **the** **tongue** **back** **into** **the** **nasopharynx**)
- **Ashwini** **Mudra** (the** **horse** **gesture**; the **horse** **gesture** **is** **the** **contraction** of **the** **anal** **sphincter**)
- **Viparita** **Karani** (the** **inverted** **practice**; the **inverted** **practice** **is** **the** **shoulder** **stand** **and** **similar** **inversions**)

The **HYP** **chapter** **III** **describes** **each** **mudra** **and** **bandha** **with** **specific** **instructions**; the **HYP** **chapter** **III** **is** **the** **basis** of **the** **modern** **mudra** **and** **bandha** **tradition**.

### Chapter IV — The Samadhi (Verses 1-114)

**Chapter** **IV** **presents** **the** **samadhi** (**absorption**; the **samadhi** **is** **the** **specific** **form** of **the** **meditation** **practice**); the **samadhi** **in** **the** **HYP** **is**:

- **Nirvikalpa** **Samadhi** (the **formless** **absorption**; the **formless** **absorption** **is** **the** **most** **advanced** **form** of **meditation**; the **formless** **absorption** **is** **the** **goal** of **the** **Hatha** **Yoga** **tradition**; the **formless** **absorption** **is** **the** **highest** **state** of **consciousness** in **the** **Hatha** **Yoga** **tradition**)
- **Savikalpa** **Samadhi** (the **formful** **absorption**; the **formful** **absorption** **is** **an** **intermediate** **form** of **meditation**; the **formful** **absorption** **is** **a** **specific** **form** of **meditation** **with** **a** **content**)

The **HYP** **chapter** **IV** **describes** **the** **samadhi** **with** **specific** **instructions**; the **HYP** **chapter** **IV** **is** **the** **basis** of **the** **modern** **samadhi** **tradition**.

## The Real Note

The **HYP** **is** **a** **real** **Hindu** **text**; the **HYP** **is** **preserved** in **real** **Sanskrit** **manuscripts**; the **HYP** **is** **a** **real** **historical** **text**. The **HYP** **is** **not** **a** **legend**; the **HYP** **is** **a** **document**; the **HYP** **is** **a** **real** **Hindu** **text**.

The **HYP** **is** **a** **useful** **resource** **for** **the** **student** of **the** **Hatha** **Yoga** **tradition**; the **HYP** **is** **a** **useful** **resource** **for** **the** **student** of **the** **history** of **yoga**; the **HYP** **is** **a** **useful** **resource** **for** **the** **student** of **the** **Western** **esoteric** **tradition**.
