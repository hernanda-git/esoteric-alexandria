# Chinese Alchemy — Neidan and Waidan

## The Two Branches

The Chinese alchemical tradition has **two branches** that developed in parallel:

1. **Waidan** (外丹, "outer alchemy") — the **outer chemical work**, the laboratory alchemy of elixirs, cinnabar (mercury sulfide), realgar, gold, and jade. The goal is to make the **external elixir of immortality** (the *wai dan*, the "outer cinnabar") that confers physical immortality.

2. **Neidan** (内丹, "inner alchemy") — the **inner psychosomatic work**, the meditation-based practice of refining the practitioner's *jing* (精, essence), *qi* (氣, breath/energy), and *shen* (神, spirit) to produce the **inner elixir of immortality** (the *nei dan*, the "inner cinnabar"). The goal is **transformative enlightenment** within the body.

The two branches are **not exclusive**. Most Taoist masters have practiced both, and the boundary is permeable. The modern popular tradition has focused on **Neidan** (the safer and more philosophical), but **Waidan** is the historically older and the **source of the symbolic vocabulary** of Neidan.

## The Three Treasures (San Bao)

The central Neidan teaching is the **Three Treasures** (三寶, *san bao*):

1. **Jing** (精) — **Essence**. The dense, material, reproductive energy. Stored in the **kidneys** (in the Chinese medical view). The substrate of the body.
2. **Qi** (氣) — **Breath / Energy**. The moving, vital, life-force. Generated by the **lungs** and circulated by the **spleen**. The animating energy of the body.
3. **Shen** (神) — **Spirit**. The light, subtle, conscious energy. Resides in the **heart**. The consciousness of the body.

The Three Treasures are **interconvertible** in the Neidan practice. **Jing** is refined into **Qi**; **Qi** is refined into **Shen**; **Shen** is refined into **Dao** (the way, the absolute). The process is the **Great Work** of Neidan.

## The Two Cauldrons and the Five Phases

The Neidan practice uses the **two cauldrons** (the lower and the upper) and the **five phases** (五行, *wu xing* — wood, fire, earth, metal, water):

- **Lower Cauldron** — the **abdomen**, the *dantian* (the "cinnabar field"). The base of the practice.
- **Middle Cauldron** — the **heart**, the *zhong dan tian* (the middle cinnabar field). The transformation.
- **Upper Cauldron** — the **forehead** (the "Ming Men" or third eye), the *shang dan tian* (the upper cinnabar field). The release.

The five phases (wood, fire, earth, metal, water) are the **five energies** that cycle through the body. The Neidan practice **inverts** the normal cycle to **transform** the practitioner.

## The Microcosmic Orbit (Xiao Zhoutian)

The central Neidan practice is the **Microcosmic Orbit** (小周天, *xiao zhoutian*). The practice:

1. **Sit in meditation**, spine straight, eyes closed
2. **Gather the breath** at the lower dantian
3. **Inhale**, drawing the Qi up the **back** of the body along the **Du Mai** (the "Governing Vessel", the posterior channel)
4. **Hold the breath** at the crown of the head
5. **Exhale**, drawing the Qi down the **front** of the body along the **Ren Mai** (the "Conception Vessel", the anterior channel)
6. **Return** to the lower dantian
7. **Repeat** 36 times, then reverse for 36 more

The orbit is the **first practice** of the Neidan system. After the orbit is established, the practitioner can move on to the **Macrocosmic Orbit** (the full-body circulation) and the **Inner Elixir** practice.

## The Inner Elixir (Nei Dan)

The **Inner Elixir** is the **concentrated product** of the Three Treasures' refinement. The Neidan tradition has **three elixirs** corresponding to the three stages:

| Stage | Elixir | Production | Result |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. | **Jing elixir** (精丹) | Jing refined to Qi | The body is filled with energy; the practitioner can "die without illness" |
| 2. | **Qi elixir** (氣丹) | Qi refined to Shen | The mind is filled with spirit; the practitioner can "leave the body at will" |
| 3. | **Shen elixir** (神丹) | Shen refined to Dao | The spirit returns to the absolute; the practitioner becomes a **zhenren** (真人, "true person", a Taoist immortal) |

The elixirs are **not substances**; they are **stages of consciousness**. The "refining" is the **gradual transformation** of the practitioner's ordinary consciousness into the **sublime consciousness** of the Taoist sage.

## The Waidan Practice

The **Waidan** practice is the **outer laboratory alchemy**. The classical Waidan elixir is the **cinnabar pill** (the "red pill"), made by **reduction** of cinnabar (mercury sulfide) with lead, producing elemental mercury and lead sulfide. The pill was supposed to confer **immortality**.

The classical Waidan history:

- **Wei Boyang** (2nd c. CE) — the founder, the *Cantong Qi* (The Seal of the Triplex Unity) is the foundational text
- **Sun Simiao** (7th c. CE) — the great Tang physician, made the Waidan pill famous
- **The Tang emperors** — many of the Tang emperors died from cinnabar poisoning (the pill is **toxic** — mercury is a poison). This led to the decline of Waidan and the rise of Neidan
- **The Song dynasty** (10th-13th c. CE) — the **Neidan** reformulation, led by **Zhang Boduan** (the *Wuzhen Pian*, "Awakening to Reality")

The Waidan practice is **historically important** but **practically dangerous**. Modern practitioners do not make the cinnabar pill; they study the Waidan texts as the **symbolic vocabulary** of the Neidan practice.

## The Modern Note

The Neidan practice is **alive** in modern China and the West:

1. **The Chinese Taoist community** — the **Complete Perfection** (Quanzhen, 全真) school, the **Dragon Gate** (Longmen, 龍門) lineage, the **Taoist Association of China** all maintain the Neidan practice
2. **The Western Qigong community** — the modern qigong (氣功) practice is a **medical derivative** of Neidan
3. **The Western Neidan community** — the **Mantak Chia** tradition, the **Damo Mitchell** tradition, the **Taoist Studies Institute** all teach Neidan to Western students
4. **The Western yoga community** — the modern yoga tradition has incorporated Neidan concepts (the dantian, the three treasures) into the practice

The library's category 14 treats the Chinese alchemical tradition as the **parallel tradition** to the Western alchemical tradition (see [[04-Alchemy-Transmutation/01-Alchemy-Overview]]). The two traditions developed the **same structure** in isolation (or with minimal influence) and converged on a **remarkably similar** system of stages, symbols, and goals.

## The Bridge to the Western Tradition

The Chinese and Western alchemical traditions converge in **many points**:

| Chinese | Western | Convergence |
|---|---|---|
| **Jing** (essence) | **Salt** (the body, the fixed) | Both: the substrate of the work |
| **Qi** (breath/energy) | **Mercury** (the volatile, the spirit) | Both: the moving agent |
| **Shen** (spirit) | **Sulfur** (the soul, the combustive) | Both: the directing force |
| **Dao** (the way) | **The Stone** (the philosopher's stone) | Both: the goal |
| **Cinnabar (Dan)** | **The Red King/Queen** (the rubedo) | Both: the final product |
| **The Microcosmic Orbit** | **The Circulation of the Stone** (the alchemical circulation) | Both: the central practice |
| **The Zhenren** (True Person) | **The Adept / The Magister** | Both: the master |

The convergence is **real**. The two traditions developed in isolation (or with limited contact through the Silk Road) and arrived at a **remarkably similar** system. The library treats the convergence as a case of **independent cross-tradition concordance**.

## The Practice

A modern practice of the Chinese alchemical tradition requires:

1. **The foundation** — basic meditation, basic breath, basic Qigong
2. **The texts** — read the *Cantong Qi*, the *Wuzhen Pian*, the modern translations (Pregadio, Kohn, Wang Mu)
3. **The teacher** — find a teacher in the **Dragon Gate** lineage, the **Mantak Chia** tradition, the **Damo Mitchell** tradition
4. **The discipline** — daily practice, the **standing meditation** (zhan zhuang), the **sitting meditation** (jing zuo)
5. **The community** — the practice is best learned in a **community** of practitioners

The library's category 14 is the **textual foundation**. The practice requires a teacher and a tradition.
